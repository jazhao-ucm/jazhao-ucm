## Key Concepts

### 1. Value Creation vs. Value Capture

**Value Creation:**
- Total amount of resources
- Total amount of value for users or customers
- Creating something that provides value

**Value Capture:**
- How much value you actually capture/make money from
- The difference between what you create and what you capture

**Key Insight:** Creating value doesn't mean you capture it. You need both value creation AND value capture.

---

### 2. Michael Porter - Value Chain

**Concept:** Value chain framework for understanding how firms create value

**Components:**
- **Primary Activities:** Directly involved in production and delivery
- **Support Activities:** Enable primary activities

**Key Point:** Value is created when resources and support activities combine to deliver value to users.

**Example:** 
- "We are here to give it to our present users to be useful, and useful."
- Value creation requires combining different activities

---

### 3. David Teece - Value Capture & Market Access

**Key Concept:** Market access goes to value capture

**David Teece's Framework:**
- Focus on how to capture value from innovation
- Importance of market access
- Need for complementary assets

**Key Insight:** Having a great product isn't enough - you need market access to capture value.

---

### 4. Case Study: Spanish Research Institution

**The Story:**
- Research project at Spanish institution (San Francisco area)
- Researchers developed a technology/product
- Initially valued at €15,000
- Got investment and conducted testing (rats/research)
- Secured investment from major Japanese foundation/company (Takeda)
- Technology had potential market value

**The Numbers:**
- **Initial valuation:** €15,000
- **Actual market valuation:** €250 million/year
- **Market size:** 150,000 people
- Shows the difference between intrinsic value and market valuation

**Key Lesson:** The difference between what you create (€15,000) and what the market values it at (€250M/year) is huge.

### 6. Why Spanish/European Companies Struggle

**The Problem:**
- Spanish companies often struggle with scale
- Limited market access compared to global players
- "Why, the Vegas? Why to Just think about the size of the market."
- "How is making my Spanish, a few girls in Europe, that that's it. Operation."

### 7. Value Capture Mechanisms

**Three Ways to Capture Value:**

1. **Patents**
   - "If I didn't know anything and if they could work the first thing I could do is they could patent"
   - Protect your innovation
   - Even if you sell it, patents help you capture value
   - "So that even if I sold it they had to to make more money I need a patent"

2. **Branding**
   - "The brand is very important"
   - Strong brand = stronger value capture
   - "The brand is very important especially for a company name Takeda"
   - More consumers trust established brands

3. **Market Access**
   - Distribution channels
   - Customer relationships
   - Market presence

**Key Insight:** "This is what we called cost specialized assets and market access."

### 9. Patents & Intellectual Property

**Key Concepts:**
- Patents protect your innovation
- Help capture value even after selling
- Important for tech companies
- "If I didn't know anything and if they could work the first thing I could do is they could patent"

**Strategy:**
- Patent your ideas
- "So that even if I sold it they had to to make more money I need a patent"
- But don't over-patent
- "Avoid patenting or they over patent"

**Key Insight:** Patents are one way to capture value, but not the only way.

**Asset-Based Approaches:**
- Based on assets you have
