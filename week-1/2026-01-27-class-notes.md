### 16. Value Creation & Capture Review

**Key Concepts:**
- **Value Creation** = Total amount of resources and value for users
- **Value Capture** = How much value you actually make money from
- **Inputs < Utility** - You create more value than inputs
- **Co-specialized assets** - Assets specialized to your business
- **Specific assets** - Unique assets for your business

**Value Capture Mechanisms:**
- **Brand** - Brand reputation
- **Market access** - Distribution and customer access
- **IP or secrecy** - Patents or keeping things secret

## Key Takeaways

1. **Decisions combine rationality AND emotion** - Not purely rational
2. **Less information** → More emotional decisions
3. **More information** → More rational decisions
4. **Strong brands** rely on emotion, not detailed information
5. **Weak brands** must provide information themselves
6. **Patents serve two purposes** - Protect your tech AND prevent competitors from blocking you
7. **Patent portfolios** are strategic tools for negotiation
8. **Open innovation** can create industry standards and generate demand
9. **IBM example** - Gave away software, made money on hardware
10. **Xerox PARC** - Great at innovation, terrible at commercialization
11. **Samsung** - 40% revenue from licensing, 60% from products
12. **Information asymmetry** affects decision making
13. **Market signaling** helps when buyers have limited information
14. **Brand strategy** depends on brand strength
