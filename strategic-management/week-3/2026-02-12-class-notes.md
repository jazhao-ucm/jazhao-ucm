I. Cost of Equity - Review
   A. Formula
      1. Cost of Equity = Rf + β × (Market Return − Rf)
   B. Components
      1. Rf = risk-free rate (govt bonds)
      2. β = company-specific risk vs. market
      3. Market Return = avg return (e.g., IBEX 35)
   C. Satisfaction
      1. Shareholders satisfied when return > cost of equity
      2. Or when better than industry avg

II. Illustration Assignments
   A. Three Options
      1. Art of War (Unit 1)
      2. Climate Change (Unit 2)
      3. Five Forces Model (Unit 2)
   B. Format
      1. Voluntary basis
      2. 10-15 min presentation
      3. Read and interpret

III. Art of War Illustration
   A. Challenge
      1. Apply four elements of strategy
      2. Find key quotations
   B. Four Elements
      1. Clear and consistent goal
      2. Know yourself
      3. Know environment
      4. Effective implementation

IV. Climate Change Illustration
   A. Task
      1. Explain as economic phenomenon
      2. Effects on economic activity
   B. Structure
      1. Part 1: Explain using PESTEL
      2. Part 2: Four industries
         a. Two w/ positive effects (opportunities)
         b. Two w/ negative effects (threats)
      3. Part 3: Company/country examples
   C. Tourism Example
      1. Better weather → good for some
      2. Too hot (Caribbean) → negative

V. Five Forces - Substitutive Products
   A. Definition
      1. Different products, different technologies
      2. In customer eyes: meet same needs
   B. Coca-Cola Example
      1. Sugar, energy, caffeine
      2. Substitutes: coffee, tea, energy drinks
   C. Cars Example
      1. Two different cars ≠ substitutive (same tech)
      2. Car vs. train, plane, bike = substitutive
         a. Different technologies
         b. Same need: transportation

VI. Why Identifying Rivals Is Difficult
   A. Challenge
      1. Similar tech = easy to identify
      2. But many competitors ≠ rivals
   B. Car Industry Example
      1. Not all car producers are rivals
      2. Customers consider status, speed, etc.
   C. Luxury Market Example
      1. Ferrari vs. Lamborghini vs. expensive travel
      2. In customer mind = substitutive

VII. Barriers to Entry
   A. Concept
      1. Don't want new competitors
      2. New entrants = dangerous
   B. Two Types
      1. Natural/economic barriers
      2. Artificial barriers

VIII. Natural Barriers - Economies of Scale
   A. Car Industry Example
      1. Need ~500,000 cars/year to be profitable
      2. Must reach scale in 4-5 years
   B. This Is Economies of Scale
      1. Large companies subject to high scale
      2. All actors must be very large
   C. Why Few Competitors
      1. Economic nature of industry
      2. Need large size for profitability
   D. Aircraft Industry
      1. Only ~4 manufacturers globally
      2. Boeing, Airbus + China, India
      3. Enormous scale required

IX. What Are Economies of Scale?
   A. Definition
      1. Large company → produce more
      2. Cost per unit decreases
   B. Sources
      1. Learning curve
         a. Repetition → fewer mistakes
         b. Time reduced, efficiency increased
      2. Purchasing power
         a. Buy 10,000M bottles → lower prices
      3. Advertising
         a. Divide expense among more products
      4. R&D
         a. Spread investment over more products
      5. Capacity utilization
         a. Use 24/7 → reduce idle capacity

X. Services vs. Manufacturing
   A. Manufacturing
      1. Learning curve very high
      2. Must be very large to compete
   B. Services
      1. Depends on type
      2. Many services can be small and profitable
      3. Harder to achieve scale

XI. Business Growth and Economies of Scale
   A. Main Reason for Growth
      1. Achievement of economies of scale
      2. Internal, M&A, or alliances

XII. Artificial Barriers
   A. Definition
      1. Imposed by govt or institutions
      2. Barriers to international competition
   B. Banking in Spain Example
      1. Must comply w/ Spanish regulations
      2. Closes door to foreign banks

XIII. Clusters - Geographic Agglomeration
   A. Definition
      1. Geography matters
      2. Companies concentrated in few sq km
   B. Silicon Valley Example
      1. Most famous cluster
      2. West Coast, northern California
   C. Advantage
      1. Companies in cluster have extra opportunities
      2. More profitable than similar companies outside

XIV. Silicon Valley - Key Institutions
   A. Question
      1. What non-company institutions dynamize?
   B. Answer
      1. Stanford University
      2. UC Berkeley
   C. Why They Matter
      1. Produce best workers
      2. Attract talent
      3. Research w/ private companies
      4. Knowledge spillovers
      5. Easy to move between companies

XV. Route 128 (Boston) Cluster
   A. Example
      1. Second largest tech cluster in US
   B. Key Institutions

XVI. What Makes a Cluster?
   A. Important Distinction

XVII. Four Advantages of Clusters
   A. To Be Explored in Unit 3

XVIII. Strategic Analysis - "Where I Am?"
   A. Three Core Questions
      1. What I want? = mission, vision, objectives
      2. Where I am? = external analysis
      3. Who I am? = internal analysis
   B. Focus Today
      1. "Where I am?" = second pillar

XIX. External Environment
   A. Definition
      1. Everything outside company
      2. Variables affecting business
   B. Main Task
      1. Discover positive externals = opportunities
      2. Discover negative externals = threats
      3. Take advantage or protect against

XX. Two Levels of External Environment
   A. Macro Environment
      1. Economic factors at national/international level
      2. Social, cultural, demographic, geopolitical
      3. Technological, legal, regulatory
   B. Industry Environment
      1. Customers, suppliers, competitors
      2. More immediate and specific

XXI. PESTEL Analysis
   A. Framework
      1. Political
      2. Economic
      3. Social
      4. Technological
      5. Environmental
      6. Legal
   B. Purpose
      1. Distinguish opportunities from threats
      2. Analyze macro environment

XXII. Five Forces Model
   A. Purpose
      1. Discover who are rivals
      2. Analyze industry environment
   B. Creator
      1. Michael Porter (Harvard)
   C. Why Important
      1. Simple and powerful
      2. Most well-known model
      3. Explains competitive dynamics

XXIII. Five Forces - Elements
   A. Five Forces
1. Rivalry among existing competitors
      2. Threat of new entrants
3. Bargaining power of suppliers
4. Bargaining power of buyers
5. Threat of substitutive products
   B. Important Note
      1. Analyze from inside industry
      2. As current competitor

XXIV. PESTEL Analysis - Two Steps
   A. Step 1: Identify
      1. According to six dimensions
      2. e.g., GDP growth = economic
   B. Step 2: Assess Impact
      1. Threat or opportunity?
      2. Depends on your business

XXV. Negative GDP as Opportunity Example
   A. Question
      1. When is negative GDP an opportunity?
   B. Answer - Fast Food
      1. Regular restaurant = threat
      2. Fast food = opportunity
      3. People need low-cost food
      4. 2008 crisis → fast food grew in Spain

## Key Takeaways

1. Cost of equity = Rf + β × (Market − Rf); satisfied when return exceeds this
2. Three illustrations: Art of War, Climate Change, Five Forces
3. Strategic analysis: What I want? Where I am? Who I am?
4. External analysis = opportunities and threats
5. Two levels: macro (PESTEL) and industry (Five Forces)
6. Substitutive products = different tech, same customer needs (hardest to identify)
7. Barriers: natural (economies of scale) and artificial (regulations)
8. Economies of scale: cost ↓ as production ↑
9. Services vs. manufacturing: scale stronger in manufacturing
10. Clusters: geographic agglomeration w/ advantages (Silicon Valley = Stanford + Berkeley)
11. Five Forces: simple, powerful framework (Porter)
12. Same factor can be threat for some, opportunity for others

X. 30-Second Cold Call Version.
A. 3-Line Speaking Frame.
   1. TOPIC: STATE THE CORE QUESTION IN ONE LINE.
   2. LOGIC: GIVE 2 DRIVERS/FACTORS THAT EXPLAIN THE ANSWER.
   3. ACTION: END WITH ONE DECISION OR IMPLICATION.
B. Delivery Rule.
   1. 25-30 SECONDS MAX.
   2. NO BACKGROUND STORY; GO STRAIGHT TO CLAIM + EVIDENCE + CLOSE.

DI. Oral Answer Template (60-90 Seconds, Class Use).
A. Opening.
   1. STATE THE CORE QUESTION OR TOPIC IN ONE LINE.
   2. STATE YOUR MAIN CLAIM/THESIS IN ONE LINE.
B. Logic.
   1. GIVE 2-3 KEY FACTORS OR DRIVERS.
   2. LINK EACH FACTOR TO THE CLAIM (CAUSE -> EFFECT).
C. Action Or Implication.
   1. END WITH ONE DECISION, RECOMMENDATION, OR CONSEQUENCE.
   2. CLOSE CLEARLY WITHOUT ADDING NEW IDEAS.
D. Delivery Rule.
   1. 60-90 SECONDS.
   2. CLAIM -> EVIDENCE -> CLOSE.
