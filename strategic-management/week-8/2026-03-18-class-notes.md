I. Exam Prep (Unit 1) — Strategy Definition, Origins, Structure
   A. Origins And Early Thinkers
      1. Military origins: “strategos” = army/general; “strategy” linked to leadership
      2. Business context: first “strategy” in business administration
      3. Von Neumann and Morgenstern (1944): applied military themes
      4. Boston Consulting Group
         a. Porter (1980) and Porter (1985)
   B. What Strategy Deals With
      1. Solve problems in uncertain future; manage “unknown”
      2. Complex decisions: causal effects, inter-relationships, lack of info
      3. Mistakes expensive (even “company’s survival”)
   C. Firm–Environment Link (Grant, 2015)
      1. Firm: goals & values; resources & capabilities; structure; people
      2. Environment: competitors; customers; suppliers; national & international context
      3. Strategy = link between firm and environment
   D. Components Of Successful Strategy
      1. Effective implementation
      2. Strongly consistent long-term goals
      3. Understanding competitive environment
      4. Appraisal of internal resources
   E. Three Levels Of Strategy
      1. Corporate strategy
         a. Set of activities/businesses to compete (company scope)
         b. Maintain/diversify; planning mergers
         c. Growth: internal growth; entering foreign markets; performance
      2. Competitive strategy
         a. How to compete successfully in a certain business
         b. Best business-unit strategy (BSU)
         c. Low-cost leadership, product diff; build/exploit strengths
      3. Functional strategy
         a. Resource utilization by functional area
         b. Contribution to competitive & corporate strategies
         c. Contexts: operations mgmt; human resources mgmt; financial mgmt; R&D mgmt; etc.

II. Exam Prep (Unit 2) — Mission, Vision, Objectives; Governance; CSR
   A. Mission, Vision, Strategic Objectives
      1. Mission statement: what business are we in; what we want to achieve; firm scope

      3. Corporate vision: where we want to go; expected future; reflection about “once”

   B. Value Creation And Stakeholders
      1. Firm performance: guide strategic decisions; areas of importance; measure process

      3. Stakeholders: any person/group affected by firm’s actions
   C. Corporate Governance
      1. Mechanisms: shareholders (owners) and managers
      2. Board of directors (governance; oversee management)
      3. Corporate values: principles, beliefs; “social” behavior toward society
   D. CSR And Sustainability (Themes)
      1. CSR: environmental policies; relationship with stakeholders; ethical considerations
      2. Business ethics: moral foundations guiding strategic decision + action; “values”
      3. Example referenced: Danone (aligning purpose with business strategy)

III. Exam Prep — Strategy Processes And Roles
   A. Deliberate (Rational) Process
      1. Strategic management often follows a fixed, rational sequence
      2. Steps (noted as sequence)
         a. Mission (vision), objectives
         b. Strategic analysis
            i. External analysis
            ii. Internal analysis
         c. Strategy formulation (corporate; competitive; functional)
         d. Strategy implementation (organizational change)
         e. Strategy control + evaluation (performance; feedback; acceptability)
   B. Advantages Of Deliberate Process
      1. Introduces rational process; quantifiable evaluation
      2. Helps coordinate actions
   C. Limitations Of Strategic Decision-Making
      1. Limited rationality: too many options; hard to find “optimal”
      2. Many constraints; imperfect information
      3. Characteristic of strategic decisions: irreversible; long-term; involve uncertainty
      4. New challenges: changes in competitive environment; organizational changes
   D. Emergent Strategy
      1. Strategy not only planned; can emerge over time
      2. Learning, adaptation; “emerges over time”
   E. Strategy View (Noted Perspective)
      1. Strategic mgmt influenced by personal preferences; shaped by environmental dynamics
      2. Many roles mentioned: CEO/president; “middle managers”; board of directors; top management team (TMT)

IV. Unit 3 — External Analysis (Environment Analysis)
   A. Purpose And Output
      1. Understand external factors affecting firm strategy
      2. Identify opportunities and threats (O/T)
      3. Determine industry attractiveness
      4. Assess impact on profitability and competitiveness
   B. External Environment Levels
      1. Macro environment: broad external area affecting all firms
      2. Industry environment: forces affecting competition within a specific industry
   C. PEST / PESTEL (External Variables Affecting Country/Firm)
      1. Political
      2. Economic: inflation, interest rates, GDP growth
      3. Social: demography, lifestyle, culture
      4. Technological: innovation, automation, AI
      5. Environmental: climate change, sustainability
      6. Legal: labor laws, competitive law
      7. Why Important
         a. Identify opportunities (e.g., new tech)
         b. Identify threats (e.g., regulations)
         c. Anticipate change
   D. Porter’s Five Forces (Goal: Measure Industry Attractiveness / Profit Potential)
      1. Rivalry among existing competitors
         a. “Industry competition”: number of competitors; product differentiation; “cost” issues; growth; “ease of entry”
      2. Threat of new entrants
         a. New entrants impact sales & profits
         b. Barriers to entry
            i. Natural: economies of scale; capital requirements
            ii. Formal requirements; product differentiation
            iii. Access to distribution
      3. Threat of substitutes
         a. Example note: “standing vs cinema”; electronic/online substitution
      4. Bargaining power of buyers
         a. “High power” = lower prices, lower profit
         b. Drivers: large volume buyers; high information
      5. Bargaining power of suppliers
         a. “High power” = input costs, lower profit
         b. Drivers: few suppliers; unique inputs; high switching costs
   E. Complementors / Sixth Force (Noted)
      1. Mentioned: complementors / government / “strategic group” factor
      2. Industry vs. firm effect
         a. Probability depends on industry structure + firm-specific capabilities
         b. Research note: firm effect stronger than industry effect

V. Unit 4 — Internal Analysis (Exam Prep)
   A. Purpose
      1. Goal: identify strengths (S) and weaknesses (W)
      2. Link to SWOT: strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, threats
   B. Internal Diagnostic Tools
      1. Firm identity
         a. Mission; goals; core values; purpose; ethics
      2. Strategic profile
         a. Functional areas: operations, finance, marketing, HR, etc.
   C. Value Chain (Porter, 1985)
      1. Primary activities: add value directly (together as “sub value chain”)
         a. Operations; marketing; distribution; service
      2. Support activities
         a. Infrastructure; technology development; HR management; procurement
      3. Horizontal and vertical links
         a. Horizontal: coordination within firm
         b. Vertical: coordination with suppliers and customers
      4. Why important
         a. Identify cost advantages
         b. Identify differentiation advantages
         c. Find sources of competitive advantage
   D. Resource-Based View (RBV)
      1. “Major paradigm” in strategy
      2. Key premise: firms are heterogeneous
      3. Resources and capabilities = base of firm’s actions and controls
      4. Types of resources
         a. Tangible: financial; physical
         b. Intangible: brand; patents; technological; “know-how”
         c. Human: skills; motivation; know-how
      5. Capabilities
         a. Ability to coordinate resources
         b. Organizational excellence; innovation capability
      6. Why intangibles and capabilities matter most
         a. Intangible nature; socially complex
         b. Harder to copy; durable
         c. Human-based; sometimes “made of complementary components”
   E. Strategic Analysis Of Resources And Capabilities
      1. Generating competitive advantage: identify relevance to industry success factors
      2. Sustaining competitive advantage: inimitability; durability; transferability; replicability
      3. Rent appropriation: who captures the value?
         a. Tangible easier to appropriate (owned)
         b. Intangible harder to appropriate (human capital can leave)
   F. Managing Resources
      1. Two main approaches noted
   G. Exploit (Strategy Options Noted)
      1. Business strategy: corporate strategy improves
      2. Internal development vs. external acquisition
   H. SWOT Notes (As Written)
      1. Strengths; weaknesses; opportunities; threats
      2. Advantage: simple; “familiar”; general
      3. Disadvantages: generic; can be “subjective”; can be “superficial”
