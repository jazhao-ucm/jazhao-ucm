I. Unit 6 Corporate Strategies: Development and Methods
   A. Key question: WHERE TO COMPETE?
      1. Firm scope = set of business(es) in which company wants to compete
   B. Development vs. growth
      1. Firm development = quantitative + qualitative changes derived of modification of scope of activity
      2. Firm growth = increases/decreases in size variables (volume of assets, outputs, sales, profitability, etc.)
      3. Corporate strategies (development) = set of growth/scope-change logics
         a. Specialization
         b. Diversification (related vs unrelated)
         c. Vertical integration
         d. Reestructuring
         e. Internationalization
   C. Methods for corporate strategies
      1. Internal growth
      2. Mergers & acquisitions (M&A)
      3. Strategic alliances

II. Firm Scope and Development (6.1)
   A. CHANGE FIRM'S SCOPE: development and growth
      1. Scope = business(es) targeted for competition
      2. Development = scope modification -> quantitative/qualitative changes
      3. Growth = size changes (assets, outputs, sales, profitability)
   B. Corporate strategies (development) taxonomy (recap)
      1. Specialization and diversification
      2. Vertical integration
      3. Reestructuring
      4. Interanationalization

III. Specialization & Diversification (6.2)
   A. SPECIALIZATION
      1. Reasons
         a. Economies of scale
         b. Consolidate position / strengthen competitive position
            i. Lower costs
            ii. More value added
         c. Increase sales via existing products
            i. More use of existing products
            ii. Seek new customers
            iii. Introduce certain improvements
         d. Low risk
            i. "Always doing the same products for the same customers needs"
   B. DIVERSIFICATION
      1. Reasons
         a. Economies of scope (synergies)
         b. Other reasons
            i. Improve competitive position
            ii. Enter attractive industries
            iii. Risk reducing
            iv. Obtain internal funding
            v. Exploit managerial capabilities
      2. Risk-spreading idea
         a. "You don't put all your eggs in one basket"
      3. Risks
         a. Synergies require effort
            i. Coordination
            ii. Commitment
         b. Greater inflexibility
         c. High diversification cost
            i. Entry barriers
            ii. High cost of internal funding, etc.

IV. Vertical (Des)Integration (6.3)
   A. Definition
      1. Vertically integrated if firm undertakes several activities related with the production process
   B. Reasons
      1. Easy coordination + planning of production process
      2. Reduce opportunism risk from external suppliers and clients
      3. Increase value added for customers
         a. High quality guarantee
         b. High service level guarantee
      4. Protect information + knowledge
         a. Advance technology
         b. New products
      5. Improve capacity to control final market
   C. Risks
      1. Augment risk of undertaking activities for the same final market
      2. More inflexibility
      3. Higher costs
         a. Administrative costs
         b. Lack of previous experience in vertically related activities
         c. Low production scale in each activity

V. Reestructuring Businesses (6.4)
   A. Definition
      1. Modification or redefinition of firm's scope
      2. Possible withdrawal (divestment) from at least one business
   B. Alternatives (scope-change patterns)
      1. Specializacion: focusing on core businesses
      2. Business portfolio reestructuring
         a. Both withdrawal and entry of businesses
      3. Business reestructuring
         a. To re-float a poor-performance business
   C. Two basic options
      1. REESTRUCTURING
         a. Possibilities of re-floating
         b. Measures
            i. Change managers
            ii. Redefine competitive strategy
            iii. Sailing assets
            iv. Refinancing debt
            v. Cost reduction
      2. WITHDRAWAL
         a. No solution
         b. Alternatives
            i. Sale
               - Best way for investment recovery
            ii. Harvest
               - Max short-term financial flows
            iii. Winding-up
               - End to all operations
               - Remaining assets sales
   D. Poor performance as trigger (link to profitability)
      1. One (or more) businesses not meeting firm profitability expectations
      2. Consequences
         a. Poor performance
            i. Decreasing sales
            ii. Decreasing profits
         b. Heavy borrowing
      3. Causes (examples)
         a. Inneficient management
         b. New competitors
         c. Unexpected changes in demand
         d. Bad competitive strategy
         e. High internal costs
         f. Organizational inertia
         g. Etc.
