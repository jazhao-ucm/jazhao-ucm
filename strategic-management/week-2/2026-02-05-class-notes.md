# Strategic Management - Class Notes
## February 5, 2026 - Week 2

**Focus:** Measuring company success, accounting vs. market valuation.

---

I. Measuring Company Success - Two Approaches
   A. Accounting Profitability (ROA, ROE)
      1. Works for small/medium companies
      2. Works for family-owned firms
   B. Market Valuation / Shareholder Value
      1. Works for listed companies
      2. Stock exchange traded

II. Market Valuation - Listed Companies
   A. When It Works
      1. Large companies on stock exchange
      2. Thousands of shareholders
   B. Calculation
      1. Share price end − share price start + dividends
      2. = Shareholder return
   C. Key Point
      1. Reflects many investors' views
      2. Not just one manager's calculation

III. Satisfaction and Opportunity Cost
   A. Concept
      1. Test if price change satisfies shareholders
      2. Minimum satisfaction = required return
   B. Benchmark
      1. Compare to main alternative
      2. Higher return than competitor → satisfied
   C. Key Insight
      1. Satisfaction = opportunity cost comparison

IV. Cost of Equity - Minimum Required Return
   A. Definition
      1. Minimum satisfaction = required return
   B. Three Components
      1. Risk-free rate (Rf)
         a. Government bonds
         b. Minimum baseline
      2. Market risk premium
         a. Market return − risk-free rate
         b. e.g., IBEX 35 return
      3. Beta (β)
         a. ~1 = average market risk
         b. >1 = more risk than market
         c. <1 = less risk than market
   C. Formula
      1. Cost of Equity = Rf + β × (Market Return − Rf)

V. Why Market Valuation Is Better (Four Reasons)
   A. External Market Calculation
      1. Price set by supply and demand
      2. External, verifiable
   B. Discounted Future Expectations
      1. Reflects many investors' views
      2. Better than one manager's calculation
   C. Harder to Manipulate
      1. Set by many participants
      2. Can verify if fair price
   D. Forward-Looking
      1. Reflects future expectations
      2. Based on investor consensus

VI. Limitation - Only Listed Companies
   A. Reality
      1. Only works for listed firms
      2. No market = impossible to calculate
   B. Who Can Use It
      1. Listed companies only
      2. Small/medium must use accounting
      3. 80-90% companies are private

VII. Companies as Social Realities
   A. Core Idea
      1. Companies = organizations of people
      2. People collaborating together
   B. Stakeholder View
      1. Economic entities + social entities
      2. Include workers, shareholders, customers, society
   C. Key Insight
      1. CSR, sustainability considerations
      2. Conflicts arise between stakeholder groups

VIII. Stakeholder Framework
   A. Framework
      1. Company objective = result of bargaining
      2. Between managers, shareholders, workers, society
   B. Why It Matters
      1. Legal fictions don't behave
      2. People behave
      3. Need to know social nature

IX. CSR and Sustainability
   A. Three Approaches
      1. Shareholder primacy (Chicago School, 1970s)
      2. Stakeholder theory (balance interests)
      3. CSR/Sustainability (social + environmental)

X. Externalities
   A. Definition
      1. Effects you create but don't pay/receive for
   B. Negative Externalities
      1. Pollution = don't pay for damage
      2. Market failure
      3. Options: reduce pollution or pay for it
   C. Positive Externalities
      1. Business growth → employment
      2. Growth → supplier revenue
      3. Growth → more taxes
   D. Key Insight
      1. Companies create unpaid effects

XI. Should Companies Consider Externalities?
   A. Question
      1. Moral/ethical obligation?
   B. Reality
      1. Current measures = economic only
      2. But people act collectively
      3. Should consider social/environmental

XII. Corporate Governance - Controlling Managers
   A. Problem
      1. Managers ≠ owners → agency problem
      2. Without control → company in manager hands
   B. M&A Example
      1. Fastest way to grow
      2. Appear in news, gain status
      3. But often destroys value (5-6 years)
      4. Premium paid using owner's money

XIII. Corporate Governance Mechanisms
   A. Carrots (Incentives)
      1. Stock options
         a. Right to shares in future
         b. At today's price
         c. Aligns manager w/ shareholder
      2. Performance bonuses
         a. Based on ROA or other metrics
         b. Free shares
   B. Sticks (Control)
      1. Board of directors
         a. In charge of shareholders
         b. Control managerial discretion
         c. Check strategic decisions
   C. Cost
      1. Both carrots and sticks = extra investment
      2. Shareholders pay to control managers

XIV. Criticisms of CSR
   A. Competitive Disadvantage
      1. CSR = voluntary expenses
      2. Competitors don't bear same costs
      3. How can you compete?
   B. Who Decides?
      1. Not customers, workers, or owners
      2. Only managers decide CSR spending
      3. Money w/ no return → not theirs
   C. Managerial Self-Interest
      1. Managers love CSR spending
      2. Increases status and image
      3. For free (to them)

XV. Arguments For CSR
   A. Climate Change
      1. All responsible for sustainability
      2. Citizens, students, voters, consumers
   B. Externalities
      1. Companies responsible for pollution
      2. Reduce or pay for it
   C. World Economic Forum (2020)
      1. Impossible to ignore social/environment
      2. Economic nature remains
      3. But not at any price

XVI. Business Sustainability vs. CSR
   A. CSR
      1. Consider stakeholders
      2. Invest in voluntary activities
      3. Cover social/environmental needs
   B. Sustainability
      1. Change strategy and DNA
      2. Change technology, products, processes
      3. Minimize negative impacts
      4. Fundamental change required

XVII. Ecoalf Example (Spanish Fashion)
   A. Case
      1. Materials from Mediterranean plastics
      2. 10,000+ fishermen collect
   B. Business Model
      1. Make money AND clean the sea
      2. "Emotional wealth"
      3. Take from nature, give back

XVIII. Ethical Principles in Business
   A. Core Idea
      1. Businesses run by people
      2. People have ethics
   B. Implication
      1. Moral/ethical issues should be included
      2. In transactions and relationships

---

## Key Takeaways

1. Two ways to measure: accounting (ROA/ROE) vs. market valuation
2. Market valuation: external, forward-looking, harder to manipulate
3. Satisfaction = opportunity cost (compare to alternative)
4. Cost of equity = Rf + β × (Market Return − Rf)
5. Market valuation only for listed (80-90% are private)
6. Companies = social realities w/ multiple stakeholders
7. Stakeholder framework: objectives from negotiation/power
8. Externalities: positive (employment, taxes) and negative (pollution)
9. Corporate governance: carrots (incentives) + sticks (oversight)
10. CSR criticisms: competitive disadvantage, agency problem
11. Sustainability vs. CSR: fundamental change vs. superficial
12. Ethics matter: businesses run by people w/ values
