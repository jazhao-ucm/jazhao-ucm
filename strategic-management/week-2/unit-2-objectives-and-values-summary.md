# Section 1: Summary (AP Level / Accessible)

*Use this section first. Plain language. Short sentences.*

## Key Words
- **Mission** = Firm’s purpose and identity; present; “what is the essence of my business?”
- **Vision** = Future; 10+ years; “what will we be?”
- **Strategic objectives** = Measurable targets with attribute, yardstick, target, timeframe.
- **Stakeholders** = People/groups related to the firm; each has own objectives.
- **Shareholder vs stakeholder** = Friedman (maximize shareholder wealth) vs Freeman (stakeholder satisfaction, CSR).
- **Corporate governance** = Mechanisms to align owners and managers (incentives, Board of Directors).
- **CSR** = Corporate Social Responsibility; voluntary activities for social/environmental demands.
- **Business ethics** = What is right/wrong in strategic decisions and transactions.

## Main Ideas (Simple Version)

1. **Mission = present; Vision = future.**
   - Mission: firm’s reason, identity, scope, capabilities, values. Stable.
   - Vision: CEO’s task; inspirational; 10+ years. Stable and clear.

2. **Strategic objectives: attribute + yardstick + target + timeframe.**
   - Example: “100% zero carbon electricity by 2030” (Disney).

3. **Performance: to whom?**
   - Accounting: profit, ROA, ROE.
   - Market: share price, market cap.
   - Social/environmental: impact, emissions.

4. **Shareholder vs stakeholder view.**
   - Friedman: managers maximize shareholder wealth.
   - Stakeholder theory: triple bottom line (economic, social, environmental); CSR; sustainability.

5. **Corporate governance: ownership separated from management.**
   - Incentives (bonuses, stock options).
   - Board of Directors: strategic advice, supervision, gate between owners and managers.

6. **Values: end does not justify the means.**
   - Guide business activities; ethical behaviour; stakeholders’ engagement.

# Section 2: Full Notes (from Unit 2 Slides)

## 2.1. Mission, Vision and Strategic Objectives

### A. Corporate Mission
- **What:** Firm’s existing reason; source of corporate identity.
- **When:** Present.
- **Characteristics:** Stable over time; statement of principles.
- **Variables:** Scope, core capabilities, values, beliefs, behaviour.

### B. Corporate Vision
- **What:** What we will be, want to do; 10 years and beyond.
- **Who:** Main task of CEO (the Visionary).
- **Characteristics:** Stable, clear, inspirational, inclusive; co-joint effort.

### C. Strategic Objectives
- **Purpose:** Gap between present and future.
- **Components:**
  1. Measurable attribute (profitability, revenue growth, etc.).
  2. Yardstick (ROE, ROA, % growth).
  3. Target (e.g. 7.5% ROE, 4.5% revenues growth in Spain).
  4. Timeframe (within 2 years, by 2030).

---

## 2.2. Value Creation and Stakeholders

### A. Firm Performance
- **Importance:** Aim of strategic decisions; assess success/failure; assess CEO and TMT.
- **Issues:** Difficult to measure; “performance” to whom?; success depends on definition.

### B. Measurement

| Type | Examples |
|------|----------|
| **Accounting** | Gross margin, EBIT, Net income, ROA, ROE |
| **Market** | Market cap, shareholder value (share price increase) |
| **Social/Environmental** | Positive impact, shared value, emissions minimization |

### C. Stakeholders
- Person or group related to the firm.
- Each has own objectives (shareholders, managers, workers, customers, competitors, public administrations).
- They try to condition the firm’s objectives.

### D. Chicago School (Friedman)
- Managers’ only responsibility: maximize shareholder wealth.
- Shareholder value creation; shareholder supremacy.

### E. Stakeholder Theory (Freeman, 1984): Sustainability
- Triple bottom line: economic, social, environmental.
- Stakeholder satisfaction; long-term orientation.
- CSR; sustainability; sustainable development.

---

## 2.3. Corporate Governance

### A. Ownership vs Management
- **Separation:** Owners (shareholders) ≠ Managers.
- **Problem:** Who establishes objectives? Managers may pursue own interests (remuneration, power, status).

### B. Mechanisms
1. **Incentives:** Bonuses, stock options, shares align managers with owners.
2. **Board of Directors:**
   - Strategic advice.
   - Management supervision.
   - Gate between owners and managers.

---

## 2.4. Corporate Values

### A. Definition (Guerras & Navas, 2020)
Set of principles, beliefs, standards and commitments designed to steer progress toward vision and mission.

### B. Key Ideas
- Guide business activities and performance.
- Ethical behaviour.
- Stakeholders’ engagement.
- Key ingredient for organizational culture, mission and vision.
- **The end does not justify the means.**

### C. CSR and Business Ethics

| Concept | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| **CSR** | Voluntary activities to meet social/environmental demands of stakeholders |
| **Business ethics** | Moral foundations; what is right/wrong in strategic decisions and transactions |

From profit maximization → purpose. From shareholder primacy → stakeholder engagement.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Mission (present) + Vision (future) + Strategic objectives (measurable, with timeframe).
2. Performance: accounting, market, social/environmental.
3. Stakeholder theory: triple bottom line, CSR, sustainability.
4. Corporate governance: incentives, Board of Directors.
5. Values: guide behaviour; end does not justify means.
