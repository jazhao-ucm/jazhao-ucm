# Strategic Management - Class Notes
## February 4, 2026 - Week 2

**Focus:** Mission, vision, and strategic objectives.

---

I. Mission, Vision, Objectives - Why They Matter
   A. Core Idea
      1. Clear chance to be successful requires clarity
      2. Must know what you want
   B. Strategic Point of View
      1. Mission = who am I? (current state)
      2. Vision = what do I want to become? (future)
      3. Strategic objectives = steps from mission to vision

II. Mission - Current State
   A. For People
      1. Define current situation
      2. Example: instructor 70% research, 30% teaching
   B. For Companies
      1. Define what business(es) you compete in
      2. Two dimensions:
         a. Set of products/services
         b. Target market(s)
   C. Keep It Simple
      1. "I produce and sell cars"
      2. "I produce mineral water"
      3. Clear, one sentence

III. Vision - Future State
   A. Time Horizon
      1. Long-term = 10+ years
      2. What will we be in 10-20 years?
   B. Characteristics
      1. Successful and worthy
      2. Aspirational: "number one in the world"
      3. Often not fully achievable (ideal, inspiration)
   C. Example
      1. Coca-Cola: everyone on Earth can have Coke in 2 min
      2. Impossible but inspirational

IV. Strategic Objectives - Bridge Mission to Vision
   A. Role
      1. Gap between mission and vision = steps to follow
B. Characteristics
      1. Time frame: 6 months to 5 years
      2. Step by step, concrete actions
      3. Measurable: targets, numbers, ratios

V. Each Company Is Different
   A. Mission, Vision, Objectives Are Unique
      1. Each person's mission totally different
      2. Each company's specific products/markets unique
   B. Implication
      1. No one-size-fits-all
      2. Depends on identity and context

VI. Economic Nature - Making Money
   A. First Approach
      1. Companies make money, profits
   B. Why Profit Is Problematic
      1. Accounting profit = relative, manipulable
      2. Different rules → different results
      3. 30+ issues can change outcome
   C. Components
1. Revenues − expenses = gross profit
      2. − taxes, − interest = net income

VII. Relative Measures - Profitability Ratios
   A. Problem w/ Absolute Profit
      1. Company A: 1M profit, Company B: 1.5M profit
      2. Who performed better? Depends on resources
B. Example
      1. A: 1M profit, 100 workers = 10,000/worker
      2. B: 1.5M profit, 200 workers = 7,500/worker
      3. A performs better per worker
   C. Key Insight
      1. Use ratios for meaningful comparisons
      2. Relative terms, not absolute

VIII. Two Main Ratios - ROA and ROE
   A. ROA (Return on Assets)
      1. Profit / total assets
      2. Measures efficiency of asset use
   B. ROE (Return on Equity)
      1. From shareholder point of view
      2. Return on shareholder investment
   C. Why Use Ratios
      1. Compare across companies, countries, industries
      2. Absolute profit doesn't allow comparison

IX. Context Matters - Benchmarking
   A. "10% Means Nothing by Itself"
      1. Need more information
   B. Ways to Interpret
      1. Industry: if avg = 8%, then 10% = competitive advantage
      2. Competitor: if rival = 7%, then 10% = better performance
   C. Key Insight
      1. Success = compare to benchmark
      2. Situation, history, circumstances matter

X. Information Asymmetry - Who Calculates?
   A. Who Has Power Over Numbers?
      1. CFO = only one w/ final calculation power
   B. Agency Problem
      1. If bonus tied to hitting target...
      2. CFO may manipulate to reach target
   C. Link to Corporate Governance
      1. Core issue: control managers
      2. Different mechanisms needed

XI. Companies as Social Realities
   A. Legal View
      1. Companies = legal fictions
      2. Good invention to avoid responsibility
   B. Social View
      1. Companies = social realities
      2. Organizations = people working together
   C. Implication
      1. Understand as complex social realities
      2. People w/ individual motivations
      3. Different utility functions

XII. Stakeholder Utility Functions
   A. Shareholders
      1. Maximize return on investment
   B. Managers
      1. Power, status, remuneration
   C. Workers
      1. Higher remuneration, better conditions
   D. Customers
      1. High quality at lowest price
   E. Reality
      1. No resources to satisfy everyone
      2. Must choose → tension
      3. Utility functions differ

XIII. Who Governs? Objectives Depend on Power
   A. If Governed by Owners
      1. Objective = make money for shareholders
   B. If Governed by Managers
      1. Objective = growth, diversification
      2. Growth → more power, remuneration, status
   C. If Governed by Workers/Unions
      1. Spain, France, Germany: unions powerful
      2. University example: professors want better conditions
      3. Profitability secondary

XIV. Corporate Governance
   A. Problem
      1. Company in manager hands w/o control
      2. Only purpose = growth (not profitability)
   B. Solution
      1. Corporate governance mechanisms
   C. Examples
1. Board of directors
2. Audits
      3. Incentive design

XV. Defining Success - Satisfaction vs. Maximization
   A. Personal Level
      1. What means success for you?
   B. Examples from Class
      1. House = success (Spanish view)
      2. Money = satisfaction level
      3. Family, salary, stability
   C. Strategic Implication
      1. Satisfaction more real than maximization
      2. Set concrete targets

XVI. Satisfaction and Opportunity Cost
   A. Concept
      1. Compare returns w/ alternatives
   B. Spain Example (Santander vs. BBVA)
      1. Santander 8%, BBVA 7% → satisfied
      2. Santander 8%, BBVA 12% → unsatisfied
   C. General Rule
      1. Satisfaction = opportunity cost
      2. Compare to best alternative

XVII. Cost of Equity
   A. Idea
      1. Minimum satisfaction for shareholders
      2. Required return
   B. Intuition
      1. Satisfied if return > alternative
      2. Compare to competitor returns

---

## Key Takeaways

1. Mission = who am I? (current, one sentence)
2. Vision = what do I want to be? (10-20 years, aspirational)
3. Strategic objectives = measurable, time-bound steps
4. Each company different; no universal mission/vision
5. Profit relative/manipulable; need ratios (ROA, ROE)
6. Context matters; 10% good/bad depends on benchmark
7. CFO/managers have discretion; governance needed
8. Companies = social realities w/ multiple stakeholders
9. Who governs shapes objectives (owners → profit; managers → growth)
10. Success = satisfaction; compare to opportunity cost
