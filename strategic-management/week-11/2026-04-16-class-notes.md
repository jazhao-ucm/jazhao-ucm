## Formal outline (Strategic Management, Week 11)

I. Final exam framing and expected answer style.
   A. Exam structure.
      1. Cumulative final covering Units 1 to 7 topics in applied format.
      2. Two short business cases with different questions.
   B. Materials policy.
      1. Open-note format with printed materials.
      2. Slides prioritized as primary study source over full textbooks.
   C. Scoring emphasis.
      1. Correct label matters.
      2. Argument quality and justification carries most weight.

II. Case diagnosis sequence required in answers.
   A. First step, identify and label strategy type.
      1. Internationalization method, alliance, acquisition, internal development.
      2. Business-level strategy, cost leadership, differentiation, hybrid, focus.
      3. Corporate-level strategy, specialization, related diversification, unrelated diversification, vertical integration.
   B. Second step, explain why the label fits.
      1. Use explicit evidence from case text.
      2. Connect facts to framework criteria, not only intuition.
   C. Third step, evaluate drivers and risks.
      1. Strategic drivers, scale, power, positioning, market access.
      2. Economic drivers, profitability, cost pressure, recession context.
      3. Organizational and private drivers where relevant.

III. Required framework integration in case analysis.
   A. External analysis.
      1. Industry structure and Five Forces logic.
      2. Macro and microeconomic context effects on viability.
   B. Internal analysis, Unit 4.
      1. Resource and capability assessment.
      2. Value chain mapping, logistics, HR, technology, brand, operations.
      3. Resource criteria application for strategic strength tests.
   C. Competitive and corporate strategy bridge.
      1. Positioning choice must match capability base.
      2. Corporate scope move must match strategic motive and execution capacity.

IV. Internationalization and market-entry method interpretation.
   A. Entry-mode labeling in cases.
      1. Acquisition of domestic firm in target country as internationalization through M and A.
      2. Alliance as cooperative external development with lower ownership integration.
   B. India and Brazil style examples.
      1. Geographic expansion rationale and local capability access.
      2. Country-specific risks and integration complexity.
   C. Risk categories.
      1. Economic and market risk.
      2. Integration and organizational risk.
      3. Strategic misfit and execution risk.

V. Mergers and acquisitions, motives, benefits, and drawbacks.
   A. Motive categories.
      1. Economic motives, profitability, efficiency, scale.
      2. Strategic motives, market power, brand strengthening, positioning.
      3. Personal or managerial motives that can be difficult to classify.
   B. Potential advantages.
      1. Faster growth and capability acquisition.
      2. Rival reduction and possible oligopolistic power increase.
      3. Access to new markets and distribution channels.
   C. Main drawbacks and risks.
      1. High cost and irreversible commitment.
      2. Cultural and system integration failure.
      3. Overestimated synergies and value-destruction risk.

VI. Strategy recommendation logic under recession scenario.
   A. Problem setup.
      1. Firm currently positioned on high differentiation with premium price.
      2. Expected recession reduces customer purchasing power.
   B. Recommended strategic adjustment.
      1. Move toward hybrid strategy.
      2. Preserve core quality signal while reducing price to protect demand.
      3. Recalibrate value proposition for constrained consumption period.
   C. Justification structure.
      1. State the position change.
      2. Explain economic mechanism.
      3. Identify implementation risks and controls.

VII. Argument quality over single-right-answer logic.
   A. Complex decisions permit multiple defensible conclusions.
      1. Related versus unrelated diversification can be ambiguous in borderline cases.
      2. Quality of reasoning can validate different labels.
   B. Evaluation principle.
      1. Strong argument links facts, framework criteria, and conclusion.
      2. Weak argument gives label without mechanism.
   C. Practical exam implication.
      1. Prioritize coherent reasoning chain.
      2. Use precise strategic vocabulary from slides.

VIII. Study method guidance from class.
   A. Best preparation approach.
      1. Repeated writing and rewriting of notes for deep retention.
      2. Active case practice rather than passive reading only.
   B. Slides as core tool.
      1. Slides combine theory and applied exhibits.
      2. Adequate for strong preparation when understood, not memorized blindly.
   C. Tutoring and support note.
      1. Instructor invites targeted follow-up for difficult topics.
      2. Focus on grasping strategic reasoning process.

- Finals are cumulative and case-based, with strong emphasis on labeling plus justification.
- Use integrated analysis, Five Forces, internal analysis, value chain, resources/capabilities, and strategy type.
- For each case, identify strategy, explain drivers, and evaluate main risks.
- In recession conditions, a differentiated firm may need a hybrid shift with defended value logic.
- Argument quality is the key grading lever in complex strategic questions.
