# Strategic Management - Week 10 Vocabulary A-Z

- **Advantage Renewal** - Ongoing adaptation needed to sustain performance over time.
- **Barriers to Entry** - Obstacles that make new competitor entry difficult.
- **Capability** - Coordinated ability to deploy resources effectively.
- **Competitive Advantage** - Superior position versus rivals that improves performance.
- **Cost Leadership** - Lower-cost position for comparable value offerings.
- **Differentiation** - Perceived uniqueness valued by customers.
- **Economies of Scale** - Lower unit costs as output increases.
- **Economies of Scope** - Shared resources reducing costs across activities/businesses.
- **Firm Scope** - Set of businesses where a firm chooses to compete.
- **Five Forces** - Framework for industry structure and attractiveness.
- **Industry Attractiveness** - Degree to which industry conditions support returns.
- **PESTEL** - Macro-environment analysis: political, economic, social, technological, ecological, legal.
- **Resource-Based View (RBV)** - Internal analysis focus on strategic resources/capabilities.
- **Restructuring** - Redefining scope via turnaround, portfolio changes, or divestment.
- **Strategy Clock** - Price-value positioning extension to Porter generic strategies.
- **Stuck in the Middle** - Weak position with no clear cost or differentiation edge.
- **Sustainable Competitive Advantage** - Advantage that remains defensible over time.
- **SWOT** - Matrix of strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, threats.
- **Value Chain** - Set of linked activities creating and delivering value.
- **Vertical Integration** - Control of multiple stages in the value chain.
- **VRIO** - Valuable, Rare, Inimitable, Organized criteria for sustained advantage.
