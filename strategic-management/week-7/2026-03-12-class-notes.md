# Strategic Management - Class Notes

**Focus:** Unit 5 – Competitive Strategy: competitive advantage, Porter's generic strategies, strategy clock, "stuck in the middle."

I. Competitive Advantage and Strategy
   A. Competitive Advantage
      1. Firm features distinguishing from rivals
      2. Better position in market
      3. Linked to key success factors (cost, quality, brand)
      4. Must be substantial and sustainable
   B. Competitive Strategy
      1. Offensive-defensive actions
      2. Build defendable position
      3. Achieve superior performance
      4. Q: "How do we compete to overcome competitors?"

II. Cost Leadership Strategy
   A. Definition
      1. Costs lower than competitors/industry avg
      2. For product of similar features/quality
   B. Allows Firm To
      1. Maintain industry price → higher margins
      2. OR cut prices → gain market share
   C. Drivers of Cost Advantage
      1. Economies of scale
         a. Input-output relations
         b. Indivisibilities, specialization
      2. Learning economies
         a. Accumulated experience
         b. Improved skills and routines
      3. Production techniques
         a. Process innovation
         b. Re-engineering
      4. Product design
         a. Standardization
         b. Design for manufacture
      5. Input costs
         a. Cheap raw materials
         b. Favorable location
         c. Bargaining power
      6. Capacity utilization
         a. Spreading fixed costs
         b. Lean/JIT production
      7. Residual efficiency (X-efficiency)
         a. Organization quality
         b. Employee motivation

III. Differentiation Strategy
   A. Definition
      1. Uniqueness perception by customers
      2. Offer seen as different and preferable
   B. Bases for Differentiation
      1. Brand
      2. Quality
      3. Design
      4. Technology
      5. Service
      6. Image
   C. Conditions for Success
      1. Attributes must matter to customers
      2. Difference difficult to copy quickly
      3. Extra price/loyalty compensates extra costs

IV. Focus Strategy and "Stuck in the Middle"
   A. Focus
      1. Choose narrow segment
      2. Specific customer group, geography, niche
      3. Combine w/ cost focus OR differentiation focus
   B. Stuck in the Middle
      1. No clear cost advantage
      2. No clear differentiation
      3. Low margins, weak loyalty
      4. Common risk when mixing w/o coherence

V. Risks of Cost Leadership
   A. Ford Model T Example
      1. One color (black), one model, same tech
      2. Massive economies of scale, cheapest car
      3. BUT market tastes changed
      4. Customers wanted variety, style
      5. GM understood: same platform, different configs
      6. Ford's system became obsolete
   B. Main Risks
      1. Market tastes change
         a. Customers stop caring only about price
         b. Want features, style, experience
      2. Technology disruption
         a. New tech makes system worthless
         b. Learning and scale disappear overnight
      3. Quality reduction backfires
         a. Cheaper materials → customers notice
         b. Leave for slightly better quality
      4. Competitors catch up
         a. Match your cost position
         b. No advantage left

VI. X-Efficiency (Residual Efficiency)
   A. Definition
      1. "Art" of getting more from same inputs
      2. Depends on motivation, management, culture
   B. Example
      1. Flamenco "duende" = cannot teach
      2. But makes all the difference
   C. Key Point
      1. Hard to copy (lives in people, not machines)

VII. Industry Examples
   A. Spanish Supermarket (Mercadona-style)
      1. Cost leadership: standardized, efficient logistics
      2. High volume, low margins
      3. Buyer power high (easy to switch)
   B. Energy/Renewables (Spain, EU)
      1. Five Forces applied:
         a. Rivalry: few big players
         b. Substitutes: renewable vs. fossil
         c. Supplier power: tech providers
         d. Buyer power: govts, large industrial
         e. Entry barriers: capital, regulation, tech
      2. Attractiveness depends on country policy
   C. Professional Services vs. Commodities
      1. Commodities: many customers, low margins, price-based
      2. Professional services: few clients, high margins, relationship-based

VIII. Industry Evolution and Strategy
   A. Different Stages Favor Different Strategies
      1. Early: innovation, differentiation, experimentation
      2. Mature: cost efficiency, consolidation, focus
      3. Decline: harvesting, exit, or niche

## Key Takeaways

I. Key Takeaways
   A. Summary Points
      1. Competitive advantage = do something important BETTER THAN rivals → earn more
      2. Cost leadership = cheapest for normal quality
      3. Differentiation = special, so people pay more or stay loyal
      4. Focus = pick small group, be cheap or special for them
      5. Stuck in the middle = not cheap, not special → bad position
      6. Cost drivers: scale, learning, production, design, inputs, capacity, X-efficiency
      7. Ford Model T: worked until tastes changed; GM won w/ variety
      8. Risks of cost leadership: taste change, tech disruption, quality cuts, competitors catch up
      9. X-efficiency = "art" part; motivation, culture (like flamenco duende)
      10. Strategy clock: low price/OK value OR higher value/higher price

X. 30-Second Cold Call Version.
A. 3-Line Speaking Frame.
   1. TOPIC: STATE THE CORE QUESTION IN ONE LINE.
   2. LOGIC: GIVE 2 DRIVERS/FACTORS THAT EXPLAIN THE ANSWER.
   3. ACTION: END WITH ONE DECISION OR IMPLICATION.
B. Delivery Rule.
   1. 25-30 SECONDS MAX.
   2. NO BACKGROUND STORY; GO STRAIGHT TO CLAIM + EVIDENCE + CLOSE.

CI. Oral Answer Template (60-90 Seconds, Class Use).
A. Opening.
   1. STATE THE CORE QUESTION OR TOPIC IN ONE LINE.
   2. STATE YOUR MAIN CLAIM/THESIS IN ONE LINE.
B. Logic.
   1. GIVE 2-3 KEY FACTORS OR DRIVERS.
   2. LINK EACH FACTOR TO THE CLAIM (CAUSE -> EFFECT).
C. Action Or Implication.
   1. END WITH ONE DECISION, RECOMMENDATION, OR CONSEQUENCE.
   2. CLOSE CLEARLY WITHOUT ADDING NEW IDEAS.
D. Delivery Rule.
   1. 60-90 SECONDS.
   2. CLAIM -> EVIDENCE -> CLOSE.
