# Section 1: Summary (AP Level / Accessible)

*Use this section first. Plain language. Short sentences.*

## Key Words
- **Value chain** = Porter’s breakdown of a firm into primary and supporting activities. Where does value get created?
- **Primary activities** = Inbound logistics, operations, outbound logistics, marketing, service.
- **Supporting activities** = Procurement, HR, technology, infrastructure.
- **Resources** = What the firm has (tangible, intangible, human).
- **Capabilities** = How the firm combines resources to do things well.
- **VRIN / VRIO** = Valuable, Rare, Inimitable, (Organized to capture) — criteria for sustained competitive advantage.
- **SWOT** = Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats. Summary of internal + external analysis.

## Main Ideas (Simple Version)

1. **Internal diagnosis has three tools: firm identity, value chain, resources & capabilities.**
   - Start with who the firm is (size, scope, ownership, activity).
   - Then map where value is created (value chain).
   - Then assess resources and capabilities (RBV).

2. **Porter’s value chain: primary + supporting activities.**
   - Primary: logistics, operations, marketing, service.
   - Supporting: procurement, HR, tech, infrastructure.
   - Advantage can come from activities themselves, horizontal links (inside the firm), or vertical links (with suppliers/buyers).

3. **Resources: tangible, intangible, human.**
   - Tangible: physical, financial.
   - Intangible: reputation, technology, culture.
   - Human: know-how, communication, motivation.
   - Intangibles and capabilities are often the main sources of sustained advantage.

4. **Strategic analysis of R&C: generating, sustaining, appropriating.**
   - **Generating:** Scarcity (not available to many) + Relevance (fits key industry factors).
   - **Sustaining:** Durability, Inimitability, Low tradeability, Limited substitutability, Complementarity.
   - **Appropriating:** Who captures the value? Tangible = easy; intangible = harder.

5. **SWOT summarizes everything.**
   - Strengths → maintain. Weaknesses → correct.
   - Opportunities → exploit. Threats → avoid.
   - Simple and popular, but static; no recipe for strategy.

## 4.1. A Firm’s Internal Diagnosis

| Variable | Options |
|----------|---------|
| **Size** | Micro, Small, Medium-sized, Large |

### B. Strategic Profile
- Assess functional areas (Operations, Marketing, Finance, People, etc.) vs. main rival or industry average.
- Scale: VN (very negative) → N → N → P → VP (very positive).
- **Advantages:** Simple, intuitive.
- **Disadvantages:** Subjective, lack of comparison.

## 4.2. The Value Chain (Porter, 1985)

### A. Definition
- A firm’s breakdown into key operations and activities necessary to produce a good / deliver a service.
- **Value chain** = firm’s own activities.
- **Value system** = includes suppliers’ and customers’ value chains.

### B. Structure
- **Primary activities:** Inbound logistics, Operations, Outbound logistics, Marketing & sales, Service.
- **Supporting activities:** Firm infrastructure, HRM, Technology development, Procurement.

### C. Sources of Competitive Advantage
1. Primary and supporting activities (by themselves).
2. **Horizontal links** (inside the company’s value chain): coordination, optimization.
3. **Vertical links** (with suppliers/buyers): coordination, optimization.

### D. Use
- Identify strengths and weaknesses.
- Inform outsourcing vs. core competence decisions.
- Find sources of sustained competitive advantage (SCA).

### E. Examples
- **Ford (Valencia):** Vertical links in factory.
- **Mercadona:** Value chain and system — primary & supporting activities, horizontal links (1 coordination, 1 optimization), vertical links (1 coordination, 1 optimization).

## 4.3. Analysis of Resources and Capabilities

### A. Resource-Based View (RBV)
- **Key authors:** Barney, Grant, Nonaka, Teece.
- **Key premises:** Firm heterogeneity; imperfect mobility of resources and capabilities.
- **Aim:** Discover and assess internal strengths (strategic resources/capabilities).

### B. Resources (Grant, 2015)
| Type | Examples |
|------|----------|
| **Tangible** | Physical, Financial |
| **Intangible** | Reputation, Technology, Culture |
| **Human** | Know-how, Communication, Motivation |

**Measurement:** Tangible (financial, material); Intangible (human-based, non-human-based, organizational, technological).

### C. Capabilities (Guerras & Navas, 2020)
- A suitable combination and coordination of individual resources.
- **Core/Distinctive competences:** Performing better than competitors.
- **Organizational routines:** Regular, predictable patterns of activities.
- **Types:** Functional (performing activities), Cultural (ways to integrate), Dynamic.

### D. Strategic Analysis of R&C — Three Dimensions

**1. Generating Competitive Advantage**
- **Scarcity:** Not available to “many” competitors. Heterogeneity.
- **Relevance:** Expediency for competing in the industry; overlap with key industry factors.

**2. Sustaining Competitive Advantage**
- **Durability:** Maintain potential over time.
- **Inimitability:** Limited competitor ability to replicate.
- **Low tradeability:** Limited transferability among firms.
- **Limited substitutability:** Limited alternatives providing same service.
- **Complementarity:** Joint value exceeds sum of individual parts.

**3. Rent Appropriation**
- **Appropriability:** Control rights over R&C and resulting rents.
- Tangible → well-defined, easy appropriation.
- Intangible → medium-defined, moderate appropriation.
- Other intangible → undefined, hard appropriation.

### E. Managing R&C
- **Exploiting:** Internal exploitation; corporate & business; external exploitation.
- **Improving provision:** External acquisition; internal development.

### F. Critical Thinking (Illustration 4.2)
- Why are intangible resources and capabilities the main sources of sustained competitive advantage?

### G. Case: Inditex (Illustration 4.3)
- Identify, measure, and strategically analyze: 2 resources (tangible & intangible) and 1 key capability.

## 4.4. SWOT Analysis

### A. Definition
- Resumes external (Opportunities & Threats) and internal (Strengths & Weaknesses) analyses.

### B. Advantages
- Popular and standard.
- Overall view.
- Simple.

### C. Disadvantages

- Lack of integration.
- No strategy formulation recipe.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Internal diagnosis** uses firm identity, value chain, and resources & capabilities.
2. **Value chain** (Porter): primary + supporting activities; advantage from activities, horizontal links, vertical links.
3. **RBV:** Resources (tangible, intangible, human) + Capabilities (combination, coordination).
4. **Strategic analysis:** Generating (scarcity, relevance), Sustaining (durability, inimitability, etc.), Appropriating (who captures value).
5. **SWOT** summarizes internal and external analysis; useful but static.
