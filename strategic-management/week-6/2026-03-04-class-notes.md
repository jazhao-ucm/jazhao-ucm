I. Madonna vs. Lola Flores - Economic vs. Social Success
   A. Purpose
      1. Compare types of "value"
      2. Judge strategic resources
   B. Madonna
      1. Among most profitable artists on Earth
      2. Economic success: revenue, profitability, reach
      3. Clearly "valuable" in market terms
   C. Lola Flores
      1. Enormous social contribution
      2. Working class, culture, fashion, identity, radio
      3. "Did more than profit" for society
   D. Lesson
      1. Both = human-based strategic resources
      2. Assess value in multiple ways (economic + social)

II. Sustainable Advantage - Not "The Best"
   A. Main Idea
      1. Success ≠ being "the best" in absolute terms
      2. Need to be BETTER THAN competitors
      3. MAINTAIN that position over time
   B. Implication
      1. Strategy = relative position + durability
      2. Personal and org attributes matter

III. Resource-Based View (RBV) and Internal Analysis
   A. Role of RBV
      1. One of most important theories
      2. Explains why some firms succeed
      3. Complements external analysis (PEST, Five Forces)
   B. Logic
      1. Identify firm's resources (tangible + intangible)
      2. Do they contribute to competitive advantage?
      3. Can we sustain that advantage (VRIN)?
   C. Empirical Claim
      1. 70-80% of success/failure depends on internal factors
      2. Management, people, resources, capabilities
      3. Not only external environment

IV. Tangible vs. Intangible Resources
   A. Tangible Resources
      1. Physical: buildings, equipment, machinery, inventory
      2. Financial: cash, investments, receivables
      3. Visible in financial statements
      4. Easier to measure and control
   B. Intangible Resources
      1. Human-based: knowledge, skills, motivation, loyalty
         a. "Encapsulated in the person"
         b. Company does not "own" the person
      2. Technology
         a. Can be in patents (firm-owned)
         b. Or in people's heads (risky if leave)
      3. Reputation/brand
         a. Use can increase or decrease value
         b. Not fully in the books
   C. Key Difficulty
      1. When key people leave → lose person AND knowledge
      2. Hard to buy or copy if complementary
      3. Retention and culture matter

V. Human Resources and Competitive Advantage
   A. Why Central
      1. Among most valuable and rarest
      2. Motivation, behavior, loyalty, skills drive performance
      3. But intangible and not in financial statements
   B. Management Challenge
      1. Cannot "own" people; they can leave
      2. Retention from non-financial factors
         a. Culture, identity, belonging
         b. Example: football "all are champions"
      3. Incentives and environment must align
   C. Complementarity and Inimitability
      1. If complementary and hard to imitate
      2. Competitors cannot easily buy/replicate
      3. Example: star player in system

VI. Football Clubs and Reputation
   A. Examples (Real Madrid, Barcelona, Man City)
      1. Economically successful clubs
      2. Players = human resources
      3. Ronaldo, Messi = rare, valuable
   B. Lessons
      1. Human resources = main source of sustained advantage
      2. When they leave, org loses capability and knowledge
      3. Financial statements show only part (~70% intangibles)

VII. Effective Tax (Opening Context)
   A. Point Raised
      1. "Effective" taxation = real burden after rules/deductions
      2. "Effective" means real burden, not nominal rate

## Key Takeaways

1. Madonna vs. Lola Flores: economic vs. social value of strategic resources
2. Sustainable advantage > being "the best"; be better than rivals and maintain
3. RBV: internal resources explain much of success (70-80% internal factors)
4. Tangible (physical, financial) appear in statements; intangibles often don't
5. Human resources = most valuable but hardest to control; knowledge leaves w/ people
6. Football clubs: rare, valuable, complementary human resources
7. Reputation/brand = intangible that can increase or decrease w/ use
