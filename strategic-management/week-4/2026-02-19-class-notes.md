I. Bargaining Power - Suppliers and Buyers
   A. Strategic vs. Marketing Perspective
      1. Marketing: analyze customer behavior/preferences
      2. Strategic: only care about bargaining power
      3. Same logic for both suppliers and buyers
   B. What Bargaining Power Means
      1. Can impose: prices, conditions, quality
      2. Higher power = bad for company
      3. Goal: no organizing power for either
   C. Main Factors
      1. Concentration
         a. Few large buyers/suppliers = high power
         b. Concentration increases power
      2. Product differentiation
         a. Differentiated products → lower buyer power
         b. Specific raw materials → higher supplier power
      3. Information
         a. More info about costs/tech = more power
         b. Lower industry attractiveness
      4. Vertical integration
         a. Can suppliers become competitors?
         b. Higher integration = higher power = bad

II. Five Forces - Simplification of Reality
   A. Managerial Simplification
      1. Tools = simplifications of complex reality
      2. Humans have limited rationality
      3. Simplification = only way to picture
   B. Purpose
      1. Picture of industry environment
      2. Detect opportunities and threats
   C. Not All Forces Equally Relevant
      1. Depends on industry, country, time
      2. May need 4, 3, or 5 forces
      3. Can add additional force (e.g., sixth)

III. Sixth Force - Public Administration
   A. When to Add
      1. Especially relevant in Continental Europe
      2. France, Spain, Italy, Germany
   B. Spain Example
      1. Public admin moves ~45% of GDP
      2. In many sectors, weight even higher
   C. Key Point
      1. Critical in some countries/industries
      2. Omitting = losing critical understanding

IV. Industry Segmentation
   A. When Industry Is Heterogeneous
      1. Cannot apply Five Forces to entire industry
      2. Example: car industry (luxury vs. standard)
   B. Process
      1. Segment into smaller, homogeneous parts
      2. Apply Five Forces to each separately
   C. Strategic Groups
      1. Market segmentation in strategy
      2. Each group has specific dynamics

V. Country Attractiveness - Diamond Model
   A. Alternative External Tool
      1. Companies affected by country circumstances
      2. Michael Porter's Diamond Model
   B. World Economic Forum
      1. Davos, Switzerland (February)
      2. Global competitiveness report
      3. All countries analyzed
   C. Spain Example
      1. Competitive in: construction, banking, tourism
      2. ACS = 4th largest construction globally
      3. Tourism = 2nd after France (~100M tourists)

VI. Clusters - Geographic Agglomeration
   A. Concept
      1. Marshall - industrial districts (19th century)
      2. Physical concentration in few sq km
      3. Provides additional advantages
   B. Three Ingredients
      1. Many companies (hundreds/thousands)
         a. Same or similar products
         b. Can see competitors across street
      2. Suppliers/distributors nearby
         a. Minimizes transportation costs
         b. Geographical proximity = opportunities
      3. Universities/research centers
         a. Best training and tech knowledge
         b. Key ingredient for success
   C. Additional Advantages
      1. Talent attraction
         a. Workers move easily
         b. Same location, schools, lifestyle
      2. Innovation and creation
         a. Higher chance to create new products
      3. Technology and knowledge
         a. Universities develop w/ companies
      4. Knowledge spillovers
         a. Workers share (school, friends, BBQs)
         b. Innovation spreads faster
      5. Economies of scope
         a. Shared resources, info
         b. Best practices spread

VII. "Who Am I?" - Internal Analysis
   A. Third Strategic Question
      1. What I want? = mission, vision, objectives
      2. Where I am? = external (opportunities, threats)
      3. Who I am? = internal (strengths, weaknesses)
   B. Purpose
      1. Discover strengths (positive)
      2. Discover weaknesses (negative)
      3. Strategies based on strengths, never weaknesses
   C. Three Main Tools
      1. Strategic reflection (functional analysis)
         a. Analyze functional areas
         b. Detect strengths/weaknesses
      2. Value chain analysis (Porter)
         a. Core competencies and capabilities
         b. Activities we perform best
      3. Resource-based view
         a. Most developed analysis
         b. List all resources and abilities
         c. Apply filter: which are strategic?

VIII. SWOT Analysis
   A. Definition
      1. Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats
      2. Matrix combining internal (S, W) and external (O, T)
   B. Purpose
      1. Executive summary of all analysis

IX. Company Complexity
   A. Different Levels
      1. Focused: single industry, specialized
      2. Diversified: multiple industries, 5-10 businesses
   B. Implications
      1. Diversified = much higher complexity
      2. Need different analysis approaches
   C. Governance Example
      1. Family-owned vs. listed company
      2. Family: decisions protect reputation
      3. Different governance = different behavior

X. Value Chain - Competitive Advantage
   A. Two Levels
      1. Value chain: raw materials to final product
      2. Value system: company + suppliers + distributors
   B. Sources of Advantage
      1. In activities (primary or support)
         a. Where we perform better than rivals
         b. Core competencies
      2. In relationships between activities
         a. Horizontal links (HR + operations, marketing + R&D)
         b. Synergies between functional areas
      3. In vertical links (suppliers/distributors)
         a. Coordination outside company
         b. Toyota production system
   C. Primary vs. Support Activities
      1. Primary: inbound logistics, operations, outbound, marketing, service
      2. Support: infrastructure, HR, technology, procurement

XI. Toyota Production System
   A. Just-in-Time (JIT)
      1. Car produced only when ordered
      2. Suppliers/distributors physically close
      3. No inventories
      4. Orders in real time
   B. Benefits
      1. Coordination minimizes time and cost
      2. ~30% of car cost = inventory in traditional model
   C. Structure
      1. Majority of manufacturers only assemble
      2. Parts from suppliers

XII. Functional Analysis - Comparative Evaluation
   A. Process
      1. Classify by functional area
      2. Compare w/ industry avg or benchmark
      3. Rate: very negative to very positive
   B. Comparative Logic
      1. 7% ROE vs. industry 5% = positive
      2. 7% ROE vs. industry 12% = negative
      3. Same number, different meaning
   C. Use of Results
      1. Identifies main strengths and weaknesses
      2. If different tools reach same conclusion → higher confidence

## Key Takeaways

1. Bargaining power factors: concentration, differentiation, info, vertical integration
2. Five Forces = simplification; not all forces equally relevant
3. Sixth force (public admin) critical in Europe, especially Spain
4. Industry segmentation: apply Five Forces to each segment
5. Diamond model: country attractiveness analysis
6. Clusters: geographic agglomeration w/ universities, suppliers, many companies
7. Internal analysis: strengths/weaknesses via value chain, RBV, SWOT
8. Value chain: advantage from activities, relationships, or vertical links
9. Toyota/JIT: coordination w/ suppliers, no inventories
10. Functional analysis: compare w/ benchmark; triangulate w/ multiple tools

X. 30-Second Cold Call Version.
A. 3-Line Speaking Frame.
   1. TOPIC: STATE THE CORE QUESTION IN ONE LINE.
   2. LOGIC: GIVE 2 DRIVERS/FACTORS THAT EXPLAIN THE ANSWER.
   3. ACTION: END WITH ONE DECISION OR IMPLICATION.
B. Delivery Rule.
   1. 25-30 SECONDS MAX.
   2. NO BACKGROUND STORY; GO STRAIGHT TO CLAIM + EVIDENCE + CLOSE.

CI. Oral Answer Template (60-90 Seconds, Class Use).
A. Opening.
   1. STATE THE CORE QUESTION OR TOPIC IN ONE LINE.
   2. STATE YOUR MAIN CLAIM/THESIS IN ONE LINE.
B. Logic.
   1. GIVE 2-3 KEY FACTORS OR DRIVERS.
   2. LINK EACH FACTOR TO THE CLAIM (CAUSE -> EFFECT).
C. Action Or Implication.
   1. END WITH ONE DECISION, RECOMMENDATION, OR CONSEQUENCE.
   2. CLOSE CLEARLY WITHOUT ADDING NEW IDEAS.
D. Delivery Rule.
   1. 60-90 SECONDS.
   2. CLAIM -> EVIDENCE -> CLOSE.
