VI. Corporate Strategy (Unit 6) Slide 6.1 - Firm Scope and Development
   A. Change firm’s scope: development and growth.
      1. FIRM DEVELOPMENT.
         a. Quantitative and qualitative changes in the firm derived of modification of its scope of activity.
         b. Development Corporate Strategies: Specialization, Diversification (Related vs Unrelated), Vertical Integration, Reestructuring, Interanationalization.
      2. FIRM GROWTH.
         a. Increases (decreases) in the size of firm variables such as volumen of assets, outputs, sales, profitability, etc.
      3. Method Corporate Strategies: Internal Growth, Mergers & Acquisitions, Strategic Alliances.
   B. Firm’s scope, development and growth.
      1. Set of business(es) in which the company wants to compete.
      2. Addresses the Key Question: WHERE TO COMPETE?

VII. Corporate Strategy (Unit 6) Slide 6.2 - Specialization & Diversification
   A. SPECIALIZATION.
      1. Main Reasons:
         a. Economies of scale.
         b. Try to consolidate its position in the markets and strengthen its competitive position in the industry (lower costs or more value added).
         c. Increasing sales by increasing the use of their existing products, seek out new customers, introduce certain improvements in the products, etc.
         d. Considered as low risk strategy “always doing the same products for the same customers needs”
   B. DIVERSIFICATION.
      1. REASONS:
         a. Economies of Scope (Synergies).
         b. Other reasons:
            i. Improve the competitive position of the firm.
            ii. Enter attractive industries.
            iii. Risk reducing.
            iv. Obtain internal funding.
            v. Exploit managerial capabilities.
         c. “You don't put all your eggs in one basket”
      2. RISKS:
         a. The creation of synergies calls for great effort: coordination and commitment.
         b. It may lead to greater inflexibility.
         c. Diversification can have a high cost: entry barriers, high cost of internal funding, etc.

VIII. Corporate Strategy (Unit 6) Slide 6.3 - Vertical Integration
   A. Vertical (Des)Integration.
      1. Definition:
         a. A firm is vertically integrated if it undertakes several of activities related with the production process.
   B. Reasons.
      1. Easy coordination and planning of the production process.
      2. Reduce the opportunism risk from external suppliers and clients.
      3. Increase the value added for customers: a high level of quality or services can be guaranteed.
      4. Protect information and knowledge regarding advance technology or new products.
      5. Improve firms’ capacity to control the final market.
   C. Risks.
      1. Augment the risk of undertaking activities for the same final market.
      2. Firms may become more inflexible.
      3. It increases costs due to administrative costs, the lack of previous experience in vertically related activities and a low production scale in each activity

IX. Corporate Strategy (Unit 6) Slide 6.4 - Reestructuring businesses
   A. Restructuring.
      1. Definition:
         a. The modification or dedefinition of the firm’s scope with the possible withdrawal (DIVESTMENT) from at least ONE of its businesess.
      2. ALTERNATIVES:
         a. Specialización: Focusing on Core Businesses.
         b. Business Portfolio Reestructuring: Both withdrawal and entry of businesses.
         c. Business Reestructuring: To re-float a poor-performance business.
   B. TWO BASIC OPTIONS.
      1. REESTRUCTURING:
         a. THERE ARE POSSIBILITIES OF RE-FLOATING.
         b. MEASURES:
            i. Change managers.
            ii. Redefine Competitive Strategy.
            iii. Sailing assets.
            iv. Refinancing debt.
            v. Cost reduction.
      2. WITHDRAWAL:
         a. THERE IS NO SOLUTION.
         b. ALTERNATIVES:
            i. SALE: The best way for investment recovery.
            ii. HARVEST: Max. Short-Term financial Flows.
            iii. WINDING -UP: End to all operations & remaining Assets Sales.
      3. Consequences (when performance is below expectations in profitability):
         a. Poor Performance (Decreasing sales, profits).
         b. Heavy borrowing.
      4. Causes of poor performance:
         a. Inneficient management.
         b. New competitors.
         c. Unexpected changes in demand.
         d. Bad competitive strategy.
         e. High internal costs.
         f. Organizational intertia.
         g. Etc.
