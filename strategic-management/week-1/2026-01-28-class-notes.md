# Strategic Management - Class Notes
## January 28, 2026 - Week 1

**Focus:** Course introduction, materials, structure.

---

I. Strategy Definition and Origins
   A. Etymology
      1. Greek "strategos" = the general
      2. Art of commanding the army
   B. Modern Meaning
      1. Lead, decide, compete
      2. Compete for scarce resources: customers, talent, capital
   C. Key Insight
      1. Business strategy = long-term decisions
      2. Hard to reverse, lasting effects

II. Strategic vs. Tactical Decisions
   A. Strategic Decisions
      1. Long-term effects (years)
      2. Affect life, career, family
      3. Require serious thought and info
   B. Tactical Decisions
      1. Short-term focus
      2. Operational, day-to-day
   C. Examples
      1. University choice = strategic
      2. Career change = strategic
      3. Daily schedule = tactical

III. Complexity and Information
   A. Reality of Decision-Making
      1. Too many factors to process fully
      2. Brain cannot find optimal solution
      3. Must simplify to decide
   B. Course Approach
      1. Use models w/ 2-3 dimensions
      2. Big firms simplify to few strategic numbers
   C. Key Insight
      1. Best decisions = sequence of steps
      2. Assume simplification necessary

IV. Forecasting and Uncertainty
   A. Forecasting Limitations
      1. Impossible to predict accurately
      2. Conditions change: personal, economic, political
   B. Role of Foresight
      1. "Prospection" = imagining 2+ years ahead
      2. More uncertainty → more need to think ahead
   C. Key Point
      1. Unexpected events always possible
      2. Strategy must account for uncertainty

V. Art of War and Business
   A. Teaching Tool
      1. Sun Tzu / Art of War applied to business
   B. Analogies
      1. Battlefield → market
      2. Enemy → competitor
      3. General → leader/strategist
   C. Main Lessons
      1. Know yourself
      2. Know environment
      3. Decide w/ incomplete info

VI. Course Structure
   A. Split
      1. ~50% theory = principles, models, frameworks
      2. ~50% practice = cases, illustrations, group work
   B. Practice Components
      1. Business cases: read before, discuss, present
      2. Illustrations: shorter, find info yourself
   C. Key Point
      1. Combine theory w/ application
      2. Apply even when situations differ

VII. Learning Objectives
   A. Goals
      1. Learn strategic decision-making principles
      2. Understand no single "correct" answer
      3. Use interpretation, not just formulas
   B. Life Decision Examples
      1. Where to study → shapes career
      2. Where to live → job, family, cost
      3. Partner/children → huge impact
   C. Key Insight
      1. Most important decisions = one shot
      2. No second chance

VIII. Know Yourself
   A. First Principle for Success
      1. Love your thing
      2. Know context around you
   B. Why It Matters
      1. Unrealistic objectives → failure
      2. Know strengths/weaknesses → choose where to compete
   C. Connection to Strategy
      1. Like Chinese general idea
      2. Better chance if know self + environment

IX. Know External Environment
   A. Second Principle
      1. Know external context
   B. Components
      1. Opportunities = positive factors to use
      2. Threats = risks to prepare for/avoid
   C. Key Point
      1. Know what you want + where you are
      2. Then take actions fitting goals/situation

X. Long-Term Success and Stakeholders
   A. Short Term
      1. Operations, profit, current focus
   B. Long Term
      1. Consider stakeholders (not just shareholders)
      2. Workers, community, society, environment
   C. Contrast
      1. Friedman: business only for shareholder profit
      2. Course also covers stakeholder/sustainability views

XI. Grading and Assessment
   A. Split
      1. 50% final exam
      2. 50% cases, participation, presentations
   B. Business Cases
      1. Read case, identify key info
      2. Apply theory/tools to situation
   C. Illustrations
      1. Shorter, search info yourself
      2. More informal, can be voluntary

XII. Business Cases Format
   A. Process
      1. Read in advance (few pages)
      2. Form small groups (3-4 people)
      3. ~40 min to prepare
      4. Present, discuss
   B. Expectations
      1. Be specific to business/situation
      2. Use course frameworks
      3. All members ready to participate

XIII. No Single Optimal Solution
   A. Important Idea
      1. No one "correct" answer in strategy
   B. Why
      1. Social science, not pure math
      2. Involves policy, negotiation, power
      3. Non-rational factors matter
   C. Key Point
      1. Different good answers possible
      2. Context and priorities vary

XIV. Real-World Constraints
   A. Decisions Filtered By
      1. Gatekeepers in organization
      2. Unions (can block staff cuts)
      3. State-owned companies (can't fire easily)
      4. Family-owned firms (family matters most)
   B. Key Insight
      1. Best strategy on paper often not chosen
      2. Power, culture, politics influence decisions

XV. Four Elements of Successful Stories
   A. Framework for Cases
      1. Clear objectives
      2. Self-knowledge + fit w/ environment
      3. External analysis (opportunities/threats)
      4. Actions aligned w/ goals/context

---

## Key Takeaways

1. Strategy = Greek "strategos"; competing w/ long-term impact
2. Strategic vs. tactical = long-term vs. short-term
3. Complexity → simplify (2-3 dimensions)
4. Forecasting limited → account for uncertainty
5. Know yourself = first success step
6. Know environment = opportunities + threats
7. No single optimum; context/politics matter
8. Social science = negotiation, power, non-rational factors
9. Stakeholders matter for long-term success
10. Course = 50% theory, 50% practice

X. 30-Second Cold Call Version.
A. 3-Line Speaking Frame.
   1. TOPIC: STATE THE CORE QUESTION IN ONE LINE.
   2. LOGIC: GIVE 2 DRIVERS/FACTORS THAT EXPLAIN THE ANSWER.
   3. ACTION: END WITH ONE DECISION OR IMPLICATION.
B. Delivery Rule.
   1. 25-30 SECONDS MAX.
   2. NO BACKGROUND STORY; GO STRAIGHT TO CLAIM + EVIDENCE + CLOSE.

CI. Oral Answer Template (60-90 Seconds, Class Use).
A. Opening.
   1. STATE THE CORE QUESTION OR TOPIC IN ONE LINE.
   2. STATE YOUR MAIN CLAIM/THESIS IN ONE LINE.
B. Logic.
   1. GIVE 2-3 KEY FACTORS OR DRIVERS.
   2. LINK EACH FACTOR TO THE CLAIM (CAUSE -> EFFECT).
C. Action Or Implication.
   1. END WITH ONE DECISION, RECOMMENDATION, OR CONSEQUENCE.
   2. CLOSE CLEARLY WITHOUT ADDING NEW IDEAS.
D. Delivery Rule.
   1. 60-90 SECONDS.
   2. CLAIM -> EVIDENCE -> CLOSE.
