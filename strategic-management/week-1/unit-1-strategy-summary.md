# Section 1: Summary (super simple)

*Use this when you want the “explain it like I’m 14” version.*

## Key Words (14-year-old version)
- **Strategy** = long-term plan to win: big goals + actions + where we put money and effort.
- **Strategic decisions** = very big choices that are risky and hard to undo.
- **Corporate strategy** = which businesses we are in.
- **Competitive strategy** = how we fight rivals in one business.
- **Functional strategy** = what each department does (HR, marketing, finance, etc.) to support the plan.

## Main Ideas (Simple Version)

1. **Strategy is about sustained success, not luck.**
   - Strategic decisions: uncertainty, complexity, expensive mistakes.
   - Chandler: “determination of long-run goals and adoption of courses of action and allocation of resources.”

2. **Successful strategy (Grant): four elements.**
   - Simple, consistent, long-term goals.
   - Understanding of competitive environment.
   - Appraisal of internal resources.
   - Effective implementation.

3. **Three levels of strategy.**
   - **Corporate:** Scope, synergies, diversification, M&A, internationalization.
   - **Competitive:** How to compete in a business; cost leadership, differentiation; SBUs.
   - **Functional:** Operations, HR, marketing, finance, R&D — support corporate and competitive.

4. **Strategic management process: rational + emergent.**
   - **Rational:** Analysis → Formulation → Implementation → Control. SWOT, mission, vision, objectives.
   - **Reality:** Limited rationality, personal features, uncertainty, complexity, political process, luck.

5. **SM as field: 1960s; ABC model (Academics, Business, Consultants).**
   - Pioneers: Chandler, Andrews, Ansoff.

# Section 2: Full Notes (from Unit 1 Slides)

## 1.1. Strategy Concept

### A. Origins
- Military: “stratós” (army) + “ēgós” (leadership) → the General.
- Von Neumann & Morgenstern (1944): first business use.
- 1960s: Harvard, BCG.
- Porter (1980, 1985): key publications.

### B. Definition (Chandler, 1962)
“The determination of the long-run goals and objectives of an Enterprise and the adoption of courses of action and the allocation of resources necessary for carrying these goals.”

### C. What Strategy Implies
- Sustained success (no luck).
- **Uncertainty:** Future effects unknown.
- **Complexity:** Causes, effects, interrelationships, lack of info.
- **Expensive mistakes:** Can threaten survival.

### D. Successful Strategy (Grant, 2015)

| Element | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| Simple, consistent, long-term goals | Clear direction |
| Understanding competitive environment | External analysis |
| Appraisal of internal resources | Internal analysis |
| Effective implementation | Execution |

### E. Levels of Strategy

| Level | Focus | Contents |
|-------|-------|----------|
| **Corporate** | Scope, synergies | Diversification, M&A, Alliances, Internationalization |
| **Competitive** | How to compete |
| **Functional** | Resource use by function | Operations, HR, Marketing, Finance, R&D |

## 1.2. Strategic Management Process

### A. Deliberate-Rational Process (Guerras & Navas, 2020)
1. **Strategic analysis:** External + Internal.
2. **Strategy formulation:** Mission, vision, objectives; SWOT; corporate & competitive strategies.
3. **Strategy implementation:** Organizational support.
4. **Control and review:** Strategic management control.

### B. Emergent Process
- Strategy is not always rational.
- Depends on: limited rationality, personal features (CEO, TMT), uncertainty, complexity, changing context, political process, luck.

### C. Integrative Process
- Strategic management = **Intended + Emergent**.
- Subject to cognitive restrictions, personal features, environmental dynamism.

### D. Responsibilities
- **Board of Directors:** Strategic advice, management control, shareholder defense.
- **CEO:** Overall strategy.
- **TMT:** Functional heads (operations, marketing, finance, etc.).
- **Staff / Consulting:** Data, advice.
