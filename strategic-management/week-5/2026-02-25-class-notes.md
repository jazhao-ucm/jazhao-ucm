I. Art of War - Competition and Self-Knowledge
   A. Comparing w/ Rivals
      1. Strategy requires comparing resources/capabilities
      2. Objective comparison (not wishful thinking)
      3. See where you have strengths, weaknesses, risks
   B. "Know Yourself, Know the Enemy"
      1. Sun Tzu: "If you know enemy and yourself, need not fear 100 battles"
      2. Know yourself = resources, capabilities, culture, limitations
      3. Know enemy = competitor strategies, strengths, weaknesses
      4. Without both = guesswork, higher failure risk
   C. Do Not Underestimate Competition
      1. Aim = analyze soberly, not "celebrate"
      2. Avoid complacency when ahead
      3. Detect vulnerabilities to exploit
      4. See where to defend or withdraw
   D. Leadership, Speed, Implementation
      1. Strategy w/o leadership/execution = nothing
      2. Effective leaders communicate clear goal
      3. Speed matters: "move like lightning"
      4. Execution must align w/ designed strategy

II. Climate Change and Arctic - PESTEL
   A. Climate Change as Economic Phenomenon
      1. Not only environmental problem
      2. Deep economic and strategic consequences
      3. Changes: resources, trade routes, geopolitics
   B. Case Focus: Arctic/Greenland
      1. Accelerated melting of Arctic ice
      2. New sea lanes: Asia-Europe shorter
      3. Resources under seabed more accessible
      4. Competition intensifies for control
   C. PESTEL Applied
      1. Political
         a. Sovereignty disputes (UN Law of Sea)
         b. Arctic states expand claims and military
      2. Economic
         a. Shorter shipping = less time/fuel
         b. New opportunities: shipping, extraction
         c. Threats: Suez Canal traffic countries
      3. Social
         a. Impact on indigenous communities
         b. Traditional livelihoods disrupted
      4. Technological
         a. Ice-breaking ships needed
         b. Navigation and extraction tech
         c. Infrastructure in harsh environments
      5. Environmental
         a. Emissions from extraction/shipping
         b. Feedback loops: more melting → more activity
      6. Legal
         a. International rules define allowed
         b. Uncertainty = risk and opportunity

III. Industry Opportunities and Threats
   A. Shipping Industry
      1. Opportunity: shorter transit via Arctic
      2. Threat: higher risk (ice, accidents), high fixed costs
   B. Countries on Existing Routes
      1. May lose traffic and income
   C. Mining and Rare-Earth
      1. Opportunity: access to critical minerals
      2. Can reduce dependence on current suppliers
      3. Creates new geopolitical tensions
   D. Agriculture and Tourism
      1. Some benefit from milder climates
      2. Others face severe disruption (snow-dependent)
   E. Insurance and Real Estate
      1. Extreme weather → higher payouts
      2. Coastal real estate riskier
      3. Adaptation businesses gain importance

IV. Five Forces and Barriers - Automotive
   A. Natural Barriers
      1. High fixed investments
         a. Factories and machinery
         b. R&D and engineering
         c. Distribution and service networks
      2. Economies of scale
         a. High fixed costs spread over volume
         b. Small entrants have higher unit costs
      3. Regulatory barriers
         a. Safety and environmental standards
         b. Tariffs protect incumbents
   B. Five Forces in Mature Industry (Auto)
      1. Competitive rivalry: high
      2. Threat of new entrants: low (scale, brand, capital)
      3. Threat of substitutes: medium (public transport, sharing)
      4. Buyer power: high (many brands to compare)
      5. Supplier power: mixed (some components commoditized)

V. Clusters and Industrial Districts
   A. Definition
      1. Geographic concentration of related companies
      2. Plus suppliers, service providers, institutions
   B. Auto Cluster Example
      1. Car manufacturers, parts suppliers, engineering
      2. High availability of engineers/technicians
      3. Shared infrastructure and know-how
   C. Benefits
      1. Easier collaboration and knowledge sharing
      2. Denser labor market w/ specialized skills
      3. Stronger ecosystem (banks, consultants, logistics)
   D. Role of Universities
      1. Provide human capital
      2. Research and new technologies
      3. Proximity facilitates joint projects

VI. Science and Technology Parks
   A. Concept
      1. Planned areas near universities
      2. High-tech firms co-located
   B. Example: Parks Around Madrid
      1. Shared facilities and services
      2. Public/private funding for startups
   C. Strategic Role
      1. Attract investment and talent
      2. Create innovation ecosystem

VII. Consumer Behavior and Ethics
   A. Changing Patterns
      1. Social/ethical concerns shift demand
      2. Companies ignoring shifts risk losing share
      3. Anticipate or drive changes = new markets
   B. Values and Soft Factors
      1. Not only capital and technology
      2. Motivation, commitment, culture, ethics
      3. Willingness to act matters more than numbers

VIII. Overall Takeaways
   A. Strategy as Interpretation
      1. Frameworks = tools; value from application
      2. Good analysis connects:
         a. Clear goals
         b. Self-knowledge (internal)
         c. External analysis (competition, environment)
         d. Consistent implementation
   B. Right Questions
      1. Who are we?
      2. Where are we?
      3. Who is competition?
      4. What resources/capabilities do we have?

---

## Key Takeaways

1. "Know yourself and competitors" = objective understanding both sides
2. Use structured frameworks (PESTEL, Five Forces) for environment
3. Location and ecosystem matter (clusters, districts, tech parks)
4. Values, behavior, execution often decisive
5. Climate change = economic phenomenon w/ industry-specific impacts
6. Barriers to entry: natural (scale, capital) and regulatory
7. Universities and research centers = key cluster ingredients
