**Focus:** Resource-based view, personal attributes as strategic resources, Madonna/Lola Flores case.

I. Resource-Based View and VRIN
   A. External vs. Internal Perspectives
      1. Earlier: PEST/PESTEL, Five Forces = outside firm
      2. RBV: starts from what firm has inside
      3. How internal resources create advantage
   B. VRIN Framework
      1. Valuable → exploits opportunities, neutralizes threats
      2. Rare → few/no competitors have it
      3. Inimitable → difficult/costly to copy
      4. Non-substitutable → cannot be replaced
      5. Only VRIN resources sustain advantage
   C. Types of Resources
      1. Tangible: physical assets, money, machines
      2. Intangible: brand, reputation, culture, routines
      3. Human capital: knowledge, skills, personal attributes

II. Madonna and Lola Flores - VRIN Applied
   A. Why These Examples
      1. Both iconic performers
      2. Success not explained by "talent" alone
      3. Unique combinations become strategic assets
   B. Valuable
      1. Attract large audiences, generate revenue
      2. Shape culture, open new markets
   C. Rare
      1. Many singers, few w/ their recognition
      2. Mixture of voice, performance, story, timing
   D. Inimitable
      1. Can copy clothes/choreography
      2. Cannot copy life stories, personalities
      3. Continuous reinvention hard to fake
   E. Non-Substitutable
      1. Cannot replace w/ another artist for same effect
      2. Brands tied to specific identities/histories
   F. Lessons for Strategy
      1. Unique human capital = most important resource
      2. In many industries, people ARE the product
      3. Strategy must consider who key people are

III. Selection, Attitude, Handling Pressure
   A. Beyond CVs and Degrees
      1. Not only diplomas/technical skills
      2. For high-pressure roles, also ask:
         a. Can they handle stress?
         b. Can they decide w/ incomplete info?
         c. Can they control emotions?
   B. Attitude as Resource
      1. Aptitude = what you CAN do (skills)
      2. Attitude = how you BEHAVE under pressure
      3. For top jobs, attitude more decisive
   C. Selection Decisions
      1. Managers prefer internal candidates they trust
      2. People who've shown they can cope
      3. External hires = fresh ideas but risky

IV. Motivation, Money, Fairness
   A. Money and Recognition
      1. High-responsibility roles bring:
         a. Higher salary
         b. Status symbols (car, office)
   B. Stress and Burnout
      1. Long hours, constant pressure
      2. Responsibility for many employees
      3. Safety risks in some contexts
      4. Can feel like "killing you"
   C. Fairness and Team Contribution
      1. Manager gets large bonus based on team
      2. Team gets little/nothing
      3. Questions: who created value?

V. Careers, Turnover, Negotiation
   A. High Turnover Among Young Workers
      1. Many change companies every few years
      2. Firms may offer only minimum at start
   B. Negotiating Your Value
      1. Understand market value
      2. Negotiate salary and benefits
      3. Ask for fair compensation
   C. Being "Extremely Good at Something"
      1. Strong bargaining power if very good at one thing
      2. Harder to replace
      3. Negotiate from strength

---

## Key Takeaways

1. RBV: strategy must consider internal resources, not only external
2. VRIN explains why certain people (not just products) sustain advantage
3. Selection for top roles: attitude and pressure-handling, not just CVs
4. High-level positions = money + power + stress + risk
5. Career planning: develop unique strengths, understand value, decide trade-offs

X. 30-Second Cold Call Version.
A. 3-Line Speaking Frame.
   1. TOPIC: STATE THE CORE QUESTION IN ONE LINE.
   2. LOGIC: GIVE 2 DRIVERS/FACTORS THAT EXPLAIN THE ANSWER.
   3. ACTION: END WITH ONE DECISION OR IMPLICATION.
B. Delivery Rule.
   1. 25-30 SECONDS MAX.
   2. NO BACKGROUND STORY; GO STRAIGHT TO CLAIM + EVIDENCE + CLOSE.

DI. Oral Answer Template (60-90 Seconds, Class Use).
A. Opening.
   1. STATE THE CORE QUESTION OR TOPIC IN ONE LINE.
   2. STATE YOUR MAIN CLAIM/THESIS IN ONE LINE.
B. Logic.
   1. GIVE 2-3 KEY FACTORS OR DRIVERS.
   2. LINK EACH FACTOR TO THE CLAIM (CAUSE -> EFFECT).
C. Action Or Implication.
   1. END WITH ONE DECISION, RECOMMENDATION, OR CONSEQUENCE.
   2. CLOSE CLEARLY WITHOUT ADDING NEW IDEAS.
D. Delivery Rule.
   1. 60-90 SECONDS.
   2. CLAIM -> EVIDENCE -> CLOSE.
