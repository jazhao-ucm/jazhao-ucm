# Example: Global Management – Key Concepts & Frameworks

### Key Points
- Globalization: Free movement of goods/services/capital; interconnected economies; China, India, Indonesia as major winners
- Slowbalization: Slowing globalization, rise of protectionism, more localization
- Glocalization: Adapting global products/services for local markets (“Think global, act local”)
- Economic Complexity: Transforming raw materials into complex products using tech, innovation, R&D
- G20: 20 countries, ~50% of global economic activity
- Demographics: Africa’s population rising, Europe aging, Asia peaking then declining
- Value Chain: Raw materials → Tech/Innovation → Complex products
- Supply Chain: Suppliers → Procurement → Manufacturing → Distribution → Consumers
- Hofstede’s Dimensions: Power Distance, Individualism, Masculinity, Uncertainty Avoidance, Long-Term Orientation, Indulgence

### Diagrams/Sketches
- Value Chain: Raw Materials → Transform → Manufacturing → Sell → Customers
- Supply Chain: Suppliers → Procurement → Manufacturing → Distribution → Consumers
