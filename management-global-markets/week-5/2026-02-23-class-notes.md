# Management in Global Markets - Class Notes
## February 23, 2026 - Week 5

**Focus:** Strategy levels, internationalization decisions, resources & capabilities, competitive vs absolute advantage, motivation in international assignments.

---

I. Internationalization Strategy - Long-Term and Simple
   A. Keep Strategy Simple
      1. Complex strategies harder to execute
      2. Long-term consistency essential
      3. Strategy for next 15 years, not 15 days
      4. "Slow is fast" - go step by step
   B. Internationalization as Long-Term Process
      1. Requires years of preparation and research
      2. Need internal resources (money, people, capabilities)
      3. External analysis (market, politics, economy)
      4. Internal analysis (resources, capabilities)

II. SMEs and Internationalization
   A. Small and Medium-Sized Enterprises (SMEs)
      1. Definition: fewer than 250 employees
      2. Less than €50 million annual income
      3. In Spain, ~99% of companies are SMEs
      4. Only small fraction involved in internationalization
   B. Constraints for SMEs
      1. Lack of financial resources
      2. Lack of capabilities and experience
      3. Internationalization not automatic
      4. Many SMEs will never go abroad

III. Location Choice - Key to Success
   A. Importance of Location
      1. Effective internationalization depends on location choice
      2. "Location choice" = where physically operate company
      3. Wrong location can ruin even good company
   B. Factors to Consider
      1. Political and economic environment
      2. Market demand and competition
      3. Legal and tax conditions
      4. Access to customers and suppliers
   C. Key Point
      1. If environment bad (no money, high political risk)
      2. Internationalization will fail regardless of internal strengths

IV. Levels of Strategy
   A. Three Main Levels
      1. Corporate Strategy - which businesses or activities?
         a. Portfolio of businesses (Business 1, Business 2, etc.)
         b. Decide which divisions to keep, grow, or exit
      2. Competitive (Business) Strategy - how to compete in each business?
         a. Positioning vs competitors
         b. Differentiation, cost, niche, etc.
      3. Functional Strategy - how to organize and manage functions?
         a. HR, marketing, finance, operations, R&D
         b. "Who does what?" inside each division
   B. Divisional Structure
      1. Each division (e.g., Cars vs. Trucks) has own manager
      2. Each has own functional departments
      3. Performance differences may reflect market conditions

V. Resources, Capabilities, and Competitive Advantage
   A. Resources and Capabilities
      1. Resources = assets firm controls (tangible, intangible, human)
      2. Capabilities = combinations of resources + know-how
      3. Value comes when competitors can't have or imitate
   B. SWOT and Resource-Based View
      1. SWOT: Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats
         a. Opportunities/threats = environment
         b. Strengths/weaknesses = internal
      2. Resource-based view: focus on internal characteristics
      3. Sources of sustainable competitive advantage
   C. Key Point
      1. Before internationalizing, ask: "Do we have strong competitive advantage?"
      2. If not, may be better to stay in home market

VI. Competitive Advantage vs. Absolute Advantage
   A. Competitive Advantage
      1. Firm offers something better/different than competitors
      2. Could be better quality, lower cost, better service, unique features
   B. Absolute Advantage
      1. Very rare and very strong
      2. Firm has capabilities or products almost no competitors have
      3. Example: highly specialized technology or know-how
   C. Key Point
      1. For internationalization, strong competitive advantage necessary
      2. Sometimes absolute advantage helps even more
      3. If "just like" existing players in mature market, very risky

VII. Alliances, Joint Ventures, and Acquisitions
   A. Ways to Enter Foreign Markets
      1. Acquisition: buy local company (requires lot of money)
      2. Joint venture: share ownership and control with local partner
      3. Strategic alliances: cooperation agreements (licensing, distribution)
   B. Why Alliances Matter
      1. Partner brings local knowledge, contacts, legitimacy
      2. Can combine strengths (resources, technology, markets)

VIII. Risk of Technology Transfer - Chile Case
   A. Example (French Company in Chile)
      1. Company moved capital, people, technology from France to Chile
      2. Shared advanced technology with local partners and clients
   B. What Went Wrong
      1. Project failed - lost money, jobs, technology
      2. Local partners later used technology independently
      3. No legal or practical protection
      4. Technology "stolen" in practice
   C. Lessons
      1. Be careful when transferring core technology abroad
      2. Consider IP protection, contracts, partner selection
      3. Culture and trust differences increase risk

IX. Motivation and International Assignments
   A. Selecting People for International Projects
      1. Need more than technical skills or grades
      2. Motivation and resilience critical
      3. Company only sends people ready to accept sacrifice and uncertainty
   B. Personal Learning
      1. International work teaches about business, management, culture
      2. Most importantly, learn about yourself
         a. What you want
         b. What you can accept or not accept
   C. Vision vs. Reality
      1. Many say they have "vision" to live and work abroad
      2. Fewer ready to leave family, comfort, language, stability
      3. Ask honestly: am I prepared to pay the price?

X. Key Takeaways
   A. Internationalization is long-term, complex process
   B. Strategy must be simple and consistent over many years
   C. SMEs dominate in Spain, but few have resources to internationalize
   D. Location choice is critical - wrong environment ruins project
   E. Strategy operates at three levels: corporate, competitive, functional
   F. Strong competitive advantage necessary before going abroad
   G. Alliances, joint ventures, acquisitions are common entry methods
   H. Technology transfer can be risky - core know-how may be lost
   I. Motivation and resilience as important as technical skills

XI. 30-Second Cold Call Version.
A. 3-Line Speaking Frame.
   1. TOPIC: STATE THE CORE QUESTION IN ONE LINE.
   2. LOGIC: GIVE 2 DRIVERS/FACTORS THAT EXPLAIN THE ANSWER.
   3. ACTION: END WITH ONE DECISION OR IMPLICATION.
B. Delivery Rule.
   1. 25-30 SECONDS MAX.
   2. NO BACKGROUND STORY; GO STRAIGHT TO CLAIM + EVIDENCE + CLOSE.

DI. Oral Answer Template (60-90 Seconds, Class Use).
A. Opening.
   1. STATE THE CORE QUESTION OR TOPIC IN ONE LINE.
   2. STATE YOUR MAIN CLAIM/THESIS IN ONE LINE.
B. Logic.
   1. GIVE 2-3 KEY FACTORS OR DRIVERS.
   2. LINK EACH FACTOR TO THE CLAIM (CAUSE -> EFFECT).
C. Action Or Implication.
   1. END WITH ONE DECISION, RECOMMENDATION, OR CONSEQUENCE.
   2. CLOSE CLEARLY WITHOUT ADDING NEW IDEAS.
D. Delivery Rule.
   1. 60-90 SECONDS.
   2. CLAIM -> EVIDENCE -> CLOSE.
