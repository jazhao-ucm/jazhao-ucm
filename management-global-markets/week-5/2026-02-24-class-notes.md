# Management in Global Markets - Class Notes
## February 24, 2026 - Week 5

**Focus:** Three scopes of activities, product scope (specialization vs. diversification), geographic internationalization, export vs. internationalization, reasons for going abroad.

---

I. Three Scopes of Activities
   A. Product Scope
      1. What you produce
      2. Specialization vs. diversification
   B. Geographic Scope
      1. Where you operate
      2. Domestic → international → global
   C. Activity Scope
      1. Which departments/activities
      2. Marketing, Finance, HR, Operations
   D. Departments
      1. Marketing - find customers, introduce improvements
      2. Finance - money, taxes, investment
      3. HR - human resources, qualified personnel
      4. All must integrate - lose info and money if not

II. Product Scope - Specialization vs. Diversification
   A. Specialization
      1. One single product
      2. Low variety
      3. High volume
      4. Same competencies, technology, production
      5. Cannot produce very different products with same capabilities
   B. Diversification
      1. Different products
      2. Different packaging
      3. Different markets
      4. Example: Fanta - orange in one country, different color/flavor in another
      5. Depends on company strategy
   C. Single Business vs. Dominant Business
      1. Single business: one product only
      2. Dominant business: one product sells best (e.g., 50%+ revenue)
         a. Others are secondary
         b. Focus on dominant one
      3. If single not selling, may add products → dominant business

III. Geographic Scope - Internationalization
   A. Why Leave Domestic Market?
      1. Domestic market collapsing
      2. Too many competitors
      3. Price pressure
      4. Can't survive
      5. Opportunities outside
      6. Need to grow
   B. The Graph: Local → Middle → International
      1. Most companies stay in middle (domestic/regional)
      2. Few move outside
      3. Requires money, competencies, capabilities
      4. More kilometers = more complexity
      5. Different regulations, features

IV. Export vs. Internationalization
   A. Export
      1. Sell product to another country
      2. Product moves, company stays
      3. Lower risk, fewer barriers
      4. No physical relocation
      5. Direct export: company sells directly
      6. Indirect export: through intermediaries
   B. Internationalization
      1. Physically move departments, production, facilities
      2. Marketing in another country
      3. Production in another country
      4. Much more complex
         a. Transport
         b. Facilities
         c. People
         d. Communication
         e. Language
      5. Higher risk, higher cost
   C. Key Distinction
      1. "When you move physically, when you detach one department"
      2. "Marketing, production - outside"
      3. "Then it is internationalization"
      4. Export = product crosses border
      5. Internationalization = company crosses border

V. Reasons for Internationalization
   A. Push Factors (Domestic)
      1. Market collapsing
      2. Too many competitors
      3. Not enough customers
      4. Need to grow
   B. Pull Factors (Abroad)
      1. Attractive market - customers, growth, opportunity
      2. Lower costs - taxes, labor
         a. France, Belgium high
         b. Relocate to lower-tax countries
      3. Natural resources - raw materials where they are
         a. Ukraine, Russia for certain resources
      4. Qualified personnel - skilled workers, R&D, technology
      5. Reputation/position - "international" looks better than "local"
      6. Economic integration - invest, create jobs, participate
   C. Relocation of Production
      1. Why move production site to another country?
      2. Market - closer to customers
      3. Cost - lower taxes, lower labor
      4. Scale - bigger space, produce more (e.g., China)
      5. Resources - access to raw materials

VI. Risk and Integration
   A. Risk Levels
      1. Export: lower risk, fewer barriers
      2. Internationalization: higher risk, more costs, more complexity
      3. Regulations, currency issues
   B. Technology and Information
      1. Must integrate departments
      2. Marketing, Finance, HR, Technology
      3. Information flows, technology belongs to company
      4. If don't integrate when going international:
         a. Lose money
         b. Lose information
         c. Lose control
   C. Finance Risk
      1. Moving money between countries
      2. Repatriation of investment
      3. Government can block
      4. Regulations, currency controls
      5. Chile case: company lost money, could not repatriate

VII. Levels of Internationalization
   A. Multi-Domestic
      1. Different production sites in different countries
      2. Each country has own operations
      3. Adapted to local market
   B. Global
      1. Integrated across countries
      2. Centralized, standardized
      3. Different logistics, departments in different places
      4. More complex, more coordinated

VIII. Key Takeaways
   A. Three scopes: product, geographic, activity - all matter
   B. Specialization = one product; diversification = many
   C. Single vs. dominant business
   D. Export ≠ Internationalization
      1. Export = product moves
      2. Internationalization = company moves
   E. Reasons: market, cost, resources, personnel, reputation
   F. Integration between departments critical
   G. Finance risk: repatriation, government, regulations

X. 30-Second Cold Call Version.
A. 3-Line Speaking Frame.
   1. TOPIC: STATE THE CORE QUESTION IN ONE LINE.
   2. LOGIC: GIVE 2 DRIVERS/FACTORS THAT EXPLAIN THE ANSWER.
   3. ACTION: END WITH ONE DECISION OR IMPLICATION.
B. Delivery Rule.
   1. 25-30 SECONDS MAX.
   2. NO BACKGROUND STORY; GO STRAIGHT TO CLAIM + EVIDENCE + CLOSE.

DI. Oral Answer Template (60-90 Seconds, Class Use).
A. Opening.
   1. STATE THE CORE QUESTION OR TOPIC IN ONE LINE.
   2. STATE YOUR MAIN CLAIM/THESIS IN ONE LINE.
B. Logic.
   1. GIVE 2-3 KEY FACTORS OR DRIVERS.
   2. LINK EACH FACTOR TO THE CLAIM (CAUSE -> EFFECT).
C. Action Or Implication.
   1. END WITH ONE DECISION, RECOMMENDATION, OR CONSEQUENCE.
   2. CLOSE CLEARLY WITHOUT ADDING NEW IDEAS.
D. Delivery Rule.
   1. 60-90 SECONDS.
   2. CLAIM -> EVIDENCE -> CLOSE.
