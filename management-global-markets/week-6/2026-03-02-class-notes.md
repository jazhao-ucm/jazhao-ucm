# Management in Global Markets - Class Notes
## March 2, 2026 - Week 6

**Focus:** From export to internationalization and introduction to international HRM.

---

I. From Export to Internationalization
   A. Export (Review)
      1. Company produces in home country
      2. Products shipped abroad
      3. Lower risk, limited commitment
   B. Internationalization
      1. Beyond export:
         a. Set up production plants abroad
         b. Open marketing and sales subsidiaries
         c. Move parts of HQ functions to other countries
      2. Requires:
         a. Capital (FDI)
         b. Strategic decisions about which markets matter
         c. People ready to implement and run foreign operations
   C. FDI (Foreign Direct Investment)
      1. "Next step" after simple trade
      2. Long-term, heavy commitment in host country
      3. Creates need for international HRM

II. International Strategies and "Thinking Global"
   A. Domestic vs. International vs. Transnational
      1. Domestic
         a. All activities inside one country
         b. Only local customers and regulations
      2. International / Multinational
         a. Activities in several countries
         b. Often few key locations rather than "everywhere"
      3. Transnational / Global
         a. Think global, act local
         b. Use global brands and systems
         c. Adapt marketing mix and service to each market
   B. Global Brands and Local Adaptation
      1. Some products truly global (same almost everywhere)
      2. Others require strong local adaptation:
         a. Cosmetics, food, fashion
         b. Same brand, different flavors, packaging, price
      3. Prices can vary across countries:
         a. Same product cheaper or more expensive
         b. Depends on local conditions
   C. Strategic Implication
      1. Strategy determines:
         a. How many foreign locations
         b. How different they are
      2. This determines:
         a. How complex international people management must be

III. Competencies, Capabilities and International HRM
   A. Need for Competencies and Capabilities
      1. Competencies = skills of individuals
      2. Capabilities = systems and routines of firm
      3. To move corporate activities abroad need both:
         a. People who can perform tasks
         b. Organization that supports them
   B. From HRM to International HRM
      1. Domestic HRM
         a. Focus on local recruitment, training, evaluation
         b. One legal system and one culture
      2. International HRM
         a. Must deal with multiple countries and cultures
         b. Includes cross-border mobility (expats, short assignments)
         c. Must coordinate HR policies across locations
   C. First Questions Before Expanding
      1. Which functions will we move abroad?
      2. How many people do we need?
      3. Do we have them inside company, or must hire outside?
      4. How will we support them and their families?

IV. Talent: Who Goes Abroad?
   A. Profile of International Talent
      1. Typically younger workers:
         a. More flexible
         b. Fewer family constraints
      2. Comfortable with:
         a. Technology and innovation
         b. Working in English and/or other languages
         c. Living in different cultures
   B. Internal vs. External Candidates
      1. Internal candidates
         a. Known performance; higher trust
         b. Already socialized into company culture
         c. Often preferred for sensitive positions
      2. External candidates
         a. Bring fresh ideas and skills
         b. Riskier to give immediate control over large assets
   C. Availability and Willingness
      1. Talent not only about skills
      2. Also about being available to move
      3. At some stage may not want to move even for more money
      4. Then no longer right profile for certain roles

V. Motivation, Ambition and Quality of Life
   A. What Motivates People?
      1. Salary and financial security
      2. Work conditions (hours, safety, work-life balance)
      3. Recognition and growth
      4. Personal ambition and desire for responsibility
   B. Ambition and the "Price to Pay"
      1. Not everyone willing to pay same price for success
      2. International roles can mean:
         a. Long hours
         b. High stress and responsibility
         c. Long time away from home and family
      3. Students encouraged to think about:
         a. How far they want to go
         b. What they are willing to sacrifice
   C. Negotiation and Turnover
      1. Young employees today often change jobs quickly
      2. Companies expect high turnover, may offer minimum
      3. Advice:
         a. Do not be afraid to negotiate salary and benefits
         b. Know your market value
         c. Compensation should reflect contribution and cost of living

VI. Teams, Trust and Working with Others
   A. Need for Teams
      1. No one manages international operations alone
      2. Baseball analogy: star player still depends on rest of team
      3. Success is collective, even if recognition individual
   B. Trust and Internal Promotion
      1. Firms prefer to send abroad:
         a. People they already know and trust
         b. People with history inside firm
      2. Reason:
         a. International roles control large budgets
         b. Giving "keys to the kingdom" to outsiders is risky
   C. Learning to Work with Diversity
      1. Group exercise: work with classmates from different countries
      2. Diversity is enriching but demanding
      3. Must develop tolerance, patience, communication skills

VII. Key Takeaways
   A. Export is only first step
   B. True internationalization means moving activities and decisions abroad
   C. Strategic choices (global, multi-domestic, transnational) determine complexity
   D. International HRM links FDI and strategy to concrete questions
   E. International talent combines skills, mobility, and motivation
   F. Motivation goes beyond salary: ambition, recognition, quality of life

X. 30-Second Cold Call Version.
A. 3-Line Speaking Frame.
   1. TOPIC: STATE THE CORE QUESTION IN ONE LINE.
   2. LOGIC: GIVE 2 DRIVERS/FACTORS THAT EXPLAIN THE ANSWER.
   3. ACTION: END WITH ONE DECISION OR IMPLICATION.
B. Delivery Rule.
   1. 25-30 SECONDS MAX.
   2. NO BACKGROUND STORY; GO STRAIGHT TO CLAIM + EVIDENCE + CLOSE.

DI. Oral Answer Template (60-90 Seconds, Class Use).
A. Opening.
   1. STATE THE CORE QUESTION OR TOPIC IN ONE LINE.
   2. STATE YOUR MAIN CLAIM/THESIS IN ONE LINE.
B. Logic.
   1. GIVE 2-3 KEY FACTORS OR DRIVERS.
   2. LINK EACH FACTOR TO THE CLAIM (CAUSE -> EFFECT).
C. Action Or Implication.
   1. END WITH ONE DECISION, RECOMMENDATION, OR CONSEQUENCE.
   2. CLOSE CLEARLY WITHOUT ADDING NEW IDEAS.
D. Delivery Rule.
   1. 60-90 SECONDS.
   2. CLAIM -> EVIDENCE -> CLOSE.
