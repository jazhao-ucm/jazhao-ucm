## Management in Global Markets – Midterm Cheat Sheet

**How to use:** Use for quick recall. In exam: define terms, give one example, tie to internationalization success/failure.

---

### Unit 1 – Globalization vs. Slowbalization

- **Globalization** = Free movement of goods, services, capital. High production + export.
- **Slowbalization** = Globalization slowing. Countries more protectionist. Localization.
- **Winners**: China, India, Indonesia (production, exports). **USA**: biggest importer.
- **Economic complexity** = Turning raw materials into complex products (tech, innovation, R&D). USA, UK, Netherlands strong.
- **Demographics**: Europe ageing (60% over 60 by 2032). Africa growing. Young workers = production.
- **Protectionism**: Tariffs. Countries protect own industries.

---

### Unit 2 – Supply Chain & Products by Country

- **Supply chain** = How parts move before product is made. Global = many countries.
- **Quality vs. cost**: Japan = high quality, higher price. China = lower cost, volume.
- **Products from many countries**: Shoes, shirt, watch—each part from different country.
- **Risks**: Culture, laws, language. **Competition**: Go where competitors are. They show market works.
- **Culture shock**: Excitement → hard → adapt over time.

---

### Unit 3 – Strategy Levels

- **Corporate strategy** = Which businesses? (Cars vs. trucks). Portfolio.
- **Competitive strategy** = How to compete? (Price, quality, differentiation, niche).
- **Functional strategy** = HR, marketing, finance, operations, R&D. Who does what?
- **Keep strategy simple. Long-term.** Strategy for 15 years, not 15 days. "Slow is fast."

---

### Unit 4 – Internationalization

- **SME** = Small/medium. Under 250 employees, <€50M revenue. Spain: ~99% are SMEs.
- **Location choice** = Critical. Wrong location can ruin good company. Politics, economy, market, legal.
- **Resources** = What you have (tangible, intangible, human). **Capabilities** = What you do with them.
- **Competitive advantage** = Better/different than competitors. **Absolute advantage** = Almost no one has it. Rare.
- **Before internationalizing**: Do we have strong competitive advantage? If not, stay home.
- **Entry modes**: Acquisition, joint venture, strategic alliances (licensing, distribution).

---

### Unit 5 – Chile Case & Technology Transfer

- **Chile case**: French company moved capital, people, tech to Chile. Failed. Lost money, jobs, technology.
- **What went wrong**: Local partners used technology independently. No protection. Communication broke down.
- **Lesson**: Be careful transferring core technology. IP protection, contracts, partner selection. Culture and trust matter.

---

### Unit 6 – Export vs. Internationalization & HRM

- **Export** = Sell abroad. Product moves. Company stays home. Lower risk.
- **Internationalization** = Build operations abroad. Plants, subsidiaries, HQ roles. FDI.
- **FDI** = Foreign Direct Investment. Long-term commitment. Creates need for international HRM.
- **International HRM** = Attract, select, train, move talent. Trust, internal promotion for sensitive roles.
- **Motivation**: Salary + ambition, recognition, quality of life. As important as technical skills.
- **Turnover**: Young workers often leave. Negotiate conditions.
- **Who goes abroad?** Motivated, resilient. Ready for sacrifice and uncertainty.

### Common Question Angles

- **"Why might internationalization fail?"** Wrong location, weak competitive advantage, technology transfer risk, culture, partner issues. Chile case.
- **"Corporate vs. competitive vs. functional strategy?"** Which business / how to compete / who does what.
- **"Export vs. internationalization?"** Export = product moves; internationalization = company moves operations.
- **"What matters for success abroad?"** Strong CA, right location, motivated people, careful tech transfer, good partners.
