II. Internationalization And Competitive Advantage.
   A. Complexity And Location Choice.
      1. COMPLEX LOCATION + COMPLEX PRODUCT = HARD TO SELL.
      2. COMPLEXITY NOT ALWAYS QUALIFIED (CUSTOMERS MAY NOT UNDERSTAND).
      3. NEED TO BE GOOD AT SOMETHING SOMEWHERE (SPECIALIZATION).
   B. Global Competencies.
      1. NOT NECESSARY TO MOVE ENTIRE COMPANY COMPETENCE ABROAD.
      2. OPTION: TRAIN PEOPLE OR HIRE PEOPLE WITH INTERNATIONAL SKILLS.
      3. EXPERIENCE = SKILLS (NOT JUST “INTERNATIONAL EXPERIENCE”).
   C. Skills And Executives.
      1. TECHNICAL SKILLS + EXECUTIVE MANAGEMENT = RARE COMBINATION.
      2. COMPANIES CLAIM “NO PEOPLE” AS FAILURE REASON.

III. HR Development (Training, Rotation, Motivation).
   A. Training Approaches.
      1. CLASSROOM, DISCUSSION, CONFERENCES.
      2. JOB ROTATION (DIVISIONS: FINANCE → PRODUCTION → SALES).
      3. CORPORATE SCHOOLS / UNIVERSITY LINKS.
   B. Job Rotation Value.
      1. BIG CORPORATIONS: HELPS UNDERSTAND OTHER FUNCTIONS.
      2. SMALL COMPANIES: LIMITED FIT.
      3. REDUCES CONFLICT (BETTER EMPATHY FOR OTHER ROLES).
   C. Motivation.
      1. WITHOUT MOTIVATION = TRAINING USELESS.
      2. SUCCESSFUL COMPANIES = MORE SKILLS + MORE COMPLEXITY.

IV. Manager Habits And Professional Behavior.
   A. Asking Questions.
      1. ASKING ≠ WEAKNESS.
      2. ASK TO AVOID MISTAKES (MONEY + RESPONSIBILITY).
      3. YOUNG WORKERS: ASK, LEARN, IMPROVE.
   B. One-Line Answers.
      1. OPEN QUESTIONS: 1 LINE OF CORE POINT.
      2. CLARITY > LENGTH.
