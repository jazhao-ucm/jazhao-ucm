II. Internationalization And Competitive Advantage.
   A. Complexity And Location Choice.
      1. COMPLEX LOCATION + COMPLEX PRODUCT = HARD TO SELL.
      2. COMPLEXITY NOT ALWAYS QUALIFIED (CUSTOMERS MAY NOT UNDERSTAND).
      3. NEED TO BE GOOD AT SOMETHING SOMEWHERE (SPECIALIZATION).
      4. CANNOT MOVE ENTIRE COMPETENCE ABROAD; ONLY OPTION = TRAIN OR HIRE.
   B. Global Competencies.
      1. NOT NECESSARY TO MOVE ENTIRE COMPANY COMPETENCE ABROAD.
      2. OPTION: TRAIN PEOPLE OR HIRE PEOPLE WITH INTERNATIONAL SKILLS.
      3. EXPERIENCE = SKILLS (COMPANIES WANT SKILLS, NOT JUST “INTERNATIONAL EXPERIENCE”).
      4. TRAINING HAS LIMITS: E.G. THREE MONTHS NOT ENOUGH; CANNOT TRAIN 25 YEARS WORTH.
      5. GAP BETWEEN TRAINING AND FINDING TALENT; BOTH NEEDED.
   C. Skills And Executives.
      1. TECHNICAL SKILLS + EXECUTIVE MANAGEMENT = RARE COMBINATION.
      2. COMPANIES CLAIM “NO PEOPLE” AS FAILURE REASON.
   D. Trust And Qualifications.
      1. DO NOT ASSUME DEGREE/MASTER = COMPETENCE; VERIFY.
      2. SOME ROLES REQUIRE DEGREE (E.G. LAWYER); OTHERS DO NOT.
      3. BE CAREFUL: QUALIFICATIONS ON PAPER ≠ GUARANTEED ABILITY.

III. HR Development (Training, Rotation, Motivation).
   A. Training Approaches.
      1. CLASSROOM, ROLE PLAYS, DISCUSSION, CONFERENCES, JOB ROTATION.
      2. JOB ROTATION (DIVISIONS: FINANCE → MARKETING → PRODUCTION → SALES).
      3. CORPORATE SCHOOLS / UNIVERSITY LINKS.
      4. BUSINESS GAMES: ONE GIVES "REAL UNIQUE INSIGHT" (RUN A DIVISION; AFTER 4 to 5 YEARS GET FULL PICTURE).
   B. Job Rotation Value.
      1. BIG CORPORATIONS: HELPS UNDERSTAND OTHER FUNCTIONS, REDUCES CONFLICT.
      2. SMALL COMPANIES: LIMITED FIT.
      3. EMPLOY FOR YOUR AREA (HIRE FOR FIT).
   C. Motivation.
      1. WITHOUT MOTIVATION = TRAINING USELESS; MOTIVATION FIRST.
      2. COMPANIES TRAIN TO ALIGN WITH GOALS (NOT JUST TO MAKE PEOPLE HAPPY).
      3. SUCCESSFUL COMPANIES = BEST ECONOMIC COMPLEXITY INDEX, MORE SKILLS, MORE COMPETENCIES.

IV. Manager Habits And Professional Behavior.
   A. Asking Questions.
      1. ASKING ≠ WEAKNESS; ASK ALWAYS; DO NOT BE AFRAID.
      2. ASK TO AVOID MISTAKES (MONEY + RESPONSIBILITY).
      3. YOUNG WORKERS: ASK; "ASKING IS BEST."
   B. Promotion.
      1. RIGHT PROMOTION = DOES NOT VIOLATE PRINCIPLES.
      2. SOMETIMES WRONG PERSON PROMOTED; ASK WHEN YOU DO NOT KNOW.
   C. One-Line Answers.
      1. OPEN QUESTIONS: ONE LINE OF CORE POINT ENOUGH.
      2. CLARITY > LENGTH.

V. Macro Context (Blocks, Prices, Energy, Power).
   A. Economic Blocks.
      1. EU = 27 COUNTRIES, TRADE TOGETHER.
      2. USA (USMCA: USA, MEXICO, CANADA THREE COUNTRIES).
      3. ASEAN (SOUTH-EAST ASIA); MERCOSUR (SOUTH AMERICA).
      4. G7: INDUSTRIAL DEMOCRACIES (E.G. EU, CANADA, UK, JAPAN).
      5. G20: ~20 COUNTRIES, ~75% WORLD POPULATION (INCLUDES CHINA, INDIA).
      6. AFRICA: AU (E.G. 55 MEMBER STATES).
   B. Prices And Current Events.
      1. GASOLINE: DIFFERENT IN EUROPE VS USA (EU MORE EXPENSIVE; LITRE VS GALLON).
      2. NORTH VS SOUTH EU PRICE DIFFERENCES (TAXES; E.G. FRANCE MORE TAX).
      3. PRODUCER COUNTRIES VS IMPORTERS (PRICE IMPACT).
   C. Dependence And Energy.
      1. EUROPE DEPENDS ON IMPORTS (E.G. ENERGY; RUSSIA).
      2. SHOCKS → COSTS INCREASE; "WE DON'T HAVE" (RESOURCES).
   D. Institutions And Global Power.
      1. UN SECURITY COUNCIL PERMANENT MEMBERS (USA, CHINA, RUSSIA, FRANCE, UK).
      2. "ONLY WAY TO SURVIVE / COMPETE" = CREATE BLOCKS (TRADE + POLITICS).
      3. SMALL COUNTRIES TOGETHER = POWER; ALONE = VULNERABLE.

VI. Economic Integration (Start Of Chapter / Topic).
   A. Community Integration.
      1. EU "GREATEST VICTORY": FREE MOVEMENT OF GOODS, SERVICES, PEOPLE, CAPITAL.
      2. REMOVE BARRIERS, UNIFY RULES; NOT FULL SINGLE ECONOMY (E.G. TAXES DIFFER BY COUNTRY).
   B. Example: Switzerland.
      1. NO SEA, NO OIL, NO GAS; STILL RICH.
      2. ASSOCIATIONS, TRADE, COMPLEX PRODUCTS, HIGHLY COMPLEX ECONOMY.
      3. SURROUNDED BY RICH NEIGHBORS; INVESTMENT AND TRADE.
   C. Dynamic Effects.
      1. COUNTRIES WORKING TOGETHER; AGREE ON POLITICS; NO WAR; TRADE EASIER.
   D. Factor Mobility.
      1. LABOR AND CAPITAL MOBILITY; CONCEPT OF IMMOBILITY (SOME FACTORS NOT FULLY MOBILE).
      2. IN EU, PEOPLE AND CAPITAL CAN MOVE (E.G. WORK ANYWHERE).
   E. Protectionism And Dumping.
      1. PROTECTIONIST POLICIES = AGAINST FOREIGN PRODUCTS; EU DOES NOT DO THAT (COMPETITION).
      2. DUMPING = SELLING BELOW COST TO ATTRACT MARKET; UNFAIR; EU HAS RULES.
   F. Static Effects.
      1. REALLOCATION OF RESOURCES (E.G. BEFORE/AFTER INTEGRATION; SPAIN AND OTHERS FREE TO MOVE).
   G. Levels Of Integration (RANKING, LEAST TO MOST ADVANCED).
      1. PREFERENTIAL AGREEMENT.
      2. FREE TRADE AREA.
      3. CUSTOMS UNION.
      4. COMMON MARKET.
      5. ECONOMIC UNION.
      6. ECONOMIC AND MONETARY UNION (MOST COMPLETE).
      7. ONLY ONE MONETARY UNION IN PRACTICE = EUROZONE (EU); USA DOES NOT SHARE CURRENCY WITH CANADA/MEXICO.
   H. EU Enlargement (Candidates).
      1. UKRAINE (FIRST IN LINE); THEN MOLDOVA.
      2. WHY THEY WANT TO JOIN: SECURITY, ECONOMIC STABILITY, PROSPERITY.
      3. WHY NOT YET: WAR, ECONOMY DESTROYED, RULE OF LAW / CORRUPTION (REFORMS NEEDED), GEOGRAPHY (BETWEEN RUSSIA AND WEST), DIVIDE (E.G. DNIEPER; EAST MORE RUSSIAN-SPEAKING, WEST MORE UKRAINIAN-SPEAKING).
      4. "27 FOR A VERY LONG TIME" (ENLARGEMENT SLOW).

X. 30-Second Cold Call Version.
A. 3-Line Speaking Frame.
   1. TOPIC: STATE THE CORE QUESTION IN ONE LINE.
   2. LOGIC: GIVE 2 DRIVERS/FACTORS THAT EXPLAIN THE ANSWER.
   3. ACTION: END WITH ONE DECISION OR IMPLICATION.
B. Delivery Rule.
   1. 25-30 SECONDS MAX.
   2. NO BACKGROUND STORY; GO STRAIGHT TO CLAIM + EVIDENCE + CLOSE.

DI. Oral Answer Template (60-90 Seconds, Class Use).
A. Opening.
   1. STATE THE CORE QUESTION OR TOPIC IN ONE LINE.
   2. STATE YOUR MAIN CLAIM/THESIS IN ONE LINE.
B. Logic.
   1. GIVE 2-3 KEY FACTORS OR DRIVERS.
   2. LINK EACH FACTOR TO THE CLAIM (CAUSE -> EFFECT).
C. Action Or Implication.
   1. END WITH ONE DECISION, RECOMMENDATION, OR CONSEQUENCE.
   2. CLOSE CLEARLY WITHOUT ADDING NEW IDEAS.
D. Delivery Rule.
   1. 60-90 SECONDS.
   2. CLAIM -> EVIDENCE -> CLOSE.
