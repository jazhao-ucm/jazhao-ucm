   B. Population Growth

V. Tax Systems & Trade Barriers
   A. Protectionism
      1. Countries use taxes to protect industries
      2. "Apply high taxes on what we don't need"
      3. "Apply low taxes on what we need but don't produce"
   B. Purpose of Trade Barriers
      1. Protect domestic industries
      2. Control imports
      3. Encourage local production
      4. Manage competition
   C. Key Point
      1. Tax systems = tools for economic protection
      2. Strategic trade management

VI. Competition Strategy
   A. What is Competition?
      1. Companies doing the same thing
      2. Offering similar products/services
      3. Competing for same customers
   B. How to Beat Competitors
      1. Lower prices - undercut competitors
      2. Better quality - superior products
      3. Better service - customer experience
      4. Innovation - new features or solutions
   C. Key Question for New Markets
      1. Avoid competitors (go where few exist)?
      2. Work with competitors (learn from them)?

VII. Market Entry Strategy - Competition Analysis
   A. Two Options
      1. Option B: Market with many competitors
         a. Lots of competition
         b. Established market
         c. Customers already exist
         d. Infrastructure in place
      2. Option C: Market with very few competitors
         a. Little competition
         b. Less established market
         c. Fewer customers
         d. Less infrastructure
   B. Correct Answer: Go to Option B (Where Competitors Exist)
      1. Competitors = market validation (demand exists)
      2. Infrastructure exists - transportation, suppliers, distribution
      3. You can learn - study what competitors do
      4. Customers exist - market already developed
      5. Resources available - money, technology, FDI
      6. You can differentiate - find what makes you different
   C. Key Insight
      1. "You cannot escape competition"
      2. Better to compete in established market than create new one alone
   D. Example
      1. French company tried to enter Mexico market alone
      2. Avoided competitors
      3. Failed: no market infrastructure, no customers, no guidance

X. Hofstede's Cultural Dimensions (Review & Expansion)
   A. Background
      1. Geert Hofstede - studied IBM employees
      2. Identified 5 different cultural dimensions
      3. Found differences across countries
   B. The Five Dimensions
      1. Power Distance
         a. High = strong hierarchy, boss clearly superior
         b. Low = flat hierarchy, more equal relationships
         c. Examples: Spain (high), Mexico (very high), Northern Europe (low)
      2. Individualism vs. Collectivism
         a. Individualism = "I want what I want" (USA, Australia)
         b. Collectivism = "What's good for the group?" (China, India)
      3. Masculinity vs. Femininity
         a. Differences in gender roles
         b. How men and women relate in business
      4. Uncertainty Avoidance
         a. How comfortable with uncertainty
         b. Some prefer rules/structure, others comfortable with ambiguity
      5. Long-Term vs. Short-Term Orientation (NEW)
         a. Long-Term (High): Focus on future planning
            i. Examples: USA, many Western countries
            ii. Think about job, marriage, house, retirement
         b. Short-Term (Low): Focus on present
            i. Examples: some developing countries
            ii. Focus on surviving today, immediate needs
   C. Key Insight
      1. Long-term orientation possible with security/resources
      2. Short-term orientation from necessity/uncertainty
   D. Gender Equality Examples
      1. Iceland: zero difference (most equal)
      2. Norway, Sweden: very high equality
      3. France: less clear, but improving

X. 30-Second Cold Call Version.
A. 3-Line Speaking Frame.
   1. TOPIC: STATE THE CORE QUESTION IN ONE LINE.
   2. LOGIC: GIVE 2 DRIVERS/FACTORS THAT EXPLAIN THE ANSWER.
   3. ACTION: END WITH ONE DECISION OR IMPLICATION.
B. Delivery Rule.
   1. 25-30 SECONDS MAX.
   2. NO BACKGROUND STORY; GO STRAIGHT TO CLAIM + EVIDENCE + CLOSE.

DI. Oral Answer Template (60-90 Seconds, Class Use).
A. Opening.
   1. STATE THE CORE QUESTION OR TOPIC IN ONE LINE.
   2. STATE YOUR MAIN CLAIM/THESIS IN ONE LINE.
B. Logic.
   1. GIVE 2-3 KEY FACTORS OR DRIVERS.
   2. LINK EACH FACTOR TO THE CLAIM (CAUSE -> EFFECT).
C. Action Or Implication.
   1. END WITH ONE DECISION, RECOMMENDATION, OR CONSEQUENCE.
   2. CLOSE CLEARLY WITHOUT ADDING NEW IDEAS.
D. Delivery Rule.
   1. 60-90 SECONDS.
   2. CLAIM -> EVIDENCE -> CLOSE.
