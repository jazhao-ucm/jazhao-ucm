   - You must adapt over time.

### VI. Competition Strategy

A. What is Competition?
1. Companies doing the same thing
2. Offering similar products/services
3. Competing for the same customers

B. How to Beat Competitors
1. Lower prices - Undercut competitors
2. Better quality - Superior products
3. Better service - Customer experience
4. Innovation - New features or solutions

C. Key question: When entering a new market, do you
1. Avoid competitors (go where there are few)?
2. Work with competitors (learn from them)?

### VII. Market Entry Strategy - Competition Analysis

A. Scenario
B. You're entering a new market. You have two options

Option B: Market with many competitors already doing what you do
1. Lots of competition
2. Established market
3. Customers already exist
4. Infrastructure in place

Option C: Market with very few competitors
5. Little competition
6. Less established market
7. Fewer customers
8. Less infrastructure

Correct Answer: Go to Option B (where competitors exist)

C. Why?
1. Competitors = Market validation - If competitors are there, there's demand
2. Infrastructure exists - Transportation, suppliers, distribution
3. You can learn - Study what competitors do
4. Customers exist - Market is already developed
5. Resources available - Money, technology, FDI (Foreign Direct Investment)
6. You can differentiate - Find what makes you different

D. Key insight
1. "You cannot escape competition" - It's better to compete in an established market than try to create a new one alone.

E. Example from Instructor
1. French company tried to enter Mexico market alone
2. Avoided competitors
3. Failed because: no market infrastructure, no customers, no guidance
4. Lesson: Working with competitors helps you learn and succeed

### X. Hofstede's Cultural Dimensions (Review & Expansion)

Geert Hofstede - Studied IBM employees across different countries
1. Identified 5 different cultural dimensions
2. Traveled with American company
3. Found differences across countries

A. The Five Dimensions

#### 1. Power Distance
1. High Power Distance: Strong hierarchy, boss is clearly superior
2. Low Power Distance: Flat hierarchy, more equal relationships
3. Examples: Spain (high), Mexico (very high), Northern Europe (low), USA (low)

#### 2. Individualism vs. Collectivism
1. Individualism: People in rich countries tend to be more individualistic
2. Collectivism: People in poorer countries tend to be more collective/group-oriented
3. Examples: USA (high individualism), many Asian countries (high collectivism)

#### 3. Masculinity vs. Femininity
1. Differences in gender roles
2. How men and women relate in business
3. Varies by country and culture

Instructor's View: "There is no big difference between men and women. They are the same people. I don't consider differences."

#### 4. Uncertainty Avoidance
1. How comfortable people are with uncertainty
2. Some cultures prefer rules and structure
3. Others are more comfortable with ambiguity

#### 5. Long-Term vs. Short-Term Orientation (NEW)

B. Long-Term Orientation (High)
1. Focus on future planning
2. Examples: USA, many Western countries
3. People think about:
   a. Getting a job
   b. Getting married
   c. Buying a house
   d. Retirement
   e. Long-term security
4. "You think now, you don't know you have a job, but you plan for it"

C. Short-Term Orientation (Low)
1. Focus on present
2. Examples: Some developing countries
3. People think about:
   a. Surviving today
   b. Immediate needs
   c. Current problems
4. "You need help now because you can't plan - you're not rich, you don't know if something will happen"

D. Key insight
1. Long-term orientation is possible when you have security and resources. Short-term orientation comes from necessity and uncertainty.

E. Special Case - Gender Equality
1. Iceland: Zero difference between men and women (most equal in the world)
2. Norway, Sweden: Also very high equality
3. France: Less clear, but improving
4. 100,000 people across 50 countries - Significant sample size
5. Still don't fully understand why some countries remain unequal

### XII. Logistics & Transportation

A. Container Shipping
1. China is the biggest - Huge vessels pulling containers
2. Different containers for different goods:
   a. **Dry containers** - Nothing special inside
   b. **Refrigerated containers** - Control temperature and humidity
   c. **Special containers** - For specific goods
3. Different sizes and colors indicate different types of goods/services

B. Why Different Containers?
1. We don't use the same transportation for all goods
2. Different products need different handling
3. Temperature control for perishables
4. Special handling for fragile/valuable items

C. Key point
1. Logistics is complex - different goods require different transportation methods.
