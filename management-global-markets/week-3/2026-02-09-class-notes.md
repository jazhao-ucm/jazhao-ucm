I. Internationalization - The 4 Phases
   A. Phase 1: Conceive (Planning)
      1. You see the market
      2. You look at opportunities
      3. You analyze potential
      4. Duration: 2-3 years
      5. Key activity: research and planning
   B. Phase 2: Execute (Implementation)
      1. You move, plant, associate
      2. You establish operations
      3. You invest money
      4. Duration: 2 years
      5. Key activity: setting up operations
   C. Phase 3: Establish (Stabilization)
      1. Company is established
      2. Operations are running
      3. You're making money
      4. Key activity: building stability
   D. Phase 4: Expand (Growth)
      1. Company is successful
      2. You expand further
      3. You grow the business
      4. Key activity: scaling operations
   E. Key Insight
      1. "Most important phase is the integration process"
      2. Phase 2 (Execute) is critical
      3. If you leave too early or wrong place = fail

II. The Internationalization Graph
   A. Phase 1-2: Investment Phase
      1. Spending money
      2. Highest investment when you leave (Phase 2)
      3. Moving from home to new country
      4. This is where you spend the most
   B. Phase 2-3: Growth Phase
      1. If you execute correctly, you grow
      2. If you execute wrong, you lose everything
      3. Critical decision point
   C. Phase 3-4: Success Phase
      1. If successful, you reach Phase 4
      2. Company is established
      3. Making money and growing
   D. Key Point
      1. "Take decision to leave or move only if sure"

III. Which Phase is Most Dangerous?
   A. Answer: Phase 3 (Establish)
      1. When conceive (Phase 1), just looking at market
      2. When execute (Phase 2), moving and planting
      3. Phase 3: seeing market, moving plant, associating with govts
      4. If not successful, money goes down
      5. Everything fails after Phase 3 if not done correctly
   B. Key Insight
      1. Phase 3 requires patience and careful execution
      2. Many companies fail here

IV. Timeline for Internationalization
   A. Total Time Needed
      1. Conceive (Phase 1): 2-3 years
      2. Execute (Phase 2): 2 years
      3. Total: 4-5 years minimum
   B. Reality Check
      1. Not a quick process
      2. Requires significant investment
      3. Takes years, not months

V. International Risk - Two Types
   A. Artificial Risk (Man-Made)
      1. Created by humans
      2. Can be controlled or managed
      3. Examples:
         a. Legal systems
         b. Government regulations
         c. Trade barriers
         d. Political systems
         e. Economic policies
   B. Natural Risk (Unavoidable)
      1. Cannot be escaped
      2. Natural barriers
      3. Examples:
         a. Geography
         b. Distance
         c. Communication barriers
         d. Language differences
         e. Cultural differences
   C. Key Insight
      1. "Communication, language is the one you can't escape"
      2. Natural risk = must face and adapt

VI. Location Selection - Most Important Factor
   A. Key Insight
      1. "The exact location you need for your company"
      2. Not about competencies or capabilities
      3. Not about right moment or hard work
      4. It's the exact location
   B. Why Location Matters
      1. Can make or break your business
      2. More important than:
         a. Your competencies
         b. Your capabilities
         c. Timing
         d. How hard you work
   C. Example from Instructor
      1. Company failed not from lack of effort
      2. Failed because of wrong location choice
      3. Location was determining factor
   D. Key Point
      1. "Choosing the right location is the masterpiece"

VII. Internal vs. External Analysis
   A. Internal Analysis (Inside Company)
      1. Strengths: what company does well
      2. Weaknesses: what company lacks
      3. Company resources
      4. Company capabilities
      5. Employee skills
      6. Technology
      7. Financial resources
   B. External Analysis (Outside Company)
      1. Opportunities: market opportunities
      2. Threats: competition, risks
      3. Market conditions
      4. Competitors
      5. Economic factors
      6. Political factors
      7. Cultural factors
   C. Key Point
      1. "Need information about company AND country/market"

VIII. Company Weaknesses - Common Problems
   A. Lack of International Experience
      1. "First one is worst - company having no experience"
      2. No previous international operations
      3. No learning from past experiences
   B. Insufficient Information
      1. "Need a lot of information about environment"
      2. Don't know enough about target market
      3. Don't understand local conditions
   C. Weak Communication Between Departments
      1. Departments don't communicate
      2. Lack of coordination
      3. Communication between departments never existed
   D. No Backup Plans
      1. No contingency planning
      2. No support systems
      3. No training department backup
   E. Being Left Alone
      1. Company sends you but doesn't support you
      2. "You sent me there. You left me alone there."
      3. No ongoing support or communication
   F. Key Insight
      1. "Weakness for companies that are not really prepared"

IX. Multi-Country Strategy
   A. Definition
      1. Successfully enter one country, then expand to others
   B. Example
      1. French company: "Start in Chile, then Colombia, then Mexico"
      2. Success in first country enables expansion
      3. Learning from one market helps in others
   C. Why It Works
      1. Learn from first market entry
      2. Apply lessons to new markets
      3. Build on success
      4. Reduce risk through experience
   D. Key Point
      1. "Positive experience, then you can go to different locations"

X. Information Gathering
   A. About the Market
      1. What are salaries?
      2. What are business conditions?
      3. What are cultural factors?
      4. Market size and potential
      5. Customer preferences
   B. About Your Company
      1. Internal strengths
      2. Internal weaknesses
      3. Resources available
      4. Capabilities
      5. Experience level
   C. Challenge
      1. Limited ability to gather information before entering
      2. "We cannot visit and know everything"

XI. The "Easy" Trap
   A. Warning
      1. "We choose to go easy, easy and fast"
      2. Choosing easy markets instead of right markets
   B. The Problem
      1. "This is not great. You're lazy."
      2. Don't want to understand
      3. Don't want to pay
      4. Don't want to work
   C. Reality
      1. "Nothing better than uncertainty"
      2. Easy doesn't mean right
      3. Need proper research
      4. Need to understand the market

XII. International Operations Complexity
   A. Key Principle
      1. "Managing inside is one thing"
      2. "Managing outside is more complex"
   B. Why More Complex
      1. Multiple factors to consider
      2. Different cultures
      3. Different languages
      4. Different legal systems
      5. Different business practices
      6. Different customer preferences

XIII. Decision Making Process
   A. Steps
      1. Gather information - about market and company
      2. Analyze - strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, threats
      3. Calculate risk - factor of risk impact
      4. Make decision - only if you are sure
      5. Execute - move forward with plan
   B. Timing
      1. Not too fast, not too slow
      2. Need right pace

XIV. Responsibility in International Business
   A. Your Responsibility
      1. Do not waste the capital
      2. Put yourself forward
   B. Reality
      1. Responsible for company money
      2. "They said, you lost the money"
      3. Company created from scratch 25 years
      4. If you lose money, consequences follow
   C. Key Point
      1. Don't waste capital invested

XV. The Failure Story
   A. Instructor's Experience
      1. Company failed in new market
      2. Lost all investment
      3. Instructor was fired
      4. "I spent all the money"
   B. Lessons Learned
      1. Location was wrong
      2. Not enough information
      3. Company weaknesses not addressed
      4. Communication broke down
      5. No backup plans
   C. Key Insight
      1. "I knew it was going to fail"

XVI. Cultural Attraction
   A. Concept
      1. "We feel attraction for different places"
   B. Why It Matters
      1. People attracted to different countries
      2. Always different answers to "where would you live?"
      3. This attraction is normal
   C. Key Point
      1. Different people attracted to different cultures/places
      2. Affects where companies choose to expand

XVII. Confusion in New Markets
   A. Reality
      1. "When you go somewhere, don't expect people like your way"
   B. The Challenge
      1. Things are different
      2. Can be confusing
      3. Different expectations
   C. Key Point
      1. Expect confusion - it's normal
      2. Different ways of doing things

XVIII. Key Takeaways
   A. Internationalization has 4 phases - Conceive, Execute, Establish, Expand
   B. Phase 3 (Establish) most dangerous - many companies fail here
   C. Location is everything - more important than competencies or timing
   D. Takes 4-5 years minimum - not a quick process
   E. Two types of risk - artificial (can manage) and natural (must adapt)
   F. Communication/language = natural risk - cannot escape
   G. Company weaknesses - lack experience, info, communication, backup plans
   H. Internal vs. external analysis - need both to succeed
   I. Multi-country strategy - success in one enables expansion
   J. Don't choose "easy" - choose the right location
   K. International more complex - managing outside harder than inside
   L. You're responsible - don't waste capital
   M. Cultural attraction is normal - people attracted to different places
   N. Expect confusion - things will be different

XIX. Final Thought
   A. "Choosing the right location is the masterpiece"
   B. Remember
      1. Location > Competencies
      2. Location > Timing
      3. Location > Hard Work
      4. Location = Success or Failure

X. 30-Second Cold Call Version.
A. 3-Line Speaking Frame.
   1. TOPIC: STATE THE CORE QUESTION IN ONE LINE.
   2. LOGIC: GIVE 2 DRIVERS/FACTORS THAT EXPLAIN THE ANSWER.
   3. ACTION: END WITH ONE DECISION OR IMPLICATION.
B. Delivery Rule.
   1. 25-30 SECONDS MAX.
   2. NO BACKGROUND STORY; GO STRAIGHT TO CLAIM + EVIDENCE + CLOSE.

MI. Oral Answer Template (60-90 Seconds, Class Use).
A. Opening.
   1. STATE THE CORE QUESTION OR TOPIC IN ONE LINE.
   2. STATE YOUR MAIN CLAIM/THESIS IN ONE LINE.
B. Logic.
   1. GIVE 2-3 KEY FACTORS OR DRIVERS.
   2. LINK EACH FACTOR TO THE CLAIM (CAUSE -> EFFECT).
C. Action Or Implication.
   1. END WITH ONE DECISION, RECOMMENDATION, OR CONSEQUENCE.
   2. CLOSE CLEARLY WITHOUT ADDING NEW IDEAS.
D. Delivery Rule.
   1. 60-90 SECONDS.
   2. CLAIM -> EVIDENCE -> CLOSE.
