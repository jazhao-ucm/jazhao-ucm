# Management in Global Markets - Class Notes
## January 26, 2026 - Week 1

**Focus:** Globalization vs. slowbalization, economic complexity.

---

I. Globalization vs. Slowbalization
   A. Globalization
      1. Free movement of goods, services, capital across borders
      2. High production + high exportation
      3. Winners: China, India, Indonesia
      4. Characterized by interconnected economies
   B. Slowbalization
      1. Slowing down of globalization
      2. Countries becoming more protectionist
      3. Reduced exports/imports
      4. Focus on localization over globalization

II. Economic Complexity
   A. Definition
      1. Ability to transform raw materials into complex products
      2. Uses technology, innovation, R&D
   B. Key Points
      1. Raw materials → complex products (computers, iPhones)
      2. Complex products = higher selling prices
      3. Requires technology, innovation, research & development
   C. Top Countries
      1. USA
      2. Britain
      3. Netherlands
      4. UK
      5. Strong innovation and technology sectors
   D. Key Insight
      1. Innovation in technology = difference between economic complexity and global distribution
      2. Without innovation, countries stuck in middle income trap

III. Major Global Players
   A. China & India - Globalization Winners
      1. Demographics: massive populations
      2. Production: high capacity
      3. Exports: China = world's biggest exporter
      4. Labor: cheap labor force (e.g., $2/day)
      5. Politics: single party system (China) = less friction for business
      6. Status: ahead of US by years in certain sectors
   B. USA
      1. World's biggest importer
      2. Strong in software and technology
   C. Indonesia
      1. Top 3 in GDP (Gross Domestic Product)
      2. Rising economic power

IV. Localization
   A. Definition
      1. Moving from global to local
      2. Companies adapting products/services for specific local markets
   B. Purpose
      1. Necessary before internationalization
      2. Understand local culture, preferences

V. Geopolitical & Economic Shifts
   A. Western Politics Moving East
      1. Production and business shifting from Europe/USA to Asia
      2. Middle East importance (Saudi Arabia)
   B. Venezuela Situation
      1. Has twice the oil reserves of Saudi Arabia
      2. "Biggest gas station ever" - 100 years of reserves
      3. Despite reserves, experiencing economic difficulties
   C. Saudi Arabia
      1. Led by Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman (MBS)
      2. Royal family (House of Saud)
      3. 50+ years of business dominance in region

VI. Trade & Tariffs
   A. US Trade Policy
      1. Tariffs on China, Indonesia, others
      2. Purpose: protect domestic companies and jobs
      3. Strategy: "If we can't stop China, we slow them down"
      4. Using tariffs as protectionist measures
   B. Current Conflict
      1. Countries producing too fast
      2. Competition over production capacity
      3. Trade wars escalating

VII. The G20
   A. Composition
      1. 20 countries with highest GDP
      2. Most important for exports/imports
      3. Best industrialization
      4. Represents ~50% of global economy (with G7, G5)
   B. Why 20 Countries?
      1. Represent the biggest economies
      2. Most influential in global trade
      3. Spain is part of G20

VIII. Demographics & Future Projections
   A. Current State (2026)
      1. Green line = Europe
      2. Blue line = Asia
      3. Asia has largest population on Earth
      4. Europe and North America populations much smaller
   B. Asia - Next 50-60 Years
      1. Will peak then decline
      2. Currently has billions of people
      3. Growth slowing
   C. Africa - Next 50-60 Years
      1. Will see massive growth
      2. Nigeria alone experiencing significant population increase
      3. Africa will become the dominant demographic region
   D. Europe - By 2032
      1. 400 million people total (27 countries)
      2. 60% will be over 60 years old
      3. Aging population = major economic challenge
      4. Very small compared to Asia/Africa
   E. Implications
      1. Can't produce a lot without workers
      2. Labor force availability affects pricing
      3. Demographics impact business and production capacity

IX. GDP & Production
   A. Gross Domestic Product (GDP)
      1. Measurement of production of goods and services annually
      2. Based on previous year's performance
      3. Top 3: China, India, Indonesia
   B. Key Differentiating Factors
      1. Access to resources
      2. Innovation/technology
      3. Not language, demographics, or culture alone
      4. High-production countries: Brazil, Germany, USA, South Korea

X. Trade & Agriculture Example
   A. France vs. Brazil
      1. France exports meat, chicken, food
      2. Brazil produces rice and other products cheaper
         a. Larger land
         b. Lower costs
      3. French producers can't compete with Brazilian prices
      4. Creates tension over trade agreements
   B. Trade Deals Debate
      1. Good vs. bad trade deals
      2. Impact on domestic industries

XI. Key Takeaways
   A. Globalization is slowing → slowbalization happening now
   B. Economic complexity = key to high-value exports
   C. Demographic shifts will reshape global economy
      1. Africa rising
      2. Europe aging
   D. China/India dominate production and export markets
   E. USA remains world's largest importer
   F. Protectionism rising through tariffs and trade barriers
   G. Future belongs to countries with young populations and resources

XII. Important Statistics
   A. Venezuela: 2x Saudi Arabia's oil reserves
   B. Europe: 400M people, 60% over age 60 (by 2032)
   C. China: biggest exporter globally
   D. USA: biggest importer globally
   E. G20: represents ~50% of global economic activity

X. 30-Second Cold Call Version.
A. 3-Line Speaking Frame.
   1. TOPIC: STATE THE CORE QUESTION IN ONE LINE.
   2. LOGIC: GIVE 2 DRIVERS/FACTORS THAT EXPLAIN THE ANSWER.
   3. ACTION: END WITH ONE DECISION OR IMPLICATION.
B. Delivery Rule.
   1. 25-30 SECONDS MAX.
   2. NO BACKGROUND STORY; GO STRAIGHT TO CLAIM + EVIDENCE + CLOSE.

DI. Oral Answer Template (60-90 Seconds, Class Use).
A. Opening.
   1. STATE THE CORE QUESTION OR TOPIC IN ONE LINE.
   2. STATE YOUR MAIN CLAIM/THESIS IN ONE LINE.
B. Logic.
   1. GIVE 2-3 KEY FACTORS OR DRIVERS.
   2. LINK EACH FACTOR TO THE CLAIM (CAUSE -> EFFECT).
C. Action Or Implication.
   1. END WITH ONE DECISION, RECOMMENDATION, OR CONSEQUENCE.
   2. CLOSE CLEARLY WITHOUT ADDING NEW IDEAS.
D. Delivery Rule.
   1. 60-90 SECONDS.
   2. CLAIM -> EVIDENCE -> CLOSE.
