# Management in Global Markets - Class Notes
## January 27, 2026 - Week 1

**Focus:** Cross-cultural competence, national vs. international operations.

---

I. National vs. International Operations
   A. National Operations
      1. Working with people from same culture
      2. Makes things less complicated
      3. Easier communication and understanding
      4. Shared language and cultural norms
   B. International Operations
      1. Working with people from different cultures
      2. More complex and challenging
      3. Requires cross-cultural competence
      4. Need for empathy and understanding
   C. Key Insight
      1. Can't assume everyone shares same culture, language, business practices
      2. "Inside company, and you go nowhere" without empathy

II. Cross-Cultural Competence & Empathy
   A. Requirements for International Work
      1. Language - communication essential
      2. Empathy - understanding different perspectives
      3. Cultural awareness - recognizing differences
   B. Practical Application
      1. If you're 25 managing people in different countries
      2. Need empathy to understand perspectives
      3. Work effectively across cultures

III. Value Chain Analysis (Michael Porter)
   A. Purpose
      1. Recognize valuable activities vs. cost sources
      2. Identify differentiation advantages
      3. Determine competitive advantages
      4. Find areas for improvement within company
   B. Key Question
      1. What can we improve inside company to increase profitability?
      2. Tool to analyze company activities

IV. Primary Activities vs. Support Activities
   A. Primary Activities (Product-Related)
      1. Inbound Logistics - receiving, storing raw materials
      2. Operations - production/transformation of goods
      3. Outbound Logistics - moving finished products to customers
      4. Marketing & Sales - promoting and selling products
      5. Service - customer support and after-sales
   B. Support Activities (Enabling Functions)
      1. Procurement - purchasing raw materials, supplies
      2. Technology Development - R&D, innovation
      3. Human Resources - hiring, training, supporting employees
      4. Infrastructure - management, finance, administration
   C. Key Difference
      1. Primary = directly create value and generate revenue
      2. Support = enable primary activities, don't directly generate revenue
   D. Integration
      1. All departments work together and are connected
      2. Managers must communicate across departments

V. Marketing vs. Sales
   A. Marketing
      1. What you show to get customers
      2. Creates awareness and interest
      3. Comes first
   B. Sales
      1. What gets the money
      2. Converts interest into purchases
      3. Comes after marketing
   C. Process Flow
      1. Marketing → Sales → Profit

VI. Internationalization Process
   A. Order of Internationalization
      1. Sales (First)
         a. Need to explore new markets
         b. Find new customers
         c. Test the market
      2. Marketing (Second)
         a. Support sales efforts
         b. Build brand presence
         c. Understand local market
      3. Operations/Production (Later)
         a. Only after establishing market presence
         b. If it becomes cheaper to produce locally
         c. After many years of successful sales
   B. Why This Order?
      1. Need to check market first (sales)
      2. Marketing supports sales efforts
      3. Production is expensive and risky
      4. Only move when economically sensible
   C. Timeline
      1. Takes many years, not just one year
      2. Instructor's experience: 2 years just to move sales dept from France to Chile

VII. Globalization vs. Internationalization
   A. Globalization
      1. Moving departments to different locations
      2. Isolating departments in different countries
      3. Example: Production in China, Sales in USA, R&D in Europe
      4. "Not moving the whole world, isolating departments"
   B. Internationalization
      1. Moving entire company operations to a new country
      2. Complete relocation of business
   C. Key Difference
      1. Globalization = fragmenting operations across countries
      2. Internationalization = moving everything together

VIII. Logistics & Supply Chain
   A. Requirements for Logistics
      1. Good and strong infrastructure
      2. Warehouses - storage facilities
      3. Transportation - how to move goods
      4. Distribution networks - how to sell and deliver
   B. Four Types of Transportation
      1. Truck - for continental/land transport
      2. Train - for land transport
      3. Ship - 95% of world trade (most important)
      4. Plane - for urgent/time-sensitive goods
   C. Key Point
      1. Ships handle 95% of all world trade
      2. Most goods move by sea

IX. Procurement & Purchasing
   A. Why Look for Different Suppliers?
      1. Different needs
      2. Price comparison
      3. Quality comparison
      4. Delivery time options
      5. Always negotiate prices
   B. Location Considerations
      1. Simple products - can produce anywhere (e.g., bread)
      2. Complex products - need specific locations (e.g., advanced technology)
      3. Can't send complex products to China without specific expertise
   C. Foreign Direct Investment (FDI)
      1. When foreign companies invest in your country
      2. Creates competition
      3. Can benefit local economy

X. Hofstede's Cultural Dimensions
   A. Background
      1. Geert Hofstede - psychologist
      2. Studied IBM employees across different countries
      3. Identified key cultural dimensions
   B. Key Dimensions
      1. Masculinity vs. Femininity
         a. Different ways men and women relate
         b. Varies by culture
      2. Collectivism vs. Individualism
         a. Individualism: rich countries more individualistic
         b. Collectivism: poorer countries more group-oriented
      3. Power Distance (Most Important for Management)
         a. High Power Distance
            i. Strong hierarchy
            ii. Boss clearly superior
            iii. Employees show great respect/fear to boss
            iv. Examples: Spain, Mexico, North Korea
         b. Low Power Distance
            i. Flat hierarchy
            ii. Boss and employees work together as equals
            iii. Examples: Northern Europe, USA
            iv. More casual relationships
   C. Examples
      1. Spain: high power distance - "talk back to manager, get fired"
      2. Mexico: very high power distance
      3. Northern Europe: low power distance - everyone works together

XI. Power Distance & Management
   A. High Power Distance Countries
      1. Boss has absolute authority
      2. Employees fear boss
      3. Employees won't challenge or question
      4. If boss asks student to leave, student leaves immediately
   B. Low Power Distance Countries
      1. More collaborative
      2. Employees can question managers
      3. More equal relationships
   C. Management Insight
      1. "A good manager always blames yourself"
      2. If you fire someone, it's because you're doing something wrong
      3. "We don't control people, we manage them"
   D. Warning
      1. Power distance can lead to abuse of power
      2. Some professors/managers think they're great just because of position

XII. Hierarchy Differences Across Cultures
   A. Spain
      1. Strong hierarchy
      2. Can't talk back to manager
      3. High power distance
   B. Mexico
      1. Very high power distance
      2. Extreme hierarchy
      3. Salary differences reflect power distance
   C. USA
      1. More individualistic
      2. Lower power distance
      3. More equal relationships
   D. Key Point
      1. "Hierarchy is never the same" across countries
      2. What works in one country doesn't work in another

XIII. Finance Department
   A. Internal Focus
      1. Budget management
      2. Travel expenses
      3. Sales tracking
      4. Profit calculation
      5. Cash flow management
   B. Purpose
      1. Manage money flow within company
      2. Ensure profitability
      3. Not about stock market - about internal finances

XIV. Business Goals
   A. Primary Goal = Profit/Profitability
      1. "Any business, tell me what they're looking for, if it's not profit"
      2. If you make money, you're successful
      3. If you don't make money, you're not successful
   B. Key Principle
      1. Profit drives all business decisions
      2. Everything leads back to profitability

XV. Key Takeaways
   A. Cross-cultural competence requires empathy - essential for international business
   B. Value chain analysis helps identify value vs. costs
   C. Primary activities generate revenue; support activities enable them
   D. Internationalization order: Sales → Marketing → Operations (takes years)
   E. Power distance varies dramatically - affects management style
   F. High power distance = strict hierarchy, fear-based management
   G. Low power distance = collaborative, equal relationships
   H. All departments are connected - managers must communicate across functions
   I. Logistics is critical - 95% of trade moves by ship
   J. Culture matters - what works in one country doesn't work in another
