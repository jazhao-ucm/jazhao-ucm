## Formal outline (Management in Global Markets, Week 10)

I. Framing international management
   A. Execution over vague ambition
      1. Choose specific markets, regions, or country sets, not generic international
      2. Risk grows when location choice is fuzzy or undifferentiated
   B. Growth logic
      1. Domestic saturation pushes search abroad
      2. Capacity and commitment must match expansion choice

II. SWOT and analysis order (instructor emphasis)
   A. Internal before external
      1. Strengths and weaknesses first (inside firm)
      2. Then opportunities and threats (outside environment)
   B. Rationale
      1. Wrong order weakens diagnosis and wastes time
      2. External scan without self-knowledge misallocates resources

III. Market and location choice
   A. Geographic precision
      1. Name region or country group, not continent-only labels
      2. Map aligns with business model and offer
   B. Realism about export and scale
      1. Many firms stay predominantly domestic
      2. International share often small unless deliberate strategy fits

IV. Country and operating risk checklist (class themes)
   A. Tax and trade
      1. Tariffs, product-specific duties, double taxation risk
      2. Precedent and treaty context where relevant
   B. Institutions and fairness
      1. Transparency vs corruption perceptions
      2. Legal system reliability and property rights
   C. Discrimination and policy toward foreign firms
      1. Unequal tax or rule treatment vs local competitors
      2. Regulatory burden as de facto barrier
   D. Expropriation and asset risk
      1. State taking or forced transfer of assets with uncertain compensation
      2. Historical examples tied to political or crisis contexts

V. Demand before bricks
   A. Customers and opportunities precede heavy facility investment
      1. Interest and paying history reduce go-to-market risk
      2. Avoid chasing prospects with no commitment timeline
   B. Sequencing
      1. Build pipeline before large fixed cost abroad
      2. Cut losses and reallocate when deadlines miss repeatedly

VI. Action planning and accountability
   A. Plan elements
      1. Clear objectives tied to measurable outcomes
      2. Dates and deadlines per initiative
      3. Named owners per action (no anonymous group failure)
   B. Visualization
      1. Gantt or timeline for teams
      2. Escalation path when blockers hit finance, legal, or ops
   C. Function ownership
      1. Capital and treasury tied to CFO or authorized finance lead
      2. Market and competitor intel to marketing or designated research owner
      3. Clarity on who is accountable after setbacks

VII. People and international staffing
   A. Mobility reality
      1. Long assignments require willingness and family stability
      2. Short stays limit tacit knowledge transfer
   B. Alternatives
      1. Train host-country staff when expatriation is not sustainable
      2. Intercultural competence beyond language

VIII. Global integration vs local adaptation (closing thread)
   A. Competing pressures
      1. Forces favoring standardization and scale
      2. Forces requiring local fit and adaptation
   B. Firm must choose balance for strategy and offer

## Quick study summary

- **SWOT order:** inside (S/W) first, then outside (O/T).
- **Pick specific geographies** and validate **demand before major fixed investment**.
- **Country due diligence:** taxes, institutions, fairness to foreigners, **expropriation** risk.
- **Execute with owners, deadlines, and a visible plan**; avoid committees with no single accountable lead.
- **Staffing:** long-term internationalization needs **people who can stay** or **strong local capability build**.
