# Management in Global Markets - Class Notes
## March 24, 2026 - Week 9

I. Session Focus: From Strategy Design To Execution.
   A. Internationalization As Managed Sequence.
      1. INTERNATIONALIZATION = TRADE GROWTH DRIVER WHEN FIRM CAPABILITIES FIT TARGET MARKET.
      2. PRIORITY: REDUCE COST OF INEXPERIENCE VIA PLANNING + EXTERNAL DIAGNOSTIC.
      3. KEY CHECK: EXPORT READINESS BEFORE MARKET ENTRY COMMITMENT.
   B. Realignment And Institutionalization.
      1. CHANGE REQUIRES CULTURAL + TECHNICAL ADJUSTMENT TO STICK.
      2. ORGANIZATIONAL REALIGNMENT FAILS WHEN STRUCTURE CHANGES BUT BEHAVIORS DO NOT.

II. Change Management In International Operations.
   A. Urgency-Power Scenarios.
      1. LOW POWER + LOW URGENCY = LIMITED MOMENTUM, LOW IMPLEMENTATION SPEED.
      2. LOW POWER + HIGH URGENCY = HIGH STRESS, HIGH EXECUTION RISK.
      3. HIGH POWER + LOW URGENCY = DELAYED ACTION, POLITICAL DRAG.
      4. HIGH POWER + HIGH URGENCY = FAST DECISION WINDOW, HIGH COST OF ERRORS.
   B. Role Allocation By Hierarchy.
      1. CEO LEVEL: FINANCING, STRATEGIC DIRECTION, FACILITY INVESTMENT FRAME.
      2. BUSINESS UNIT LEVEL: PRICING, PRODUCTION, ADMIN/SELLING, R&D EXECUTION.
      3. HR/QUALITY INVESTMENT HAS TIME-LAG EFFECT; BENEFITS EMERGE NEXT PERIOD.

III. Performance Levers For International Growth.
   A. Financial Control Base.
      1. DECISIONS SHOULD BE TRACKED ACROSS INCOME STATEMENT, BALANCE SHEET, CASH FLOW.
      2. CASH GENERATION OPTIMIZATION IS CORE, NOT ONLY REVENUE GROWTH.
   B. Market Potential Drivers.
      1. INDUSTRY GROWTH RATE.
      2. MACRO BUSINESS CONDITIONS.
      3. PRICE LEVELS VS COMPETITORS.
      4. PRODUCT AVAILABILITY VOLUME.
      5. ADMIN/SELLING INTENSITY.
      6. R&D INVESTMENT EFFECT ON APPEAL + COST.
   C. Investment Logic.
      1. R&D: IMMEDIATE + CUMULATIVE EFFECT ON ATTRACTIVENESS AND COST.
      2. HR/QUALITY: PROCESS EFFECTIVENESS BOOST THROUGH MORALE + CAPABILITY.

IV. Economic Integration As Market Access Architecture.
   A. Why Integration Matters.
      1. REDUCES ECONOMIC BORDERS AND PROTECTIONIST FRICTION.
      2. REDUCES CURRENCY EXCHANGE COST/UNCERTAINTY.
      3. INCREASES MOBILITY OF GOODS, SERVICES, LABOR, CAPITAL.
   B. Integration Ladder (LOW TO HIGH).
      1. PREFERENTIAL AGREEMENTS.
      2. FREE TRADE AREA.
      3. CUSTOMS UNION.
      4. COMMON MARKET.
      5. ECONOMIC UNION.
      6. MONETARY UNION.
   C. Motivation Clusters.
      1. ECONOMIC: SCALE + COMPETITION + GROWTH.
      2. POLITICAL: COORDINATION + GLOBAL WEIGHT.
      3. SECURITY: LOWER INTERSTATE TENSION VIA INTERDEPENDENCE.
   D. Mercosur Application Notes.
      1. CORE MEMBERS: ARGENTINA, BRAZIL, PARAGUAY, URUGUAY.
      2. WORKING LANGUAGES: SPANISH + PORTUGUESE.
      3. MANAGERIAL AXES: GEOGRAPHY, COMMUNICATION, LAW, CULTURE.

V. B2B Sales Process Execution (LC0036).
   A. End-To-End Sales Loop.
      1. FIND LEADS.
      2. QUALIFY.
      3. ASSESS NEEDS.
      4. PRESENT SOLUTION.
      5. PROPOSAL.
      6. NEGOTIATE/CLOSE.
      7. REPEAT BUSINESS + REFERRALS.
   B. Revenue Strategy Inputs.
      1. PRODUCT-MARKET FIT.
      2. TARGET CUSTOMERS.
      3. BUYERS/USERS DISTINCTION.
      4. CUSTOMER PROCESS/SYSTEM MAP.
      5. FUNNEL DESIGN BY SEGMENT (NICHE SMB VS LARGE ENTERPRISE).
   C. Commercial Unit Economics.
      1. LTV.
      2. CAC.
      3. PROFIT MARGIN.
      4. REQUIREMENT: POSITIVE CAC PAYBACK LOGIC BEFORE SCALE.
   D. ICP And Persona Operationalization.
      1. ICP DIMENSIONS: INDUSTRY, SIZE, GEOGRAPHY, TIMING, PRIORITIES, INITIATIVES, CHALLENGES.
      2. PERSONA DIMENSIONS: FUNCTION, TITLE, BACKGROUND, KPI PRESSURES.
      3. QUALIFICATION QUALITY DETERMINES PIPELINE EFFICIENCY.

VII. Class Emphasis (Transcript-Derived Clarifications).
   A. Expansion Without Full Relocation.
      1. QUESTION FRAME = MOVE SELECTED DEPARTMENTS, NOT THE WHOLE COMPANY.
      2. DECISION UNIT = WHICH FUNCTION MOVES FIRST AND WHY.
      3. TARGET = SECURE BUSINESS CONTINUITY WHILE SCALING ABROAD.
   B. People-First Internationalization.
      1. DEPARTMENTS = PEOPLE + SKILLS, NOT JUST ORG CHART BOXES.
      2. CRITICAL COMPETENCIES MUST BE IDENTIFIED BEFORE LOCATION COMMITMENT.
      3. SOME CAPABILITIES ARE SCARCE AND REQUIRE LONGER HIRING/TRAINING WINDOWS.
   C. Location Choice Discipline.
      1. CHOOSE ONE PRIORITY LOCATION FIRST (LIMITED MONEY/TIME/TEAM).
      2. USE FACTORS, NOT PREFERENCE: DEMAND, PRICE/COST LEVEL, TAXES, CONNECTIVITY, RISK.
      3. SITE VISIT + LOCAL VALIDATION REQUIRED BEFORE FINAL COMMITMENT.
   D. Risk And Resource Logic.
      1. ZERO RISK DOES NOT EXIST; OBJECTIVE = RISK REDUCTION, NOT ELIMINATION.
      2. BUDGET IS CONSTRAINED, SO SEQUENCING MUST BE STRICT.
      3. HIRE IN PHASES (SLOW-FAST MIX) BASED ON OPERATIONAL PRIORITIES.
   E. Execution Order Reminder.
      1. DEFINE REQUIRED COMPETENCIES.
      2. SCREEN LOCATION WITH NUMERIC INDICATORS.
      3. DECIDE FUNCTION TRANSFER ORDER.
      4. DEPLOY CORE TRUSTED TEAM, THEN SCALE LOCAL STAFF.

VIII. Slide-Accurate Market Screening Matrix.
   A. GDP/Population Logic.
      1. HIGHER GDP PER CAPITA + GROWTH FORECAST = HIGHER MARKET ATTRACTIVENESS.
      2. LOWER GDP PER CAPITA MAY OFFER LOWER PRICE/COST LEVELS.
   B. Risk/Barrier Variables.
      1. COUNTRY RISK (POLITICAL CRISIS PROBABILITY).
      2. TRADE BARRIERS (TARIFF/COST IMPACT).
      3. INDUSTRY RIVALRY (MARGIN PRESSURE + PROMOTION INTENSITY).
   C. Internal SWOT Capability Fields.
      1. FINANCIAL RESOURCES FOR FOREIGN INVESTMENT.
      2. KNOWLEDGE OF FOREIGN MARKETS.
      3. INTERNATIONAL EXPERIENCE OF PERSONNEL.
      4. COMMUNICATION SYSTEMS + NETWORKING + LANGUAGE + BRAND + CAPACITY.
   D. External SWOT Opportunity/Threat Fields.
      1. TARIFFS/BARRIERS.
      2. FREE TRADE AGREEMENTS.
      3. INTERNET USAGE LEVEL.
      4. LOGISTICS EASE.
      5. MARKET MATURITY + COMPETITOR DENSITY + INSTITUTIONAL TRANSPARENCY.

IX. Function-Transfer Strategy (Without Moving Whole Company).
   A. Keep At HQ First.
      1. CAPITAL ALLOCATION + TREASURY CONTROL.
      2. CORE BRAND GOVERNANCE + STRATEGIC DIRECTION.
      3. HIGH-SENSITIVITY TAX/COMPLIANCE OVERSIGHT.
   B. Move Early (Pilot Phase).
      1. LOCAL COMMERCIAL/SALES + RELATIONSHIP BUILDING.
      2. CUSTOMER SUPPORT + NEEDS FEEDBACK LOOP.
      3. SELECTED SUPPLY CHAIN/OPERATIONS ROLES FOR EXECUTION SPEED.
   C. Move Later (Scale Phase).
      1. PARTIAL PRODUCTION/PROCESS CELLS (IF VOLUME JUSTIFIES).
      2. LOCAL HR HIRING ENGINE + QUALITY SYSTEM.
      3. EXPANDED ADMIN FUNCTIONS AFTER PROCESS STABILITY.
   D. Transfer Rule.
      1. MOVE FUNCTIONS ONLY WHEN SKILLS, COST, AND CONTROL THRESHOLDS ARE MET.
      2. AVOID BIG-BANG TRANSFER; USE STAGE GATES.

X. Quant And Control Layer (What To Measure Before/After Entry).
   A. Pre-Entry Minimum Dashboard.
      1. MARKET DEMAND PROXY.
      2. PRICE/COST SPREAD.
      3. TAX/REGULATORY BURDEN.
      4. LOGISTICS LEAD TIME + CONNECTIVITY SCORE.
      5. SKILL AVAILABILITY INDEX.
   B. Post-Entry Execution Dashboard.
      1. PIPELINE VELOCITY (LEADS -> QUALIFIED -> WON).
      2. CASH GENERATION VS PLAN.
      3. QUALITY/CYCLE TIME.
      4. TEAM STABILITY (HIRING, RETENTION, TRAINING TIME-TO-PRODUCTIVITY).
   C. Decision Triggers.
      1. IF CAC PAYBACK WORSENS -> TIGHTEN ICP + QUALIFICATION.
      2. IF QUALITY DROPS -> SLOW EXPANSION + REINFORCE HR/QUALITY.
      3. IF RISK SPIKES -> HOLD CAPEX + RESEQUENCE FUNCTION TRANSFER.

XI. Oral Answer Template (60-90 Seconds, Class Use).
   A. Opening (10-15 Seconds).
      1. "MY STRATEGY IS TO INTERNATIONALIZE WITHOUT MOVING THE WHOLE COMPANY."
      2. "I WILL SELECT ONE PRIORITY MARKET, THEN MOVE FUNCTIONS IN PHASES."
   B. Location Choice (20-25 Seconds).
      1. "I CHOOSE [COUNTRY/CITY] BASED ON FIVE FACTORS: DEMAND, COST LEVEL, TAX/REGULATION, LOGISTICS, SKILL AVAILABILITY."
      2. "I VALIDATE WITH SWOT + SITE VISIT, NOT PERSONAL PREFERENCE."
      3. "I ACCEPT RISK CANNOT BE ZERO, SO I PLAN RISK MITIGATION BEFORE ENTRY."
   C. Department Transfer Plan (20-25 Seconds).
      1. "PHASE 1: KEEP FINANCE/STRATEGY CORE AT HQ, MOVE SALES + CUSTOMER SUPPORT FIRST."
      2. "PHASE 2: ADD SELECTED OPERATIONS/SUPPLY CHAIN ROLES WHEN DEMAND IS STABLE."
      3. "PHASE 3: SCALE LOCAL HR/QUALITY + ADMIN AFTER PROCESS KPIs STABILIZE."
   D. People And Competencies (10-15 Seconds).
      1. "DEPARTMENTS MEAN PEOPLE, SO I MAP REQUIRED SKILLS BEFORE TRANSFER."
      2. "I DEPLOY A SMALL TRUSTED CORE TEAM, THEN HIRE/TRAIN LOCALLY IN STAGES."
   E. Control And Close (10 Seconds).
      1. "I TRACK PIPELINE, CASH GENERATION, QUALITY, AND RETENTION."
      2. "IF METRICS DETERIORATE, I SLOW EXPANSION, FIX PROCESS, THEN RESUME."

XII. March 24 Lecture Addendum (Decision Discipline).
   A. Speed Vs Safety In Market Entry.
      1. GOING TOO FAST IN A NEW LOCATION CAN DESTROY INVESTMENT VALUE.
      2. WRONG LOCATION/FUNCTION CHOICE MAY NOT KILL DEMAND, BUT CAN KILL RETURNS.
      3. MANAGEMENT QUALITY = HOW WELL RISK IS REDUCED BEFORE SCALE.
   B. Trust And Control In Early Internationalization.
      1. EARLY STAGE SHOULD NOT DEPEND ON PURE TRUST OR INSTINCT.
      2. USE VERIFIED INDICATORS, SCHEDULE, CAPABILITY MAP, AND LOCATION FACTORS.
      3. PERSONAL BELIEF IS NOT A SUBSTITUTE FOR OPERATING EVIDENCE.
   C. Risk Logic From Class Emphasis.
      1. OBJECTIVE IS NOT ZERO RISK; OBJECTIVE IS CONTROLLED RISK.
      2. IF UNCERTAINTY IS HIGH, CHOOSE SAFER/LOWER-COMPLEXITY ENTRY MODE.
      3. PHASED DECISIONS PROTECT CAPITAL AND LEARNING CURVE.
   D. Stage-Dependent Strategy Rule.
      1. BEST OPTION DEPENDS ON COMPANY STAGE (EARLY ENTRY VS ESTABLISHED GLOBAL PLAYER).
      2. AT BEGINNING: PRIORITIZE STABLE, WELL-CONTROLLED LOCATIONS/FUNCTIONS.
      3. AFTER CONSOLIDATION: EXPAND INTO HIGHER-COMPLEXITY MARKETS.
   E. Capital Responsibility Reminder.
      1. MANAGERS DEPLOY COMPANY CAPITAL, NOT PERSONAL CAPITAL.
      2. DECISIONS MUST PROTECT INVESTMENT, PEOPLE, AND LONG-TERM OPERATIONS.
      3. DISCIPLINE > EGO IN INTERNATIONAL EXECUTION.
