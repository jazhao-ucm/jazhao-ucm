# Management in Global Markets - Class Notes
## March 23, 2026 - Week 9

I. Internationalization Strategy (Doing Business In Europe Part 2).
   A. Definition And Logic.
      1. INTERNATIONALIZATION = DEVELOP COMPANY ACTIVITIES ABROAD.
      2. OFTEN PROGRESSIVE (SEARCH FOR NEW MARKETS), NOT ONE-TIME DECISION.
      3. IMPACTS BOTH CULTURE AND ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE.
   B. Internationalization Schedule.
      1. INTERNAL SWOT.
      2. EXTERNAL SWOT.
      3. TARGET MARKET SELECTION.
      4. ACTION PLAN (SMART GOALS, ROLES, TIMING).
   C. Internal SWOT Focus.
      1. FINANCIAL CAPACITY.
      2. FOREIGN MARKET KNOWLEDGE.
      3. PERSONNEL INTERNATIONAL EXPERIENCE.
      4. COMMUNICATION/TECH SYSTEMS.
      5. NETWORKS, LANGUAGE, BRAND, CAPACITY, DECISION PROCESS.
   D. External SWOT Focus.
      1. TARIFFS/BARRIERS.
      2. TRADE AGREEMENTS.
      3. INTERNET PENETRATION.
      4. LOGISTICS EASE.
      5. CONSUMER HABITS + MARKET MATURITY.
      6. INSTITUTIONAL TRANSPARENCY + COMPETITOR DENSITY.

II. Market Selection And Location Choice.
   A. Strategic Orientation.
      1. CONCENTRATION = FEW MARKETS, DEEP FOCUS.
      2. DIVERSIFICATION = MANY MARKETS, RISK SPREAD.
   B. Screening Variables.
      1. GDP PER CAPITA + GDP GROWTH FORECAST.
      2. COUNTRY RISK + TRADE BARRIERS + INDUSTRY RIVALRY.
      3. PRICE LEVEL/COST STRUCTURE IN TARGET MARKET.
   C. Location Decision Pattern.
      1. CHOICE MUST FIT COST, SECURITY, ACCESS, REGULATORY STABILITY, OPERATIONS.
      2. DO NOT USE REGION STEREOTYPES; USE COUNTRY-LEVEL DATA.
      3. CHOKEPOINTS/BORDER GEOGRAPHY CAN DRIVE LOGISTICS VALUE.
      4. POLITICAL INSTABILITY CAN CANCEL HIGH MARKET POTENTIAL.
      5. HIGH UNCERTAINTY = START WITH PARTIAL RELOCATION (SELECTED FUNCTIONS).
      6. KEY QUESTION: WHICH FUNCTION MOVES FIRST FOR VALUE + MANAGEABLE RISK?

III. Global-Local Organization And Export Path.
   A. Global/Local Grid.
      1. GLOBAL DOMINANCE (HIGH GLOBAL PRESSURE, LOW LOCAL PRESSURE).
      2. LOCAL DOMINANCE (HIGH LOCAL ADAPTATION PRESSURE).
      3. MIXED/GLOCAL (HYBRID MODEL).
   B. Export Development Phases.
      1. PRE-ENGAGEMENT.
      2. INITIAL EXPORTING.
      3. ADVANCED EXPORTING.
   C. Centralization Vs Decentralization.
      1. CENTRALIZED = HQ-LED DECISIONS.
      2. DECENTRALIZED = MORE LOCAL AUTHORITY.
   D. Project Management Levers.
      1. SCOPE.
      2. RESOURCES.
      3. SCHEDULE.
      4. BROOKS'S LAW: MORE PEOPLE CAN INCREASE COORDINATION COST.

IV. Organizational Change (Doing Business In Europe Part 3).
   A. Urgency/Authority Matrix.
      1. LOW POWER + LOW URGENCY.
      2. HIGH POWER + LOW URGENCY.
      3. LOW POWER + HIGH URGENCY.
      4. HIGH POWER + HIGH URGENCY.
   B. Types Of Change.
      1. REACTIVE (PERFORMANCE GAP).
      2. PROACTIVE (OPPORTUNITY GAP).
      3. PASSIVE (LAST-RESORT RESPONSE).
   C. Process Orientation.
      1. CRISIS CONTEXT = FAST RESULTS + DIRECTION SHIFT.
      2. STABLE CONTEXT = EDUCATIONAL, SLOWER CHANGE PATH.
   D. CEO Vs Middle Management.
      1. CEO: CAPITAL, STRATEGIC MARGIN, POLICY/TAX, HIGH-LEVEL RELATIONSHIPS.
      2. MIDDLE/LOWER LEVELS: EXECUTION, CUSTOMER RELATIONS, SUPPLY CHAIN, TENDERS.
      3. ANTI-PATTERNS: ROLE OVERREACH, AUTONOMY VIOLATIONS, TOP-DOWN MICROMANAGEMENT.

V. Performance Drivers And Leadership.
   A. Core Financial Statements.
      1. INCOME STATEMENT.
      2. BALANCE SHEET.
      3. CASH FLOW STATEMENT.
   B. Investment Effects.
      1. R&D: PRODUCT ATTRACTIVENESS + COST REDUCTION.
      2. HR/QUALITY: MORALE + EXECUTION IMPROVEMENT (LAG EFFECT).
   C. Market Size Drivers.
      1. INDUSTRY GROWTH.
      2. GENERAL ECONOMY.
      3. PRICE LEVEL.
      4. AVAILABLE QUANTITY.
      5. ADMIN/SELLING EXPENSES.
      6. R&D SPEND.
   D. Team Conflict.
      1. DISAGREEMENT.
      2. CONSEQUENCE CONFLICT.
      3. PERSONALITY CONFLICT.
   E. Leadership Notes.
      1. ADAPTIVE LEADERSHIP = ITERATIVE INTERVENTIONS.
      2. FAILURE PROVIDES LEARNING SIGNALS.
      3. CONFLICT MANAGEMENT > CONFLICT AVOIDANCE.

VI. Economic Integration And Mercosur.
   A. Macro Outlook Framing.
      1. G7 TO G20 SHIFT = BROADER GLOBAL COORDINATION.
      2. G20 WEIGHT: LARGE SHARE OF WORLD OUTPUT/TRADE/POPULATION.
   B. Why Integrate.
      1. REDUCE ECONOMIC BORDERS.
      2. LOWER CURRENCY FRICTION.
      3. IMPROVE FACTOR MOBILITY.
      4. ALIGN MACRO/MICRO POLICY CONDITIONS.
   C. Integration Ladder (LOW TO HIGH).
      1. PREFERENTIAL AGREEMENT.
      2. FREE TRADE AREA.
      3. CUSTOMS UNION.
      4. COMMON MARKET.
      5. ECONOMIC UNION.
      6. MONETARY UNION.
   D. Integration Motivations.
      1. ECONOMIC (MARKET SCALE, COMPETITION, GROWTH).
      2. POLITICAL (COORDINATION, GLOBAL WEIGHT).
      3. SECURITY (INTERDEPENDENCE, LOWER CONFLICT RISK).
   E. Mercosur Notes.
      1. CORE: ARGENTINA, BRAZIL, PARAGUAY, URUGUAY.
      2. WORKING LANGUAGES: SPANISH + PORTUGUESE.
      3. SOUTH AMERICA CONTEXT MATTERS FOR TRADE AND LOGISTICS.
   F. International Manager Axes.
      1. GEOGRAPHY.
      2. COMMUNICATION.
      3. LAW.
      4. CULTURE.

VII. B2B Sales Process (LC0036 iSense LiveCase).
   A. Continuous Sales Loop.
      1. FIND LEADS.
      2. QUALIFY.
      3. ASSESS NEEDS.
      4. PRESENT SOLUTION.
      5. PROPOSAL.
      6. NEGOTIATE/CLOSE.
      7. REPEAT BUSINESS + REFERRALS.
   B. Revenue Strategy Foundations.
      1. PRODUCT-MARKET FIT.
      2. TARGET CUSTOMERS.
      3. BUYER/USER MAP.
      4. CURRENT PROCESS/SYSTEM MAP.
      5. FUNNEL SHAPE BY SEGMENT (NICHE, ENTERPRISE, SMB).
   C. Metrics.
      1. LTV.
      2. CAC.
      3. PROFIT MARGIN.
      4. UNIT ECONOMICS CHECK (E.G. \$3 SPEND FOR \$1 REVENUE = RED FLAG).
   D. Lead Quality Tools.
      1. ICP (ORG PROFILE).
      2. BUYER PERSONAS (INDIVIDUAL PROFILE).
      3. RESEARCH STACK: LINKEDIN + DATA TOOLS + PUBLIC FILINGS.
      4. INBOUND VS OUTBOUND FIT BY SEGMENT/SALES CYCLE.
   E. Qualification.
      1. FRAMEWORKS: BANT, CHAMP, MEDDPICC.
      2. OPEN-ENDED QUESTIONS > YES/NO QUESTIONS.
      3. CURIOSITY MODE, NOT INTERROGATION MODE.
      4. BAD QUALIFICATION = HIGH SALARY + OPPORTUNITY COST.
   F. Needs, Value, And Proposal.
      1. ASK BEFORE PITCH.
      2. MAP PAIN -> IDEAL STATE -> BUSINESS OUTCOME.
      3. TIE VALUE TO METRICS (REVENUE, COST, RISK).
      4. USE INDUSTRY-RELEVANT SUCCESS STORIES.
      5. PACKAGE PROPOSALS CLEARLY; OFFER OPTIONS WHEN NEEDED.
   G. Negotiation And Close.
      1. NEGOTIATION = TRUST + RECIPROCITY (GIVES/GETS).
      2. VALIDATE PROCUREMENT/BUDGET/LEGAL PROCESS EARLY.
      3. BE READY TO WALK AWAY FROM BAD-FIT DEALS.
      4. HARD CLOSE VS SOFT CLOSE DEPENDS ON CONTEXT.
      5. CLOSING IS INCREMENTAL ACROSS ALL TOUCHPOINTS.
