% ========== INTRODUCTION ==========
\section*{\color{sectionblue}Introduction to Caf\'eOl\'e! Clubworking}\label{sec:intro}

\textbf{So here's the deal.} Caf\'eOl\'e! is an existing work cafe in Chamber\'i, Madrid. Wifi, private workspaces, networking events, affordable snacks and drinks. Reserve online or walk in. Five euros per hour or 25 euros day pass... unlimited coffee and snacks. From class: a business plan explains the opportunity, identifies the market, and details how we pursue it (Harvard Business School definition).

\textbf{Scope and objective (from the course structure 4.1):}\\
Make it suitable for students, especially Erasmus and study abroad. Right now we're geared towards professionals. The improvement: student discounts (e.g. 30 euros/month), university partnerships (Complutense), events (study groups, language exchanges). Target: students who need safe, reliable workspace and community. ``Feel at home in a new city''... that's the pathos from class.

\textbf{Competitive advantage (from class):}\\
Professor said ``location is the masterpiece.'' Chamber\'i is near University City, corporate areas, tourism. The gap: most Madrid cafes have limited space, limited free wifi, turn away laptops at peak. Caf\'eOl\'e! has high-speed internet, quiet zones, communal seating. We can bridge the gap. When students find community, they return. That increases profit and reputation. ``Go where competitors are''... La Bicicleta and Federal validate the market. We differentiate with student focus and infrastructure.

% ========== EXECUTIVE SUMMARY ==========
\section*{\color{sectionblue}Executive Summary}\label{sec:exec}

\textbf{Alright, let me break this down.} Caf\'eOl\'e! is a work cafe in Chamber\'i... 5 euros/hour, 25 euros day pass, unlimited coffee and snacks, wifi, workspaces. Right now it's pro-focused. We're shifting to students. Madrid has 11M+ tourists (from class), Erasmus every semester, and basically zero cafes built for the ``work 4 hours and maybe meet people'' crowd. We're already set up for it. High-speed internet, quiet zones, printers. The gap is real.

\textbf{The improvement (course 4.1):} Student discount (20\% off), 30 euros/month membership, Complutense partnership, community events (language exchange, study groups). When students find community, they come back. Customer loyalty... that's our competitive advantage from class.

\textbf{PESTLE (macro-environment from class):} Political... Spain SL, Madrid regulations, health certs. Economic... unemployment 9\% (lowest since 2008), tourism 11M+, student budgets. Social... demand for wifi, work-friendly cafes, healthy food trend. Tech... reviews (Yelp, Google), social media, fast wifi. Legal... AESAN, HACCP, minimum wage (1,134 euros full-time, 620 part-time). Environmental... sustainable packaging, younger generations care.

\textbf{SWOT (from class):} Strengths... location, wifi, printers, community. Weaknesses... no menu online, prices add up. Opportunities... university partnerships, gentrification. Threats... La Bicicleta, Federal Cafe, seasonal tourism.

\textbf{Key takeaway:} We're the only one targeting students. Most Madrid cafes have limited wifi and turn away laptops at peak. We don't. Viable. Execute.

% ========== PESTLE ANALYSIS (course 4.3) ==========
\section*{\color{sectionblue}PESTLE Analysis}\label{sec:pestle}

\textbf{From class: PESTLE = macro-environment.} Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal, Environmental. Here's how it applies to Caf\'eOl\'e!:

\textbf{\textcolor{cafebrown}{Political:}} Spain Sociedad Limitada (SL). Madrid: health and hygiene certifications, business operating license. Labour laws: minimum wage, working hours.

\textbf{\textcolor{cafebrown}{Economic:}} Student budgets limited... discount helps. Unemployment 9\% (2025) lowest since 2008... more customers. Tourism 11M+... remote workers need workspace. Inflation affects food and supplies.

\textbf{\textcolor{cafebrown}{Social:}} Demand for reliable wifi and work-friendly cafes. Students want study spaces near university. Professionals want change of environment. Trend: healthy food and drinks in cafes.

\textbf{\textcolor{cafebrown}{Technological:}} Online reviews (Yelp, Google) influence visits. Social media (Instagram, TikTok) drives traffic. Fast wifi, reliable internet: key for retention. Printers, contactless payment (Apple Pay).

\textbf{\textcolor{cafebrown}{Legal:}} AESAN (Agency for Food Safety and Nutrition). HACCP required. Minimum wage: 1,134 euros full-time (40h), 620 euros part-time (20h).

\textbf{\textcolor{cafebrown}{Environmental:}} Sustainable packaging improves brand image. Younger generations value recycling and sustainability. Cleaner energy: reduce impact and long-term costs.

% ========== SWOT ANALYSIS (course 4.3) ==========
\section*{\color{sectionblue}SWOT Analysis}\label{sec:swot}

\textbf{From class:} SWOT = internal (Strengths, Weaknesses) + external (Opportunities, Threats). Be specific.

\textbf{\textcolor{cafebrown}{Strengths:}} Caf\'eOl\'e!'s ability to bring a reliable environment for a wide range of professional and student audiences. Quality coffee and pastries, all included in the hourly usage fee. Central location in Chamber\'i, close to corporate areas and universities. Fast WiFi and printers, variables that are not found in all coffee shops in Madrid. Community aspects like networking and language exchanges.

\textbf{\textcolor{cafebrown}{Weaknesses:}} Menu not available on the website, making it difficult for potential customers to determine their visit. Prices can get expensive for longer periods of time, and customers may not feel like they are getting their money's worth if they are not utilising the unlimited food and drink offer as much as they can. Dependence on in-store sales instead of delivery options.

\textbf{\textcolor{cafebrown}{Opportunities:}} Increased access to study abroad and Erasmus networks through partnerships with universities. Tourism in Madrid is experiencing increasing popularity, and the Chamber\'i neighbourhood is experiencing gentrification. Sustainable packaging and vegan options could increase customer interest. Collaborations with local and beloved bakeries to increase popularity.

\textbf{\textcolor{cafebrown}{Threats:}} Other coworking spaces in different locations. La Bicicleta in Malasa\~na and Federal Cafe in Chamber\'i are laptop and study-friendly competitors. Changes in consumer preferences in the coffee shop aesthetic or location. Fluctuations in tourism through different seasons. Fluctuating costs of resources like coffee beans, milk, and pastries from local bakeries.

% ========== ECONOMIC AND SOCIAL ANALYSIS (like PDF) ==========
\section*{\color{sectionblue}Economic and Social Analysis}\label{sec:economic}

In 2025, Madrid had over 11 million tourists, meaning that tourism is a reliable source of customers for Caf\'eOl\'e!. Considering the number of tourists, many people want a reliable space where they can catch up on work, which is why Caf\'eOl\'e! is beneficial. Additionally, there is an increasing social trend of cafe culture, which is ideal for the cafe. Spain is facing inflation, as many countries are. This could increase the cost of food and cafe supplies, which could lead to price increases. Fortunately, Spain's unemployment rate was 9 percent in 2025, the lowest it has been since 2008. This allows for more customers, especially those who need a space to work.

% ========== COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS (course 4.4) ==========
\section*{\color{sectionblue}Competitive Analysis}\label{sec:competition}

\textbf{So who else is in this market?} From class: competitive analysis asks who serves the market and what's the gap.

As discussed, Caf\'eOl\'e! caters itself as a ``clubworking'' and ``professional/coworking'' coffee destination with premium membership prices up to 300 euros per month with inclusion such as wifi, workstations, available coffee, printers, scanners, storage services, private rooms, and meeting rooms. However, 300 euros a month can be a steep price for students, as many do not work, isolating this cafe as a ``professional'' one. Moreover, Caf\'eOl\'e! faces stiff competition due to Madrid's competitive cafe scene and already-established cafes that are laptop and study-friendly, such as La Bicicleta in Malasa\~na and the Federal Cafe in Chamber\'i. For example, La Bicicleta offers moderate pricing for food and specialty coffees, laptop-friendly tables, access to wifi, and an urban space that makes this cafe highly competitive and extremely appealing to not only professionals, but the student population as well.

However, the majority of cafes in Madrid that cater to students and professionals looking to work with peers on technology tend to have limited space, limited free wifi, and individuals can be turned away or told to put their laptops away during peak hours of the day, especially during the weekends. These regulations and protocols can make it challenging for students and professionals looking for an affordable space to work and collaborate with one another at any time during the day, into the late afternoon. Where these cafes fall short, Caf\'eOl\'e! can step in to bridge that gap. Caf\'eOl\'e! has existing infrastructure such as high-speed internet, dedicated quiet zones, and communal seating. With further additions that help cater more toward a student population, Caf\'eOl\'e! has the potential to become the next best cafe that both students and professionals can thrive in.

% ========== STRATEGIC PLAN (course 4.5) ==========
\section*{\color{sectionblue}Strategic Plan}\label{sec:strategy}

\textbf{From class:} Strategic plan needs vision, mission, values, objectives, short-term (0--12 months), long-term (1--3 years). Quote from Week 5: ``Strategy is for 15 years, not for 15 days. Slow is fast.''

\textbf{Objectives:} (1) Make Caf\'eOl\'e! the go-to place for students and Erasmus in Chamber\'i. (2) Retain professionals. We're not replacing them, just adding. Differentiation: we focus on students, community events (language exchange, study groups), wifi and printers that work, unlimited coffee and snacks. Other cafes limit laptop use or have unreliable wifi. We have the location (Chamber\'i, near universities, corporate, tourism), the infrastructure, the space.

\textbf{Short-term (0--12 months) from class:} Roll out student discount (20\% off hourly, 30 euros monthly membership). Put the menu on the website. Get in touch with Complutense for a partnership. Flyers, accept student cards. Run 1 event per week at first (language exchange or study group). Scale to 2--3 per month if attendance justifies it.

\textbf{Long-term (1--3 years) from class:} Be the recognised work cafe for study abroad in Madrid. Second location or franchise. Key success factors: university partnerships, retention, word-of-mouth. Maintain wifi, coffee, community.

% ========== CONTINGENCY PLAN (course 4.6) ==========
\section*{\color{sectionblue}Contingency Plan}\label{sec:contingency}

\textbf{From class:} Contingency plan covers key scenarios. Here's what we do if things go wrong:

\textbf{\textcolor{cafebrown}{Low student uptake:}} Push university marketing. Complutense, flyers. Run a survey to find out what's not working. Adjust the offer. If it still doesn't work after 12 months, refocus on professionals. We're not burning bridges.

\textbf{\textcolor{cafebrown}{Competitor response:}} La Bicicleta and Federal Cafe are the main competitors. If they copy our student offer, we lean into what they can't easily copy: dedicated work setup, printers, quiet zones, university partnerships. We have the infrastructure.

\textbf{\textcolor{cafebrown}{Rising costs:}} Negotiate suppliers for better terms. Maybe a small price adjustment. Keep the student rate reasonable.

\textbf{\textcolor{cafebrown}{Seasonal drop:}} Tourism peaks summer, dips Jan--Feb. Erasmus arrivals Sept/Feb. When it's quiet, focus on locals and semester memberships for students.

\textbf{\textcolor{cafebrown}{Tech failure:}} If the wifi goes, we're in trouble. From class: ``If the WiFi goes out, we are all in trouble.'' Backup internet and proper maintenance. Fast wifi and reliable internet are key for retention.
