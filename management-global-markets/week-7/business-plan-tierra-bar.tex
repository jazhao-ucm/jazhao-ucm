% ===== 1. OVERVIEW =====
\section{Overview}\label{sec:overview}

Tierra Bar (Tierra Burrito Bar) operates at Plaza de Isabel II, 5, Madrid Centro, near \'Opera. The concept: customers assemble their own meals from bases, proteins (chicken, carnitas, steak, vegetarian), rice, beans, and salsas. The venue functions as both restaurant and bar, with margaritas and 16 craft beer taps. Weekend closing: 1:30 AM. Delivery via Glovo and Just Eat. Current rating: 9/10 (3,500+ reviews). Locations: Madrid, Barcelona, Salamanca.

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,nosep]
\item \textbf{Goal:} Position Tierra Bar as the late-night Mexican option for students (Erasmus, study abroad) who want affordable food, drinks, and a place to meet or work.
\item \textbf{Plan:} Introduce 15\% student discount, Tierra Student Pass (20 euros/month: one free meal per week + drink discounts), reserved late-night tables, and events (Taco Tuesday language exchange, Margarita Meetups).
\item \textbf{Edge:} Few bars in Madrid combine late hours, craft beer, quality food, student offers, and community events. Tierra Bar has the location and reviews; the student programme creates loyalty.
\end{itemize}

\vspace{1em}

% ===== 2. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY =====
\section{Executive Summary}

Tierra Bar is a Mexican bar-restaurant in Madrid Centro. Current clientele: tourists, professionals, locals. The proposal is to add students as a core segment.

Erasmus and study abroad students need affordable late-night options. The Student Pass (20 euros/month) and 15\% discount address this. Events build community. Repeat visits matter: cash comes from customers who return.

Market gap: Madrid has many bars and Mexican restaurants, but almost none target students with discounts and events. Tierra Bar can occupy this space.

\textbf{PESTLE:} Strong tourism (11M in 2025), unemployment at 9\% (lowest since 2008), demand for casual dining, clear regulations, social media influence, interest in sustainability.

\textbf{SWOT:} Strengths: location, food, craft beer, late hours, reviews. Weaknesses: no student programme yet. Opportunities: Complutense, UAM, Erasmus, tourism. Threats: Taco Bell, local taquer\'ias, cost increases.

\textbf{Risks \& response:} Low student uptake $\rightarrow$ intensify university marketing or refocus. Competitors copy $\rightarrow$ emphasise events and partnerships. Costs rise $\rightarrow$ negotiate suppliers. Conclusion: the student focus is viable.

% ===== 7. ECONOMIC \& SOCIAL =====
\section{Economic and Social Analysis}

Madrid received over 11 million tourists in 2025. Casual Mexican food and craft beer are in demand. Inflation raises food and alcohol costs; unemployment at 9\% (lowest since 2008) supports spending. Students and young professionals seek affordable venues. The market exists; cash follows when customers return.

\vspace{1em}

% ===== 8. COMPETITION =====
\section{Competitive Analysis}

Competitors: Taco Bell (chain, different positioning), local taquer\'ias (smaller, less bar-focused), general bars in Centro. Tierra Bar differentiates through customisable food, 16 craft taps, margaritas, late hours, and reviews. Almost no competitor targets students with discounts and events. Going where competitors are confirms the market; our student programme creates differentiation. Resources (location, kitchen, bar) and capabilities (events, partnerships) support this.

\vspace{1em}

% ===== 9. STRATEGY =====
\section{Strategic Plan}

\textbf{Objectives:} (1) Make Tierra Bar the Mexican bar of choice for students in Centro. (2) Retain tourists and locals. Differentiation via student focus, events, discount, Student Pass.

\textbf{Short-term (0--12 months):} Launch 15\% discount and Student Pass. Reserve study tables. First Taco Tuesday and Margarita Meetup. Flyers at Complutense and UAM. Scale events if attendance justifies.

\textbf{Long-term (1--3 years):} Establish as the Mexican bar for study abroad. Consider second location. Focus: university partnerships, retention, word-of-mouth. Strategy is long-term (15 years, not 15 days).

\vspace{1em}

% ===== 10. CONTINGENCY =====
\section{Contingency Plan}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,nosep]
\item \textbf{Low student uptake:} Intensify marketing at Complutense and UAM. Survey and adjust. After 12 months, refocus on tourists and locals if needed.
\item \textbf{Competitor copy:} Emphasise events, partnerships, craft beer, study tables.
\item \textbf{Rising costs:} Negotiate suppliers; small price adjustment if necessary; keep student offer reasonable.
\item \textbf{Seasonal dip:} Focus on locals and semester Student Passes. Erasmus peaks: Sept, Feb.
\item \textbf{Events underperform:} Change format, timing, or type (e.g. quiz night, live music).
\end{itemize}

\vspace{1em}
