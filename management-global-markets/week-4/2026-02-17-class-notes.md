I. Economy and Politics Are Never the Same
   A. The Reality
      1. Economy and politics always changing
      2. Never stable
   B. When Moving a Company
      1. Can't go everywhere
      2. Some places can do business
      3. Some places can't
      4. Depends on what you produce and export
      5. Politics and geopolitics matter
   C. Why Some Places Don't Work
      1. No regulation or not enough
      2. Not enough safety for company
      3. Some places just empty
      4. Political issues block business
   D. Corporate Decision Making
      1. Companies know exactly where to go
      2. Not a quick decision
      3. Takes years of research

II. Internationalization is Complex
   A. What It Is
      1. Starting complex process
      2. Not simple - complicated
      3. Takes a long time
   B. Reality
      1. Requires years of research
      2. Not quick decisions

III. Economic Complexity - What Is It?
   A. The Basic Idea
      1. Some countries produce complex products
      2. Other countries have no competencies
      3. Countries are different
   B. Where It's Studied
      1. Center in Boston (Harvard Business School / Harvard Growth Lab)
      2. Study product diversity and export complexity
      3. Atlas of Economic Complexity measures economic advancement
   C. Key Principle
      1. "Not enough to produce only one product"
      2. "Produce a lot, all of them very good"
      3. "Otherwise stuck in the middle"

IV. Simple Products vs. Complex Products
   A. The Problem
      1. Countries get stuck in the middle
      2. Not because not exporting
      3. Because exporting simple, not complex products
   B. Simple Products (Low Complexity)
      1. Examples: bananas, coffee, raw materials
      2. Easy to export, easy to sell
      3. Many countries need these
      4. Problem: countries stuck in middle income trap
   C. Complex Products (High Complexity)
      1. Examples: machines, medical machines, cars, electronics, semiconductors
      2. Need: advanced knowledge, skills, technology, factories
      3. Higher value, more competitive
      4. Countries with these have higher economic complexity

V. Why Some Countries Get Stuck
   A. What Helps Countries Succeed
      1. Investment in R&D
      2. Education
      3. Culture
      4. Institutions
      5. Technological adoption
      6. Geographic location
   B. What Hurts Countries
      1. Corruption
      2. Weak legal systems
      3. Poor infrastructure
      4. Extractive model (just taking resources)
   C. Key Insight
      1. Countries that invest in R&D, education, culture, institutions
      2. Tend to escape poverty trap

VI. Country Examples
   A. Taiwan
      1. Special case - high complexity
      2. Complex products, advanced technology
      3. Geopolitically interesting
   B. Netherlands
      1. Very high complexity
      2. Top of complexity index
      3. Very expensive
   C. Ghana (West Africa)
      1. Low complexity
      2. Exports fish
      3. No institutions, no politics, just fish
      4. Stuck with simple products

VII. Diversity Matters Too
   A. Key Point
      1. Complexity not just about what kind
      2. Also how many parts/variations
   B. Example
      1. Producing in 2 colors vs. 3 colors
      2. Not just what product, but how many parts
   C. Reality
      1. Economic complexity based on both diversity and complexity
      2. Need many variations, not just one product

VIII. You Must Export

      1. "If you don't export, useless to make money"
      2. "Need to export to make money"
   B. Why Export Matters

IX. Isolation Problems

      3. Harder to export
   B. Political Isolation
      1. North Korea - extreme isolation
      2. Politics can completely block international business
   C. Key Point

X. Scale Matters - France vs. Brazil
   A. France (Small)
      1. Small country, smaller scale
      2. "Export, produce"
   B. Brazil (Large)

X. 30-Second Cold Call Version.
A. 3-Line Speaking Frame.
   1. TOPIC: STATE THE CORE QUESTION IN ONE LINE.
   2. LOGIC: GIVE 2 DRIVERS/FACTORS THAT EXPLAIN THE ANSWER.
   3. ACTION: END WITH ONE DECISION OR IMPLICATION.
B. Delivery Rule.
   1. 25-30 SECONDS MAX.
   2. NO BACKGROUND STORY; GO STRAIGHT TO CLAIM + EVIDENCE + CLOSE.

MI. Oral Answer Template (60-90 Seconds, Class Use).
A. Opening.
   1. STATE THE CORE QUESTION OR TOPIC IN ONE LINE.
   2. STATE YOUR MAIN CLAIM/THESIS IN ONE LINE.
B. Logic.
   1. GIVE 2-3 KEY FACTORS OR DRIVERS.
   2. LINK EACH FACTOR TO THE CLAIM (CAUSE -> EFFECT).
C. Action Or Implication.
   1. END WITH ONE DECISION, RECOMMENDATION, OR CONSEQUENCE.
   2. CLOSE CLEARLY WITHOUT ADDING NEW IDEAS.
D. Delivery Rule.
   1. 60-90 SECONDS.
   2. CLAIM -> EVIDENCE -> CLOSE.
