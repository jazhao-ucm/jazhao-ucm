Product Management — Weeks 1–6
Exam-Focused Handwriting Notes (detailed; bold high-yield items)

Format: headings, short definitions, exam prompts, quick examples, blanks for answers. Bold the key phrases for handwriting.

---
Week 1 — PM Fundamentals & BMC (Jan 26–27, 2026)
- **Goal:** understand PM role and the Business Model Canvas (BMC).
- **BMC 9 blocks (write and memorize):** Customer Segments; Value Proposition; Channels; Customer Relationships; Revenue Streams; Key Activities; Key Resources; Key Partnerships; Cost Structure.
- **PM role:** balance **business, tech, UX**; align stakeholders; set roadmap.

- Quick exam prompts to practice (handwrite answers):
  - "Define the Value Proposition and give two examples for a subscription service." (Answer: _____)
  - "Fill BMC for a local coffee shop (3 bullets per block)." (Answer: _____)

- High-yield sentence: **Value creation ≠ value capture** — explain with revenue example.

---
Week 2 — Customer Discovery & Roles (Feb 2–3, 2026)
- **Goal:** validate problems before solutions; know PM functions across value chain.
- **Discovery rules:** ask about **past behavior**, not hypotheticals; probe specifics (who, when, cost, feelings).
- **Roles mapping:** Stakeholders: Users, Business Owners, Engineers, Designers, Sales, Support — list one responsibility each.

- Exam task: "Design 5 interview questions to validate a pain point about commuting." (Write them)

---
Week 3 — CSDA & Strategic Thinking (Feb 9–10, 2026)
- **Goal:** learn CSDA mindset — Customer-focused, Strategic, Data-driven, Action-oriented.
- **Application:** map an example feature to CSDA: customer need, strategy alignment, metric, quick experiment.

- Handwrite quick framework: For any feature, list: 1) Customer need 2) Strategic goal 3) Signal metric 4) First experiment

---
Week 4 — Case Study: Zara & BMC Applied (Feb 16–17, 2026)
- **Goal:** apply BMC to a real firm; connect operations to value capture.
- **Key takeaways from Zara case:** fast fashion uses tight supply chain, design-to-shelf speed, and frequent SKUs to drive **turnover**.
- Exam prompt: "How does Zara's supply chain support its Value Proposition? Give 3 points." (Answer box)

---
Week 5 — Design Process & Lean Validation (Feb 23, 2026)
- **Goal:** combine design thinking with lean validation.
- **Design thinking steps (write):** Empathize, Define, Ideate, Prototype, Test.
- **Lean checks:** list riskiest assumptions and cheapest test for each.

- Practice: For a proposed feature, list the riskiest assumption and one cheap way to test it (handwrite).

---
Week 6 — Midterm Prep / Core Frameworks (Mar 2–3, 2026)
- **Goal:** consolidate core frameworks for midterm: BMC, CSDA, Process Design, MVP, RICE/ICE, Funnels, LTV/CAC.
- **Exam tactic (write & use):** Always start answers with the framework name, then apply with evidence, finish with recommendation and metric.

- High-yield quick formulas to memorize and handwrite:
  - **RICE = Reach × Impact × Confidence / Effort**
  - **ICE = Impact × Confidence / Effort**
  - **LTV = (ARPU × Gross Margin) / Churn Rate**
  - **Conversion = Actions / Visitors**

- Midterm checklist (write): define frameworks, one example, one calculation, one recommendation.

Practice prompt: "Take a case about a new product idea. Outline your answer in 5 minutes using a recommended framework." (Answer area)

---
Study & Exam Tips (write on margin):
- Start with the problem statement and the framework.
- Use one calculation or metric when possible (even simple percent changes).
- For memos, keep structure: Exec summary, Options, Trade-offs, Recommendation.
