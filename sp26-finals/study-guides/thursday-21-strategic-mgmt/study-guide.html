Quick exam tips

Master Pattern: "From a [framework] perspective, [force/concept] is [high/low] because [reason], which makes the industry [attractive/unattractive] for [who]; therefore [strategic implication]."
Emergency checklist: opener → choose framework → 2–3 strong points → close with recommendation.

Timing: 5min outline / 10–15min main argument / leave 2–3min conclusion.

Core frameworks (use these first)

Porter's Five Forces

Threat of entrants, supplier power, buyer power, substitutes, rivalry — diagnose industry profit potential.

PESTEL

Macro drivers: Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Environmental, Legal.

VRIO / RBV

Firm resources: Valuable, Rare, Inimitable, Organised. Use to explain why a firm can capture value.

Competitive advantage & VRIO

  • Link resources directly to customer choice (why customers pick you).
  • Give concrete examples (brand, network effects, proprietary tech).
  • When industry unattractive, ask: does the firm have a VRIO resource that offsets it?

Business models & portfolio

Canvas elements and BCG matrix — quick rules: invest in Stars, harvest Cash Cows, decide on Question Marks carefully.

Formulation → Implementation

Use Grant's four: Goals → Environment → Resources (VRIO) → Implementation. Always end with feasibility and implementation risks.

Case practice: TechCore (short)

Key questions: SWOT; industry attractiveness; VRIO diagnosis; recommended growth path (Ansoff); implementation steps.

  1. Write a 2‑sentence opening naming the framework.
  2. Give 3 supporting points (apply master pattern to each).
  3. Finish with a one‑line recommendation and two concrete next steps.

Topic 2: Strategic Analysis (SWOT & Porter)

SWOT Analysis

Internal and external environmental analysis to identify strategic opportunities and threats.

STRENGTHS (Internal) WEAKNESSES (Internal)
• Core competencies
• Strong brand
• Financial resources
• Loyal customers		 • Limited capabilities
• Poor brand perception
• High costs
• Weak distribution
OPPORTUNITIES (External) THREATS (External)
• Market growth
• New technologies
• Emerging markets
• Weak competitors		 • Strong competitors
• Market decline
• Regulatory changes
• Economic downturn

Porter's Five Forces Analysis

Framework for analyzing industry attractiveness and competitive intensity:

Force Definition Strong = Bad for Profit
Threat of New Entrants How easily can new competitors enter? Low barriers to entry = harder to maintain advantage
Bargaining Power of Suppliers How much power do suppliers have? Few suppliers = high input costs
Bargaining Power of Buyers How much power do customers have? Many options available = must compete on price
Threat of Substitutes Do alternative products exist? Many substitutes = less customer loyalty
Rivalry Among Competitors How intense is competitive fighting? High rivalry = pressure on margins
Airline Industry (High Rivalry): Many competitors, low switching costs, commoditized service = low profitability

Topic 3: Competitive Advantage

What is Competitive Advantage?

Unique capabilities or characteristics that allow a company to perform better than competitors and create superior customer value.

Types of Competitive Advantage

1. Cost Leadership

  • Strategy: Lowest cost producer in the industry
  • How: Economies of scale, efficient operations, low-cost suppliers
  • Risk: Price wars, imitation, quality perception
  • Example: Walmart, Southwest Airlines

2. Differentiation

  • Strategy: Unique features/quality that customers value
  • How: Innovation, brand, design, quality, customer service
  • Risk: Price sensitivity, imitation, changing preferences
  • Example: Apple, Mercedes-Benz, Nike

3. Focus/Niche

  • Strategy: Dominate specific market segment
  • How: Deep understanding of target segment, tailored offerings
  • Risk: Segment shrinking, larger competitors entering
  • Example: Luxury brands, specialized software

Sustainable Competitive Advantage (VRIO)

Resources must be:

  • Valuable: Help exploit opportunities or neutralize threats
  • Rare: Not widely available to competitors
  • Inimitable: Difficult to copy (patents, culture, relationships)
  • Organized: Company structure allows exploitation

Topic 4: Business Models

What is a Business Model?

The logic of how a company creates, delivers, and captures value. It shows how the business makes money.

Business Model Canvas

Element Description
Value Proposition What value do we deliver to customers?
Customer Segments Who are our target customers?
Revenue Streams How do we make money?
Key Resources What capabilities/assets do we need?
Key Activities What must we do well?
Cost Structure What are our main costs?

Common Business Models

  • Freemium: Free basic, pay for premium (Spotify)
  • Subscription: Recurring revenue model (Netflix)
  • Marketplace: Connect buyers and sellers (eBay, Airbnb)
  • Platform: Enable others to create value (App Store)
  • Licensing: Sell intellectual property rights (Microsoft)

Topic 5: Strategy Formulation

The Strategic Management Process

  1. Environmental Analysis: SWOT, Porter's Five Forces, PEST
  2. Strategic Objectives: What do we want to achieve?
  3. Strategy Formulation: What strategies achieve objectives?
  4. Implementation: Execute the strategy
  5. Monitoring & Control: Track performance, adjust as needed

Growth Strategies (Ansoff Matrix)

Current Products New Products
Current Markets Market Penetration - sell more to existing customers (lowest risk) Product Development - new products for existing markets
New Markets Market Development - sell existing products to new markets Diversification - new products for new markets (highest risk)

Generic Strategies (Porter)

Overall Cost Leadership: Compete on price, operational efficiency, economies of scale

Differentiation: Compete on unique features, quality, brand, innovation

Focus: Concentrate on specific segment, depth over breadth

Topic 6: Strategy Implementation

Turning Strategy Into Action

Many strategies fail because of poor implementation. Key elements:

1. Organizational Structure

  • Functional structure - by function (Marketing, HR, Operations)
  • Divisional structure - by product or geography
  • Matrix structure - combines multiple structures

2. Resource Allocation

  • Budget capital for strategic initiatives
  • Invest in capabilities needed
  • Hire/reassign talent

3. Performance Metrics (Balanced Scorecard)

Perspective Questions Examples
Financial Are we making money? Revenue growth, ROI, profit margin
Customer What do customers think? Satisfaction, retention, market share
Internal Processes What processes matter? Quality, efficiency, innovation
Learning & Growth Are we improving? Employee skills, culture, systems

4. Change Management

  • Communicate the vision and why change is needed
  • Build coalitions of support
  • Remove barriers to implementation
  • Celebrate quick wins

Topic 7: Corporate & Portfolio Strategy

Corporate Strategy Decisions

1. Vertical Integration

  • Forward: Control distribution/customers
  • Backward: Control suppliers
  • Benefit: Control value chain, reduce costs, ensure quality
  • Risk: Capital intensive, less flexibility

2. Horizontal Integration (Mergers & Acquisitions)

  • Acquire competitors to gain market share
  • Eliminate competition
  • Achieve economies of scale
  • Risk: Cultural clashes, overpaying, integration challenges

3. Related vs Unrelated Diversification

  • Related: New businesses related to core competencies (lower risk)
  • Unrelated: Completely different businesses (higher risk, synergies unclear)

Portfolio Management (BCG Matrix)

Category Market Growth Market Share Strategy
Stars ⭐ High High Invest to maintain leadership
Cash Cows 🐄 Low High Harvest profits with minimal investment
Question Marks ❓ High Low Invest selectively or divest
Dogs 🐶 Low Low Divest or maintain for cash

📋 Case Study: TechCore - Strategic Pivot

Scenario

TechCore is a 15-year-old software company that built its reputation on enterprise data management software. However, cloud computing and AI are disrupting their market. Revenue has stalled at $200M, and they're losing customers to newer competitors.

Strategic Questions

  1. What are TechCore's current strengths and weaknesses? (SWOT)
  2. Is the industry attractive? (Porter's Five Forces)
  3. What competitive advantages can TechCore build? (VRIO)
  4. What growth strategy should they pursue? (Ansoff)
  5. Should they diversify, focus, or pivot? Why?
  6. What business model changes are needed?

Possible Strategic Options

  • Option A: Keep focus on legacy products, serve shrinking market profitably
  • Option B: Pivot to AI-powered solutions, rebuild product line
  • Option C: Acquire an AI startup to fast-track capability
  • Option D: Move to cloud-based subscription model with existing tech

📝 Key Terms

Competitive Advantage: Unique capability that allows superior performance relative to competitors
Synergy: When combined value of two entities exceeds sum of parts (2+2=5)
Core Competency: Unique strength that differentiates a company from competitors
Market Segmentation: Dividing market into distinct groups with different needs
Strategic Fit: How well new initiatives align with existing strategy and capabilities
Value Chain: All activities involved in creating and delivering products to customers
Mission: Organization's purpose and why it exists
Vision: Where the organization aspires to be in future
