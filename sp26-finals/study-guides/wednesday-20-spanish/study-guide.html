    Overview & study strategy

    Goals

    • Comprehend authentic short texts and conversations on everyday topics.
    • Produce coherent short spoken descriptions, narrations, and emails.
    • Use multiple past and present forms with controlled accuracy.

    Weekly routine (practical)

    1. Day 1: Intensive listening (10–20m) + note-taking.
    2. Day 2: Grammar drill + guided writing (20–30m).
    3. Day 3: Vocabulary expansion + SRS flashcards (15m).
    4. Day 4: Speaking practice (roleplay / timed monologue, 10m).
    5. Day 5: Reading authentic short article + comprehension questions.
    6. Day 6: Mixed review: quick tests for weak points.
    7. Day 7: Free production: write or record a 2–3 minute summary.

    Advanced grammar summary (practical reference)

    1. Verbs — present, past, future, and mood

    Focus on meaning and form: when a tense is required, what it conveys, and typical time markers.

    • Present simple — facts, habitual actions. Markers: siempre, normalmente.
    • Pretérito perfecto — recent past with relevance to present. Markers: hoy, ya, todavía no.
    • Pretérito indefinido — completed actions with specific time. Markers: ayer, el año pasado.
    • Imperfecto — background descriptions, repeated past actions. Markers: cuando era niño, todos los días.
    • Futuro simple and ir + a + infinitivo — predictions vs. near future.
    • Subjuntivo (present) — expressions of desire, doubt, recommendation: quiero que, es posible que. Practice set sentences until forms become automatic.

    2. Essential contrasts

    • Ser vs. Estar: identity/characteristic vs. state/location. Practice minimal pairs: es aburrido / está aburrido.
    • Por vs. Para: cause/mode vs. purpose/recipient.
    • Pretérito perfecto vs. indefinido: recent/indefinite distinctions — make timelines when practicing.

    3. Pronouns & clitics

    Learn placement rules for direct/indirect object pronouns in affirmative, negative, and imperative sentences. Drill: replace nouns with pronouns until sentences sound natural.

    4. Useful grammar hacks

    • Use signal words to decide tense quickly: create a one-page cheat-sheet with markers and sample sentences.
    • Transformations: rewrite 10 short sentences from present → imperfect → indefinido to feel contrasts.

    Pronunciation & listening

    Key pronunciation features to master

    • Vowel clarity: Spanish has five stable vowels /a e i o u/ — keep them pure.
    • Tap vs trill: single r vs rr — practice minimal pairs (pero vs perro).
    • Stress and accents: learn rules for default stress and typical exceptions; mark stress when reading aloud.
    • Connected speech: practice linking words — native speed uses reductions and links.

    Listening practice (progression)

    1. Slow graded audio (comprehension + transcript matching).
    2. Dictation of short sentences (awareness of sounds & punctuation).
    3. Authentic clips (news headlines, short interviews). Listen twice: gist, then details.

    High-value vocabulary & collocations

    Prioritize words by frequency and usefulness. Learn collocations (verb + noun, adjective + noun) rather than isolated words.

    Thematic lists (examples)

    • Daily life: despertarse, ducharse, desayunar, salir.
    • Travel & directions: girar, seguir recto, cruzar, la rotonda.
    • Food verbs & collocations: pedir la carta, poner la mesa, traer la cuenta.

    Vocabulary techniques

    • Create sentences for each new word (3 contextual sentences).
    • Use spaced repetition (Anki or Quizlet) with sample collocations.
    • Build short word families: cocinar, la cocina, cocido.

    Productive skills: speaking & writing

    Speaking — scaffolded fluency tasks

    • 1-minute daily monologue: topic + 3 facts + opinion.
    • Roleplays: order in a restaurant, ask for directions, interview a classmate.
    • Fluency drill: 60-second descriptions with a single filler phrase allowed (e.g., o sea).

    Writing — structure templates

    Use short, reusable templates for common tasks:

    Correo informal: saludo — motivo — 3 ideas — despedida
Ej: Hola Marta, Te escribo porque... + cierro con un deseo.

    Practice exercises (with model answers)

    Exercise 1 — Ser / Estar

    Rewrite with the correct verb: (a) Madrid ___ en España. (b) Ana ___ cansada hoy.

    Answers: (a) está; (b) está

    Exercise 2 — Por vs Para

    Choose: Trabajo ___ ganar dinero. Viajo ___ conocer otras culturas.

    Answers: Trabajo para ganar dinero. Viajo para conocer otras culturas. (Use por for reason/cause contexts like ‘por la lluvia’.)

    Exercise 3 — Past contrast (perfect vs indefinido)

    Complete: Este mes yo ___ (visitar) tres ciudades. Ayer ___ (visitar) Sevilla.

    Answers: Este mes yo he visitado tres ciudades. Ayer visité Sevilla.

    Study plans & self-assessment

    8-week accelerated plan

    1. Weeks 1–2: consolidate Units 0–3 (focus: listening + present/simple past).
    2. Weeks 3–4: deepen Units 4–6 (focus: description, routines, object pronouns).
    3. Weeks 5–6: practice Units 7–9 (focus: interactions, past forms, abilities).
    4. Weeks 7–8: synthesis — produce an oral presentation and a written project.

    Self-assessment checklist (do this weekly)

    • Can I describe my day in 8–10 sentences without long pauses?
    • Can I ask and answer 8 basic information questions fluently?
    • Can I use past tenses correctly in short stories?
