## 3. KEY FRAMEWORKS & CONCEPTS (5 High-Yield)

### **1. BUSINESS MODEL CANVAS (BMC) – CORE FRAMEWORK**
**Files:** [bmc-value-creation.tex](week-1/bmc-value-creation.tex), [zara-bmc.tex](week-4/zara-bmc.tex)  
**Weeks:** W1, W4  
**Summary (75 words):**  
The Business Model Canvas is a one-page visual framework with 9 interconnected blocks: (1) Customer Segments—who you serve; (2) Value Proposition—what unique value you offer; (3) Channels—how you reach customers; (4) Customer Relationships—how you build loyalty; (5) Revenue Streams—how you make money; (6) Key Activities—critical tasks; (7) Key Resources—essential assets; (8) Key Partnerships—external collaborators; (9) Cost Structure—main expenses. Used for startups and incumbents to map business model logic, test assumptions, and identify innovation opportunities.

### **2. SIX DIMENSIONS OF PROCESS DESIGN – MANUFACTURING & OPERATIONS**
**Files:** [piano-process-design-memo.md](week-7/piano-process-design-memo.md)  
**Week:** W7  
**Summary (85 words):**  
A strategic framework for aligning manufacturing operations with business goals. Six trade-off dimensions: (1) **Lot Size**—small batch (differentiation) vs. large batch (economies of scale); (2) **Equipment**—general-purpose (flexible) vs. specific-purpose (dedicated); (3) **Skills**—artisan expertise vs. standardized labor; (4) **Scheduling**—make-to-order (flexibility) vs. make-to-stock (speed); (5) **Product Range**—high variety vs. limited standard models; (6) **Inventory**—low finished goods vs. high flow. Key insight: choices must align; cannot optimize all dimensions simultaneously. Real examples: Spanish craftsman pianos vs. Yamaha factory assembly lines.

### **3. CSDA FRAMEWORK – PRODUCT MANAGEMENT PHILOSOPHY**
**Files:** [pm3-csda.tex](week-3/pm3-csda.tex), [README.md](README.md)  
**Week:** W3  
**Summary (80 words):**  
CSDA (Customer-focused, Strategic, Data-driven, Action-oriented) is the core PM mindset. **(C)** Customer-focused: understand jobs-to-be-done and customer pain before prescribing solutions. **(S)** Strategic: align product to corporate goals and competitive position. **(D)** Data-driven: validate assumptions with evidence (interviews, experiments, metrics), not intuition. **(A)** Action-oriented: move fast, prototype, iterate, and ship. The course reinforces that great products require both creation (solving real problems) AND capture (market access, pricing, positioning).

---

### **4. DESIGN THINKING & LEAN VALIDATION – DISCOVERY TO MVP**
**Files:** [design-process-lean.tex](week-5/design-process-lean.tex), [loveable-mvp-helper.tex](week-9/loveable-mvp-helper.tex)  
**Weeks:** W5, W9  
**Summary (95 words):**  
Design Thinking (IDEO framework) combines empathy, rapid prototyping, and iteration. Phases: (1) **Empathize**—observe users in context, listen without judgment; (2) **Define**—articulate the core problem; (3) **Ideate**—generate many solutions (defer judgment, build on ideas); (4) **Prototype**—create low-fidelity artifacts (sketches, role-play, post-its); (5) **Test**—get user feedback, refine. **Lean Validation** pairs this with minimal spend: test assumptions cheaply before major investment. **MVP** (Minimum Viable Product) = smallest artifact testing one hypothesis. For social apps: unfiltered Q&A, gamification, and social buffering reduce friction and build psychological safety.

### **5. CUSTOMER DISCOVERY & INTERVIEW SYNTHESIS – RESEARCH METHODOLOGY**
**Files:** [customer-interviews-synthesis.tex](week-8/customer-interviews-synthesis.tex), [2026-03-17-class-notes.md](week-8/2026-03-17-class-notes.md)  
**Week:** W8  
**Summary (90 words):**  
Structured customer interviews extract deep insights without leading questions. **Methodology:** (1) Ask about past behavior (not hypotheticals—"How did you last meet someone?" vs. "Would you use an app?"); (2) Use emotion and vibe prompts ("Did it feel natural? Forced?"); (3) Probe for honest signals ("Are you being real or just small-talking?"); (4) **Synthesize patterns** across interviews to identify jobs-to-be-done. Example: 4 users (Karol, Aina, Laura, Mimi) revealed that structured ice-breakers, authenticity, and low-pressure social settings drive meaningful connections—informing the app's Unfiltered Q&A and social-buffering MVP features.

## 4. ADDITIONAL KEY TERMS & CONCEPTS

**(From Week 14 Vocabulary A–Z glossary – [product-management-vocabulary-a-z-mon-tue-spring2026.md](week-14/product-management-vocabulary-a-z-mon-tue-spring2026.md))**

| Concept | Definition |
|---------|-----------|
| **Value Creation vs. Capture** | Creating value = increasing usefulness for customers; capturing value = converting created value into revenue and profit. Not the same as headline valuation. Core course insight. |
| **Porter Value Chain** | Linked primary (inbound logistics, operations, sales, service) and support activities (HR, tech, procurement) that differentiate offers. |
| **Disruptive Innovation** | Entrants with fewer resources win via new value networks or "good enough" offers until incumbents are displaced. |
| **Jobs to Be Done (JTBD)** | Underlying goal the user hires a product to accomplish (e.g., "meet chill people authentically" vs. "swipe profiles"). |
| **North Star Metric** | Single leading indicator of long-run value delivery (e.g., weekly active engaged users, retention). |
| **MVP (Minimum Viable Product)** | Smallest artifact testing one specific learning hypothesis, not a full feature dump. |
| **Product-Market Fit** | Condition where a product strongly satisfies market demand; indicates scalability potential. |
| **Lean Validation** | Fast, low-cost testing before major investment; "testing as investing." |
| **Gamification** | Game-like elements (challenges, icebreakers, progress badges) applied to non-game experiences. |
| **Social Buffering** | Letting users attend/join with a friend or +1 to reduce social anxiety and friction. |
| **Economies of Scale** | Per-unit cost falls as volume rises because fixed costs spread over more units. |
| **Make-to-Order vs. Make-to-Stock** | MTO (production starts after order; flexibility) vs. MTS (production to forecast; availability). |
| **Churn** | Percentage of users/customers who stop using or paying for a product. |
| **Network Effect** | Product becomes more valuable as more participants join (platforms, ecosystems). |
