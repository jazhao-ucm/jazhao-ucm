# International Business Simulation: Market Entry – Study Notes

## Overview
- You manage the international strategy and operations of a casual dining restaurant chain for 6 years.
- Responsible for all business outside your home country.
- Each year: review country info and news, then decide:
  - Which countries to enter, grow, or exit
  - Entry modes (franchise, joint venture, wholly owned, etc.)
  - Local marketing decisions

---

## Key Concepts

### 1. Globalization, Glocalization, Slowbalization
- **Globalization:** Free movement of goods, services, capital; interconnected economies.
- **Glocalization:** Adapting global products/services for local markets.
- **Slowbalization:** Slowing of globalization, rise of protectionism, more localization.

### 2. Market Attractiveness Factors
- **GDP per Capita:** Income level; higher = more purchasing power.
- **GDP Growth:** Indicates market growth potential.
- **Population Growth:** Expanding customer base.

### 3. Market Characteristics
- **Country Risk:** Economic/political instability, general risk.
- **Trade Barriers (Tariffs):** Increase import costs, affect margins.
- **Industry Rivalry:** Intensity of competition in the market.

### 4. Culture & Cultural Distance
- **Culture:** Shared beliefs, values, norms distinguishing groups.
- **Hofstede’s Six Dimensions:**
  1. Power Distance
  2. Individualism
  3. Masculinity vs. Femininity
  4. Uncertainty Avoidance
  5. Long-Term Orientation
  6. Indulgence
- **Cultural Distance:** Sum of differences in Hofstede scores between countries.
- **High Indulgence Score:** More attractive for restaurant market.

### 5. News & Crises
- News and crises can impact country attractiveness and optimal strategies.
- Crises are unpredictable; always consider risk in decision-making.

---

## Simulation Tips
- Analyze all country data before making decisions.
- Balance market attractiveness (GDP, growth, population) with risk (instability, tariffs, rivalry).
- Consider cultural distance for adaptation and entry mode.
- Monitor news and be ready to adapt to crises.
- Choose entry modes based on risk, control, and local conditions.
