# Product Management Class Notes
## March 24, 2026 Week 9

**Topic:** Service business internationalization; service operations constraints; market-entry analysis; organizational setup for service delivery.

---

I. Service Internationalization Focus.
   A. Core Question.
      1. HOW TO EXPAND SERVICE BUSINESS ACROSS MARKETS.
      2. HOW TO ADAPT WITHOUT LOSING SERVICE CONSISTENCY.
   B. Why Services Differ From Products.
      1. SERVICE DEPENDS ON PEOPLE, PROCESS, AND CONTEXT.
      2. DELIVERY VARIABILITY ACROSS CITIES/COUNTRIES IS HIGH.

II. Main Service Expansion Challenges.
   A. Intangibility And Standardization Limits.
      1. QUALITY HARDER TO CONTROL THAN PHYSICAL GOODS.
      2. CUSTOMER EXPECTATIONS DIFFER BY MARKET.
   B. Capacity And Timing.
      1. SERVICE CAPACITY MUST MATCH DEMAND WINDOW.
      2. DELAYS DIRECTLY IMPACT CUSTOMER EXPERIENCE.
   C. Localization Pressure.
      1. LOCAL NORMS/PREFERENCES AFFECT DELIVERY MODEL.
      2. ONE GLOBAL TEMPLATE OFTEN FAILS WITHOUT ADAPTATION.

III. Market And Customer Analysis Logic.
   A. Segment Differences.
      1. DIFFERENT CUSTOMER GROUPS HAVE DIFFERENT EXPECTATIONS.
      2. VALUE PROPOSITION MUST FIT EACH SEGMENT CONTEXT.
   B. Data-Driven Entry Screening.
      1. USE LOCAL DEMAND + COST + OPERABILITY SIGNALS.
      2. TEST ASSUMPTIONS BEFORE FULL-SCALE ROLLOUT.
   C. Process Mapping.
      1. DEFINE FRONTSTAGE (CUSTOMER-FACING) VS BACKSTAGE OPERATIONS.
      2. IDENTIFY WHERE SERVICE FAILURES MOST LIKELY OCCUR.

IV. Organizational Design For Service Scale.
   A. Structural Choice.
      1. CENTRALIZED CONTROL VS LOCAL AUTONOMY TRADEOFF.
      2. CLARIFY WHICH DECISIONS STAY HQ VS LOCAL TEAM.
   B. Capability Building.
      1. STAFF TRAINING + SOPs FOR SERVICE CONSISTENCY.
      2. METRICS FOR QUALITY, RESPONSE TIME, AND CUSTOMER OUTCOME.
   C. Delivery Coordination.
      1. ALIGN COMMERCIAL, OPERATIONS, AND SUPPORT FUNCTIONS.
      2. ENSURE HANDOFFS DO NOT BREAK CUSTOMER EXPERIENCE.

V. Execution Sequence (Practical Flow).
   A. Stage 1.
      1. SELECT TARGET MARKET + CUSTOMER SEGMENT.
      2. DEFINE MINIMUM SERVICE MODEL.
   B. Stage 2.
      1. PILOT IN LIMITED SCOPE.
      2. MEASURE QUALITY GAPS + UNIT ECONOMICS.
   C. Stage 3.
      1. ADAPT PROCESS BASED ON PILOT LEARNING.
      2. SCALE WITH GOVERNANCE + KPI REVIEW.

      2. EMPHASIS ON MARKET FIT + PROCESS + ORGANIZATION.
   B. Low Confidence (Noisy Audio).
