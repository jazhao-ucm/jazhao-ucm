# Product Management Class Notes
## March 3, 2026 Week 6

**Topic:** Design thinking in practice (IDEO shopping cart); limits of design thinking; customer interview design.

---

I. Design Thinking in Practice IDEO Shopping Cart
   A. Brainstorming rules
      1. One conversation at a time
      2. Defer judgment no criticism during ideation
      3. Encourage wild ideas
      4. Build on others' ideas ("yes, and...")
      5. Go for quantity first, quality later
   B. Why rules matter
      1. Separate idea generation from evaluation
      2. Protect fragile early ideas
      3. Create psychological safety
   C. Process
      1. Dozens of ideas on sticky notes
      2. Cluster and filter after free-flow
      3. Select promising ideas for prototyping

II. Prototyping
   A. Purpose
      1. Quick, rough version to test idea
      2. Learn before investing heavily
      3. Fail fast with cheap materials
   B. IDEO approach
      1. Build multiple prototypes quickly
      2. Test in real environment (supermarket)
      3. Iterate based on feedback
   C. Key insight
      1. Prototype is not final product
      2. Goal is learning, not perfection
      3. Better to test wrong idea cheaply

III. Creative Office Environment
   A. Physical space
      1. Flexible spaces, wheels on furniture
      2. Playful details encourage creativity
      3. Walls for posting ideas
   B. Culture
      1. "Ask forgiveness, not permission"
      2. Process is serious, vibe is fun
      3. Encourage experimentation

IV. Limits of Design Thinking
   A. Core limitation
      1. Great design cannot save weak business model
      2. Process is necessary, not sufficient
      3. Still need strategy, pricing, channels
   B. Example folding bike company
      1. Good product design
      2. Bad market fit
      3. Company failed despite good design
   C. Key insight
      1. Design thinking is tool, not magic
      2. Must combine with business fundamentals
      3. Product quality ≠ business success

V. Customer Interview Design
   A. Good questions focus on real stories
      1. "Tell me about the last time..."
      2. "What happened when..."
      3. "Walk me through how you..."
   B. Bad questions to avoid
      1. "What features do you want?"
      2. "Imagine an app that..."
      3. Yes/no questions
      4. Leading questions
   C. Key insight
      1. Ask about behavior, not preferences
      2. Past events, not hypotheticals
      3. Specific situations, not generalities

VI. Interview Guide Structure
   A. Length
      1. 10 to 15 questions sufficient
      2. Too many = overwhelming
      3. Too few = not enough depth
   B. Flow
      1. Start with context (who are you, what do you do)
      2. Move to specific situations (last time you...)
      3. End with feelings and reflections
   C. Consistency
      1. Use same script across team
      2. Enables comparison of answers
      3. Reduces interviewer bias

VII. Behavioral Questions
   A. Definition
      1. Ask about real past events
      2. Not dreams or wishes
      3. Not hypothetical scenarios
   B. Examples
      1. "Tell me about a time when..."
      2. "What did you do when..."
      3. "How did you handle..."
   C. Why behavioral works
      1. Past behavior predicts future
      2. Harder to make up stories
      3. Reveals actual experience

VIII. Common Interview Mistakes
   A. Leading questions
      1. "Don't you think X would be better?"
      2. Suggests the answer
      3. Biases response
   B. Hypotheticals
      1. "What would you do if..."
      2. People imagine idealized behavior
      3. Not reliable predictor
   C. Feature requests
      1. "What features do you want?"
      2. Users are not designers
      3. Focuses on solutions, not problems

X. 30-Second Cold Call Version.
A. 3-Line Speaking Frame.
   1. TOPIC: STATE THE CORE QUESTION IN ONE LINE.
   2. LOGIC: GIVE 2 DRIVERS/FACTORS THAT EXPLAIN THE ANSWER.
   3. ACTION: END WITH ONE DECISION OR IMPLICATION.
B. Delivery Rule.
   1. 25-30 SECONDS MAX.
   2. NO BACKGROUND STORY; GO STRAIGHT TO CLAIM + EVIDENCE + CLOSE.

CI. Oral Answer Template (60-90 Seconds, Class Use).
A. Opening.
   1. STATE THE CORE QUESTION OR TOPIC IN ONE LINE.
   2. STATE YOUR MAIN CLAIM/THESIS IN ONE LINE.
B. Logic.
   1. GIVE 2-3 KEY FACTORS OR DRIVERS.
   2. LINK EACH FACTOR TO THE CLAIM (CAUSE -> EFFECT).
C. Action Or Implication.
   1. END WITH ONE DECISION, RECOMMENDATION, OR CONSEQUENCE.
   2. CLOSE CLEARLY WITHOUT ADDING NEW IDEAS.
D. Delivery Rule.
   1. 60-90 SECONDS.
   2. CLAIM -> EVIDENCE -> CLOSE.
