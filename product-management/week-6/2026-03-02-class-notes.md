# Product Management — Class Notes
## March 2, 2026 — Week 6

**Topic:** Design thinking; human-centered design; IDEO process; observation vs. asking; extreme users.

---

I. Design Thinking — Overview
   A. Definition
      1. Process to solve problems by understanding users first
      2. Human-centered approach
      3. Iterative and creative
   B. Five stages
      1. Empathize — understand the user
      2. Define — find the real problem
      3. Ideate — generate ideas
      4. Prototype — build something to test
      5. Test — learn from real use
   C. Key insight
      1. Not linear process
      2. Cycle back as needed
      3. User insight drives design

II. Don't Ask Users What They Want
   A. Key principle (Jobs/Ford)
      1. "People don't know what they want until you show them"
      2. Users can't articulate future needs
      3. They describe current limitations
   B. Why asking fails
      1. Users are not designers
      2. They think in current solutions
      3. Social desirability bias
   C. Better approach
      1. Ask about behavior, not preferences
      2. Observe what they do
      3. Identify pain points, not solutions

III. Observation Over Asking
   A. Core principle
      1. Watch what people do, not what they say
      2. Actions reveal true needs
      3. Words can mislead
   B. Gemba (go to the source)
      1. Go where work happens
      2. Observe in real context
      3. Don't rely on reports
   C. Two observers
      1. Avoid single-person bias
      2. Different perspectives catch more
      3. Compare notes afterward

IV. Extreme Users
   A. Definition
      1. People who use product intensely
      2. Often reveal problems others miss
      3. Edge cases expose design flaws
   B. IDEO shopping cart example
      1. 22,000 kids hospitalized yearly from cart injuries
      2. Extreme users revealed safety issues
      3. Led to redesigned cart
   C. Why extreme users matter
      1. Average users adapt to problems
      2. Extreme users feel pain more acutely
      3. Solutions for extreme often help average

V. IDEO Process
   A. Diverse teams
      1. Engineers, psychologists, linguists, biologists
      2. Different perspectives = better solutions
      3. Not just one type of expert
   B. Rapid prototyping
      1. Build quickly to test ideas
      2. Fail fast with cheap prototypes
      3. Learn before investing
   C. Brainstorming rules
      1. Defer judgment
      2. Build on others' ideas
      3. Encourage wild ideas
      4. Go for quantity

VI. Empathy in Design
   A. Definition
      1. Understanding what users feel and do
      2. Not what they say they want
      3. Walking in their shoes
   B. Methods
      1. Observation
      2. Immersion (try it yourself)
      3. Interviews (about experience, not solutions)
   C. Key insight
      1. Empathy reveals latent needs
      2. Users can't always express needs
      3. Designer must understand context

VII. Corporate Culture Trap
   A. The problem
      1. Bosses reward people at desks
      2. Visible work valued over field work
      3. Real insight comes from customers
   B. Better approach
      1. Best people are in the field
      2. Talking to customers is work
      3. Observation takes time
   C. Key insight
      1. Desk work feels productive
      2. Customer work is productive
      3. Culture must support field work

VIII. Problem Definition
   A. Key principle
      1. Right problem definition = half the solution
      2. Most fail by solving wrong problem
      3. Spend time on definition
   B. How to define well
      1. Ask "why" five times
      2. Reframe the problem
      3. Question assumptions
   C. Example
      1. "Design a better chair" vs.
      2. "Help people work comfortably for long periods"
      3. Second definition opens more solutions
