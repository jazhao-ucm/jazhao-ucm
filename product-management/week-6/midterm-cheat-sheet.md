## Product Management – Midterm Cheat Sheet

**How to use:** Use for quick recall. In exam: define terms, give one example, tie to value creation/capture or product success.

---

### Unit 1 – Value Creation vs. Value Capture

- **Value creation** = Making something useful. Total value for users.
- **Value capture** = How much money you make from it. You need both.
- **Market access** = Can you reach customers? Distribution. Channels. Teece: market access → value capture.
- **Porter value chain**: Primary (production, delivery) + support (HR, tech). Value = resources + activities → users.
- **Takeda case**: Spanish research €15K → Takeda €250M/year. Why? Better market access, distribution, scale.
- **Value capture mechanisms**: Patents, branding, market access, co-specialized assets.
- **European challenge**: Smaller market reach limits value capture vs. global players.

---

### Unit 2 – Business Model Canvas & Product Design

- **BMC**: Customer segments, value proposition, channels, revenue streams, key resources, key activities, cost structure.
- **Product design process**: Problem discovery → customer discovery → solution validation → build/launch.
- **Lean validation**: Don't spend money until validated. Test. Learn. Iterate.
- **Problem–solution fit first**: Understand the problem before building.
- **Customer discovery**: Go to market. Talk to customers. Don't assume.

---

### Unit 3 – Exploration vs. Exploitation

- **Exploration (sensing)** = Finding new opportunities. Searching. Discovering.
- **Exploitation (seizing)** = Doing what works. Scaling. Capturing value.
- **Ambidextrous organization**: Need both. Exploration finds; exploitation captures.
- **Microsoft example**: Strong in exploitation, weaker in exploration.
- **SDLC** = Software Development Life Cycle: Plan → Build → Test → Deploy. Iterate.

---

### Unit 4 – Design Thinking & User Research

- **Design thinking**: Empathize → Define → Ideate → Prototype → Test. Iterate.
- **Don't ask users what they want**: Steve Jobs / Henry Ford—"People don't know what they want until you show them."
- **Observe behavior**: Watch what they do. Ask about problems, past experience. Not solutions.
- **Gemba** = Go where the work happens. Observe in real context.
- **Two observers rule**: Send two people. Reduces bias. Richer insight.
- **Extreme users**: People who use product intensely. Reveal problems average user doesn't. IDEO shopping cart: 22,000 kids/year injured.
- **Diverse teams**: IDEO—engineers, psychologists, linguists, biologists. Different perspectives = better solutions.
- **Corporate culture trap**: Bosses reward desk time. Best insight comes from the field.

---

### Unit 5 – Innovation & Technology

- **S-curve**: Technology performance over time. Slow start → rapid growth → plateau → decline.
- **Disruptive innovation**: New tech starts lower, cheaper. Improves. Overtakes old tech.
- **Innovator's dilemma**: Focus on current customers. Miss the new thing. Old tech still profitable.
- **Kodak case**: Dominant in film. Failed to adapt to digital. Canon, Sony, Nikon won.
- **iPhone**: Apple didn't invent smartphone; revolutionized it. Touchscreen, size, ease of use.
- **First mover advantage**: Being first can capture resources, market position. Or disadvantage if too early.
- **Radical innovation**: Often comes from outside established companies.
- **Uber**: Created new market. Disrupted taxi industry.

### Common Question Angles

- **"Why might a firm create value but not capture it?"** Market access, distribution, patents, branding, scale. Takeda vs. Spanish research.
- **"Apply design thinking to [product]."** Empathize (observe, don't ask) → define problem → ideate → prototype → test.
- **"Exploration vs. exploitation—why both?"** Exploration finds opportunities; exploitation captures value. Ambidextrous org.
- **"Why did Kodak fail?"** Innovator's dilemma; invested in film; didn't adapt to digital; competitors (Canon, Sony, Nikon) did.
