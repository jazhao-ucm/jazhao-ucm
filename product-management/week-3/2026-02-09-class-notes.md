# Product Management Class Notes
## February 9, 2026 Week 3

**Topic:** Production strategies; cost vs. price; fixed and variable costs; economies of scale.

---

I. Cost vs. Price
   A. Key distinction
      1. Cost = what you spend to produce
      2. Price = what you charge customers
      3. Low cost ≠ low price
   B. Strategic choice
      1. Low cost can mean higher margins at same price
      2. Or lower price to gain market share
      3. Depends on competitive strategy

II. Fixed vs. Variable Costs
   A. Fixed costs
      1. Don't change with production volume
      2. Ex: building, insurance, equipment, salaries
      3. Must pay even if producing nothing
   B. Variable costs
      1. Change with production volume
      2. Ex: raw materials, direct labor, packaging
      3. More production = more cost
   C. Key insight
      1. High fixed costs require high volume
      2. Spread fixed costs over more units = lower unit cost

III. Economies of Scale
   A. Definition
      1. Bigger production = cheaper per unit
      2. Fixed costs spread over more units
      3. Bulk purchasing discounts
   B. Sources
      1. Spreading fixed costs
      2. Specialization of labor
      3. Better bargaining power with suppliers
      4. Learning effects
   C. Limitations
      1. Diseconomies at very large scale
      2. Coordination becomes difficult
      3. Flexibility decreases

IV. Mass Production
   A. Requirements
      1. High volume
      2. Standardization
      3. Specialization
      4. Efficient processes
   B. Benefits
      1. Lower unit costs
      2. Consistent quality
      3. Predictable output
   C. Trade-offs
      1. Less flexibility
      2. Less customization
      3. High upfront investment

V. Competing on Low Cost
   A. Strategy
      1. Cut costs everywhere possible
      2. Achieve economies of scale
      3. Optimize processes
   B. Requirements
      1. High volume
      2. Efficient operations
      3. Low overhead
   C. Risks
      1. Race to bottom on price
      2. Quality may suffer
      3. Margins can be thin

VI. Alternative Strategy Differentiation
   A. Definition
      1. Compete on uniqueness, not price
      2. Premium pricing for premium features
      3. Less price-sensitive customers
   B. Examples
      1. Apple premium tech
      2. Mercedes luxury cars
      3. Starbucks premium coffee
   C. Trade-offs
      1. Smaller market
      2. Higher margins
      3. Different capabilities needed

VII. Cost Structure Analysis
   A. Components
      1. Direct materials
      2. Direct labor
      3. Manufacturing overhead
      4. Administrative costs
   B. Analysis
      1. Which costs are fixed vs. variable?
      2. Where can costs be reduced?
      3. What is the break-even volume?
   C. Strategic implications
      1. High fixed costs = need for volume
      2. High variable costs = focus on efficiency
      3. Mix affects pricing flexibility

VIII. Break-Even Analysis
   A. Definition
      1. Volume where revenue = total costs
      2. No profit, no loss
      3. Below = loss; Above = profit
   B. Formula
      1. Break-even units = Fixed costs ÷ (Price - Variable cost per unit)
      2. Contribution margin = Price - Variable cost
   C. Use
      1. Pricing decisions
      2. Volume targets
      3. Investment decisions
