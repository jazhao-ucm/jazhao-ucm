# Product Management Class Notes
## February 10, 2026 Week 3

**Topic:** Car industry efficiency; market share; McDonald's case; speed vs. customization trade-off.

---

I. Car Industry Efficiency
   A. Market context
      1. Many brands competing (Lexus, Mercedes, BMW, etc.)
      2. Efficiency as competitive advantage
      3. Market share comparisons
   B. Brand ownership
      1. Some brands owned by parent companies
      2. Ex: Lexus = Toyota luxury division
      3. Affects market share interpretation
   C. Key insight
      1. Look at parent company performance
      2. Not just individual brand performance

II. Case Study McDonald's Production Systems
   A. Original system (forecast-based)
      1. Predict demand based on patterns
      2. Pre-prepare food before customers arrive
      3. Peak periods: breakfast, lunch
      4. Standardized menu items
   B. Problems with old system
      1. Food waste from over-preparation
      2. Cold food if demand lower than forecast
      3. Limited menu flexibility
   C. New system ("Made for You")
      1. Produce when ordered
      2. Customization possible
      3. Less waste
      4. Fresher food

III. Forecast-Based vs. Responsive Production
   A. Forecast-based
      1. Predict demand in advance
      2. Produce before order
      3. Risk of waste or stockouts
      4. Fast delivery
   B. Responsive (make-to-order)
      1. Produce when ordered
      2. No forecast needed
      3. No waste from overproduction
      4. Slower delivery
   C. Trade-off
      1. Speed vs. flexibility
      2. Cost vs. customization
      3. Efficiency vs. responsiveness

IV. Speed vs. Customization Trade-Off
   A. Fast/standardized
      1. Limited options
      2. Quick delivery
      3. Lower cost per unit
      4. Economies of scale
   B. Slow/customized
      1. Many options
      2. Longer wait time
      3. Higher cost per unit
      4. Premium pricing possible
   C. Strategic choice
      1. Depends on target customer
      2. Some want speed, others want choice
      3. Different markets = different strategies

V. Business Model Differences
   A. Key insight
      1. No one-size-fits-all strategy
      2. Different companies = different approaches
      3. Market positioning matters
   B. Fast food examples
      1. McDonald's balance of speed and customization
      2. Subway high customization, slower
      3. Chick-fil-A quality focus
   C. Car industry examples
      1. Toyota efficiency and reliability
      2. BMW performance and luxury
      3. Tesla innovation and technology

VI. Production Strategy Options
   A. Make-to-stock
      1. Produce in advance
      2. Hold inventory
      3. Immediate delivery
      4. Risk of obsolescence
   B. Make-to-order
      1. Produce when ordered
      2. No finished goods inventory
      3. Lead time required
      4. Customization possible
   C. Assemble-to-order
      1. Pre-make components
      2. Assemble when ordered
      3. Balance of speed and customization
      4. Ex: Dell computers

VII. Inventory Trade-Offs
   A. High inventory
      1. Fast delivery
      2. Higher carrying costs
      3. Risk of waste/obsolescence
      4. Cash tied up
   B. Low inventory
      1. Lower carrying costs
      2. Less waste risk
      3. Longer lead times
      4. Risk of stockouts
   C. Just-in-time (JIT)
      1. Minimize inventory
      2. Receive materials when needed
      3. Requires reliable suppliers
      4. Toyota Production System
