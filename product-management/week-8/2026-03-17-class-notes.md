# Product Management - Class Notes
## March 17, 2026 - Week 8

I. Customer Interview Assignment (Pairs).
   A. Requirements.
      1. EACH COUPLE MUST INTERVIEW AT LEAST 8 CUSTOMERS.
      2. INTERVIEWS FOCUSED ON HOW PEOPLE DISCOVER AND EVALUATE NEW PEOPLE AND EVENTS.
      3. GOAL: UNDERSTAND BEHAVIORS, CONTEXT, AND MOTIVATIONS (WHAT / HOW / WHY).
   B. Minimum Questions (Per Interview).
      1. HOW DO YOU USUALLY DISCOVER OR MEET NEW PEOPLE.
      2. HOW DID YOU FIGURE OUT WHETHER YOU WANTED TO STAY IN TOUCH WITH PEOPLE YOU HAVE MET FOR THE FIRST TIME.
      3. WHAT MAKES MEETING NEW PEOPLE FEEL MEANINGFUL FOR YOU.
      4. WHEN WAS THE LAST TIME YOU MET SOMEONE IN A STRUCTURED WAY; HOW DID YOU FEEL ABOUT IT.
      5. WHAT TRAITS DO YOU LOOK FOR IN NEW PEOPLE YOU MEET.
      6. HOW DO YOU USUALLY FIND OUT ABOUT EVENTS, PLANS, OR THINGS HAPPENING AROUND YOU; TELL ME ABOUT A RECENT EXAMPLE.
   C. Improvised Questions.
      1. ENCOURAGED TO IMPROVISE FOLLOW-UP QUESTIONS TO MAKE INTERVIEWS MORE ENGAGING.
      2. USE WHY / HOW / TELL ME MORE TO DIG DEEPER INTO BEHAVIOUR AND CONTEXT.
      3. AVOID LEADING QUESTIONS; LET CUSTOMERS DESCRIBE REAL EXPERIENCES.

II. Analysis Framework (Per Pair).
   A. Literal Answers.
      1. TRANSCRIBE OR SUMMARISE WHAT CUSTOMERS SAID IN THEIR OWN WORDS.
      2. CAPTURE KEY PHRASES THAT REVEAL HABITS, TOOLS, OR EMOTIONS.
   B. WHAT: Behaviours.
      1. DESCRIPTION OF OBSERVED BEHAVIOURS (E.G. HOW THEY DISCOVER, DECIDE, FOLLOW UP).
      2. CHANNELS USED: APPS, SOCIAL MEDIA, FRIENDS, UNIVERSITY, WORK, EVENTS.
      3. ACTIONS TAKEN TO STAY IN TOUCH OR DROP CONTACT.
   C. HOW: Context.
      1. SITUATIONS WHERE THEY MEET NEW PEOPLE (CLASSES, PARTIES, WORK, APPS, TRIPS, HOBBIES).
      2. CONSTRAINTS: TIME, MONEY, CITY SIZE, SOCIAL ANXIETY, LANGUAGE, SCHEDULE.
      3. PATTERNS IN HOW THEY HEAR ABOUT EVENTS OR PLANS (GROUP CHATS, INSTAGRAM, CAMPUS CHANNELS, ETC.).
   D. WHY: Motivations.
      1. REASONS WHY MEETING NEW PEOPLE FEELS MEANINGFUL (BELONGING, FUN, CAREER, LEARNING, SUPPORT).
      2. REASONS FOR STAYING IN TOUCH OR NOT (VALUES, TRUST, SHARED INTERESTS, FIRST IMPRESSION).
      3. EMOTIONS AROUND STRUCTURED SETTINGS (COMFORTABLE, AWKWARD, SAFE, FORCED).

III. Customer Journey (Meeting New People).
   A. Stages.
      1. AWARENESS: HOW PEOPLE REALISE THEY WANT OR NEED TO MEET NEW PEOPLE.
      2. DISCOVERY: WHERE AND HOW THEY FIND NEW PEOPLE OR EVENTS.
      3. FIRST CONTACT: INITIAL INTERACTION (ONLINE MESSAGE, INTRODUCTION, EVENT, CLUB MEETING, PARTY).
      4. EVALUATION: HOW THEY DECIDE WHETHER TO STAY IN TOUCH (FIRST IMPRESSION, VIBES, SHARED INTERESTS).
      5. FOLLOW-UP: ACTIONS AFTER FIRST MEETING (MESSAGES, INVITES, SHARED ACTIVITIES).
      6. RELATIONSHIP BUILDING: REPEATED INTERACTIONS OR MOVING ON.
   B. Touchpoints.
      1. DIGITAL: SOCIAL NETWORKS, MESSAGING APPS, MATCHING APPS, EMAIL (UNIVERSITY BLASTS, EVENT NEWSLETTERS).
      2. PHYSICAL: CAMPUS, WORKPLACE, EVENTS, HOBBY GROUPS, PARTIES, STUDENT SOCIETIES.
      3. SOCIAL: FRIEND-OF-A-FRIEND INTRODUCTIONS, STUDENT GROUPS, CLUBS.
   C. Pain Points.
      1. DIFFICULTY FINDING RELEVANT OR TRUSTWORTHY EVENTS/PEOPLE.
      2. AWKWARDNESS OR ANXIETY IN STRUCTURED SETTINGS.
      3. FRICTION IN MOVING FROM FIRST CONTACT TO REGULAR CONNECTION.
      4. NOTIFICATION FATIGUE: TOO MANY EMAILS / MESSAGES ABOUT EVENTS; PEOPLE STOP READING.
      5. REPEATING THE SAME INTRO QUESTIONS (“NAME, MAJOR, WHERE ARE YOU FROM”) UNTIL IT FEELS TIRED.

IV. Customer Personas (Segmentation).
   A. Based On Behaviour.
      1. PLANNERS: USE CALENDARS, EVENTS, AND FORMAL GROUPS TO MEET PEOPLE.
      2. SPONTANEOUS: PREFER LAST-MINUTE PLANS, PARTIES, ORGANIC INTRODUCTIONS.
      3. DIGITAL-FIRST: DISCOVER PEOPLE AND EVENTS PRIMARILY THROUGH APPS AND SOCIAL MEDIA.
      4. OFFLINE-FIRST: RELY ON CAMPUS, WORK, OR HOBBIES RATHER THAN APPS.
   B. Based On Context.
      1. NEW IN CITY / EXCHANGE STUDENTS: HIGH MOTIVATION TO MEET PEOPLE QUICKLY.
      2. LOCALS WITH ESTABLISHED NETWORKS: LOWER URGENCY, MORE SELECTIVE.
      3. WORKING-STUDENTS: TIME-CONSTRAINED; PREFER HIGH-SIGNAL EVENTS.
   C. Based On Motivation.
      1. SOCIAL / FUN-ORIENTED: LOOKING FOR FRIENDS, PARTIES, SHARED INTERESTS.
      2. PROFESSIONAL / CAREER: NETWORKING FOR INTERNSHIPS, JOBS, MENTORS.
      3. SUPPORT / COMMUNITY: LOOKING FOR BELONGING, SAFE SPACES, MUTUAL HELP.

V. Expected Output From Each Pair.
   A. Interview Summary.
      1. SHORT SYNTHESIS OF 8+ INTERVIEWS, HIGHLIGHTING COMMON PATTERNS.
      2. EXAMPLES AND QUOTES THAT ILLUSTRATE BEHAVIOURS AND MOTIVATIONS.
   B. Journey Map.
      1. CLEAR STAGES FROM AWARENESS TO RELATIONSHIP BUILDING.
      2. KEY TOUCHPOINTS, PAIN POINTS, AND OPPORTUNITIES.
   C. Personas.
      1. 2–3 CUSTOMER PERSONAS WITH NAME, CONTEXT, BEHAVIOURS, AND GOALS.
      2. LINK FROM PERSONAS BACK TO SPECIFIC INTERVIEW EVIDENCE.


X. 30-Second Cold Call Version.
A. 3-Line Speaking Frame.
   1. TOPIC: STATE THE CORE QUESTION IN ONE LINE.
   2. LOGIC: GIVE 2 DRIVERS/FACTORS THAT EXPLAIN THE ANSWER.
   3. ACTION: END WITH ONE DECISION OR IMPLICATION.
B. Delivery Rule.
   1. 25-30 SECONDS MAX.
   2. NO BACKGROUND STORY; GO STRAIGHT TO CLAIM + EVIDENCE + CLOSE.

DI. Oral Answer Template (60-90 Seconds, Class Use).
A. Opening.
   1. STATE THE CORE QUESTION OR TOPIC IN ONE LINE.
   2. STATE YOUR MAIN CLAIM/THESIS IN ONE LINE.
B. Logic.
   1. GIVE 2-3 KEY FACTORS OR DRIVERS.
   2. LINK EACH FACTOR TO THE CLAIM (CAUSE -> EFFECT).
C. Action Or Implication.
   1. END WITH ONE DECISION, RECOMMENDATION, OR CONSEQUENCE.
   2. CLOSE CLEARLY WITHOUT ADDING NEW IDEAS.
D. Delivery Rule.
   1. 60-90 SECONDS.
   2. CLAIM -> EVIDENCE -> CLOSE.
