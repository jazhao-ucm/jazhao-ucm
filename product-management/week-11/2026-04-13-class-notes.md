**Topic:** Cumulative course synthesis, Weeks 1 to 10.

I. Course Throughline.
   A. Core Product Management Goal.
      1. Create user value and capture business value.
      2. Balance desirability, feasibility, and viability.
   B. End-To-End PM Logic.
      1. Identify real user problem.
      2. Design value proposition and business model.
      3. Build and test fast.
      4. Learn from evidence and iterate.

II. Strategy Foundations (Weeks 1 to 4).
   A. Value Creation Versus Value Capture.
      1. Strong solution does not guarantee strong capture.
      2. Complementary assets determine scaling power.
   B. Value Chain and Positioning.
      1. Product decisions connect to operations, marketing, and service.
      2. PM aligns functional choices with strategic advantage.
   C. Business Model Design.
      1. Clarify customer segments, value proposition, channels, revenue, and costs.
      2. Test assumptions early instead of treating the model as fixed.
   D. Case-Based Learning (Zara and Related Examples).
      1. Speed, feedback loops, and execution discipline create edge.
      2. Operational model is part of product strategy.

III. Discovery and Design (Weeks 5 to 6).
   A. Design Process.
      1. Problem framing before solutioning.
      2. Divergence then convergence in concept generation.
   B. Design Thinking Practices.
      1. Empathy and observation reduce false assumptions.
      2. Interview quality drives insight quality.
   C. Validation Mindset.
      1. Use small tests before full investment.
      2. Evidence over opinion in feature and prioritization choices.

IV. Operations and Execution (Weeks 7 and 9).
   A. Manufacturing and Process Design Lens.
      1. Product promise must match delivery capability.
      2. Process tradeoffs shape quality, speed, and cost.
   B. Service Product Internationalization.
      1. Service scale requires local adaptation with quality control.
      2. Frontstage and backstage process mapping prevents execution gaps.
   C. Organizational Alignment.
      1. Clarify HQ versus local decision rights.
      2. Align commercial, operations, and support handoffs.

V. Application Projects and Product Framing (Weeks 8 to 10).
   A. Customer Interviews Project.
      1. Use interview synthesis to identify recurring pain points.
      2. Translate observations into opportunity statements.
   B. Concept Development Examples (Social and Dating App).
      1. Focus on quality interactions over volume.
      2. Build for trust, safety, and onboarding clarity.
   C. AI-Assisted Prototyping Workflow.
      1. Prompt to prototype to UX refinement to user feedback loop.
      2. Compare tool outputs; do not trust first draft.

VI. Repeated Evaluation Criteria Across The Course.
   A. User Value.
      1. Does it solve a meaningful problem for a clear segment.
      2. Is the experience understandable and low-friction.
   B. Business Value.
      1. Is there a credible capture mechanism.
      2. Do costs, channels, and scalability support sustainability.
   C. Execution Reality.
      1. Can the team deliver reliably with current capabilities.
      2. What must be standardized versus localized.

VII. Final Exam Preparation Arc (Working Synthesis).
   A. Master Structure For Answers.
      1. State topic and claim.
      2. Explain with 2 to 3 drivers.
      3. End with one decision or implication.
   B. Cross-Topic Connections To Practice.
      1. Value chain plus business model plus process design.
      2. Discovery insight plus prototype plus validation evidence.
   C. Common Mistakes To Avoid.
      1. Describing tools without product logic.
      2. Skipping market access and delivery constraints.
      3. Giving abstract theory without action recommendation.
