# Product Management Class Notes
## February 23, 2026 Week 5

**Topic:** Exploration vs. exploitation; lean validation; product design process; SDLC.

---

I. Exploration vs. Exploitation
   A. Exploration (sensing)
      1. Finding new opportunities
      2. Searching and discovering
      3. Looking for what could be
      4. Higher risk, uncertain returns
   B. Exploitation (seizing)
      1. Executing on known opportunities
      2. Scaling what works
      3. Capturing value from existing knowledge
      4. Lower risk, predictable returns
   C. Ambidextrous organization
      1. Need both exploration and exploitation
      2. Exploration finds, exploitation captures
      3. Balance required for long-term success
   D. Example Microsoft
      1. Strong in exploitation (scaling products)
      2. Weaker in exploration historically
      3. Recent shift toward more exploration

II. Product Design Process Stages
   A. Problem-solution fit
      1. Does your solution solve the problem?
      2. Validate before building
      3. Customer discovery phase
   B. Product-market fit
      1. Does the market want your product?
      2. Are customers willing to pay?
      3. Can you scale profitably?
   C. Growth
      1. Scale what works
      2. Expand market
      3. Optimize operations

III. Lean Validation
   A. Core principle
      1. Test before you spend
      2. Validate hypotheses early
      3. Fail fast, fail cheap
   B. Process
      1. Build create minimum viable product
      2. Measure collect data
      3. Learn analyze and decide
      4. Iterate or pivot
   C. Key insight
      1. Don't build until validation
      2. Assumptions are hypotheses to test
      3. Evidence over intuition

IV. Case Studies
   A. Peloton
      1. Strong initial product-market fit
      2. COVID accelerated growth
      3. Post-COVID challenges
   B. Tesla
      1. Long road to product-market fit
      2. Eventually found mass market
      3. Required significant investment
   C. Apple Vision Pro
      1. Technology impressive
      2. Market fit uncertain
      3. Too early to judge success

V. SDLC Software Development Life Cycle
   A. Phases
      1. Planning define requirements
      2. Analysis understand needs
      3. Design create architecture
      4. Development build product
      5. Testing verify quality
      6. Deployment release to users
      7. Maintenance ongoing support
   B. Methodologies
      1. Waterfall sequential, plan upfront
      2. Agile iterative, adaptive
      3. DevOps continuous integration/delivery
   C. Key insight
      1. Process should match product type
      2. Agile better for uncertainty
      3. Waterfall better for known requirements

VI. Customer Discovery
   A. Purpose
      1. Understand customer needs
      2. Validate problem exists
      3. Before building solution
   B. Methods
      1. Interviews
      2. Observation
      3. Surveys
      4. Prototypes
   C. Key insight
      1. Don't spend money before discovery
      2. Learn before building
      3. Assumptions must be tested

VII. Iteration and Pivoting
   A. Iteration
      1. Small improvements to existing approach
      2. Same direction, better execution
      3. Based on feedback
   B. Pivot
      1. Fundamental change in direction
      2. Same vision, different strategy
      3. Based on failed validation
   C. When to pivot
      1. Multiple iterations don't improve
      2. Evidence suggests different approach
      3. Market changed significantly
