## Key Concepts

### I. Exploration vs. Exploitation (Ambidextrous Organization)

Purpose: Organizations need to balance two types of activities to succeed.

A. Two types of activities

1. **Exploration (sensing)**
   a. Finding new opportunities
   b. Searching, discovering
   c. Looking for what could be

2. **Exploitation (seizing)**
   a. Executing on known opportunities
   b. Doing, scaling what works
   c. Capturing value from what we already know

B. Key insight
1. Organizations need both; exploration finds opportunities, exploitation captures value from them.

C. Example
1. Microsoft: often cited as strong in exploitation (scaling existing products, execution) rather than exploration.

---

### II. Product Design Process - Stages

A. Problem-solution fit

1. First stage: understand the problem before building
2. Customer discovery: find out what customers want and need
3. Do not spend money yet; validate before investing

B. Key principle
1. Do not spend money until you have validation.
2. Lean approach: learn before you build.

C. Stages (general flow)

1. Problem discovery
   a. What are the main problems?
   b. What do customers struggle with?

2. Customer discovery
   a. What do customers want?
   b. What are they doing?
   c. Go to the market; talk to customers

3. Solution validation
   a. Does the solution make sense?
   b. Find out what is doing well, what customers value

4. Build/launch
   a. Only after validation
   b. Then invest money

Note: Relationship with communities and customers is critical. Find insight before spending.

---

### III. When Things Go Wrong

A. Market conditions

1. If market is not growing, consider pivoting or exiting
2. Sometimes need to stop going to market and reassess

B. Rebranding

1. Expensive
2. May not solve underlying problem
3. If product is not working in market, throwing money at rebrand may not fix it

C. Key point
1. Need to understand why things are not working before spending more.

B. Key point
