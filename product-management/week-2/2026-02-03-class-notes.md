# Product Management Class Notes
## February 3, 2026 Week 2

**Topic:** Personal vs. market problems; community validation; development cycles.

---

I. Personal vs. Market Problems
   A. Key distinction
      1. Personal problem = you have it
      2. Market problem = many people have it AND will pay
   B. Validation required
      1. Test if others have same problem
      2. Test willingness to pay
      3. Don't assume your problem is universal
   C. Common mistake
      1. Building solution to personal problem
      2. Assuming others will want it
      3. "Build it and they will come" fallacy

II. Community Before Product
   A. Core principle
      1. Find community first
      2. Understand their needs
      3. Then build product
   B. Process
      1. Find people with similar interests
      2. Assess what they actually need
      3. Validate problem exists at scale
   C. Key insight
      1. Community validates market
      2. Product serves community

III. Value Creation Through Community
   A. Finding the gap
      1. "Box of space" = unmet needs
      2. What is happening vs. what they want
      3. Opportunity in the difference
   B. Process
      1. Observe current behavior
      2. Identify pain points
      3. Understand desired outcomes
   C. Key principle
      1. Value created by filling gaps
      2. Community defines what gaps matter

IV. Design & Development Cycle
   A. Iterative process
      1. Sprint develop feature
      2. Test get user feedback
      3. Learn analyze results
      4. Iterate improve and repeat
   B. Prioritization
      1. Focus investment on most important features
      2. Don't try to build everything
      3. Each cycle = one improvement
   C. Testing as investing
      1. Testing is not cost, it's investment
      2. Learn before committing resources
      3. Fail fast, fail cheap

V. The "Build It and They Will Come" Problem
   A. The mistake
      1. Building without validation
      2. Assuming people will pay
      3. Ignoring market signals
   B. Example from class
      1. Student spent $500K
      2. No market validation
      3. Complete failure
   C. Lesson
      1. Understand problem before building
      2. Validate willingness to pay
      3. Small tests before big investments

VI. Understanding User Needs
   A. Key questions
      1. What do they actually need?
      2. What are they willing to pay for?
      3. What problems frustrate them?
   B. Methods
      1. Observation over asking
      2. Behavior over stated preferences
      3. Actions over opinions
   C. Key insight
      1. Users don't always know what they want
      2. Watch what they do, not what they say

VII. Market Validation Process
   A. Steps
      1. Identify potential problem
      2. Find people who have it
      3. Test if they'll pay to solve it
      4. Validate at scale
   B. Signals
      1. Strong signal = paying customers
      2. Weak signal = "I would use it"
      3. No signal = no one interested
   C. Key principle
      1. Revenue validates market
      2. Intentions don't equal actions

VIII. Pivoting When Needed
   A. When to pivot
      1. No willingness to pay
      2. Wrong target customer
      3. Wrong value proposition
   B. How to pivot
      1. Keep core insight
      2. Change customer or solution
      3. Test new hypothesis
   C. Key insight
      1. Pivoting is not failure
      2. Learning leads to better product
