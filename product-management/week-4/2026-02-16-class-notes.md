# Product Management Class Notes
## February 16, 2026 Week 4

**Topic:** Technology adoption curve (S-curve); disruptive innovation; first mover advantage.

---

I. Technology S-Curve
   A. Definition
      1. Technology performance follows S-shaped pattern over time
      2. Describes lifecycle of technology adoption
   B. Phases
      1. Beginning slow improvement, technology evolving
      2. Middle rapid performance improvement (steep curve)
      3. Maturity performance plateaus, incremental gains
      4. Decline old tech replaced by new
   C. Key insight
      1. Every technology has limits
      2. Investment should shift as tech matures

II. Investment Pattern Along S-Curve
   A. Early stage
      1. Low investment
      2. High uncertainty
      3. Few players
   B. Growth stage
      1. Heavy investment
      2. Rapid improvement
      3. Many entrants
   C. Maturity stage
      1. Investment shifts to new tech
      2. Diminishing returns
      3. Market consolidation

III. Disruptive Innovation
   A. Definition
      1. New tech starts with lower performance
      2. Often cheaper/simpler
      3. Eventually overtakes established tech
   B. Pattern
      1. Enters at bottom of market
      2. Improves faster than customer needs increase
      3. Eventually meets mainstream needs
   C. Examples
      1. Digital cameras vs. film
      2. Streaming vs. DVDs
      3. Electric cars vs. gas

IV. Innovator's Dilemma
   A. The problem
      1. Established firms focus on current customers
      2. Current customers want better performance
      3. Miss emerging technology threat
   B. Why leaders fail
      1. Listen to best customers
      2. Invest in high-margin products
      3. Ignore low-end disruption
   C. Key insight
      1. Lower performance tech can win
      2. "Good enough" beats "best" for many customers

V. First Mover Advantage
   A. Potential advantages
      1. Brand recognition
      2. Customer loyalty
      3. Learning curve benefits
      4. Network effects
   B. Potential disadvantages
      1. Market education costs
      2. Technology uncertainty
      3. Fast followers learn from mistakes
   C. When advantage holds
      1. High switching costs
      2. Strong network effects
      3. Significant learning curve

VI. Technology Transitions
   A. Signals of transition
      1. Diminishing returns on current tech
      2. New tech improving rapidly
      3. Customer needs changing
   B. Strategic responses
      1. Invest in new tech early
      2. Acquire disruptive startups
      3. Create separate division for new tech
   C. Common mistakes
      1. Waiting too long
      2. Underestimating new tech
      3. Over-investing in mature tech

VII. Platform vs. Product Strategy
   A. Product focus
      1. Compete on features
      2. Direct revenue from sales
      3. Incremental improvements
   B. Platform focus
      1. Enable others to create value
      2. Revenue from ecosystem
      3. Network effects compound
   C. Transition
      1. Products can become platforms
      2. Platforms can disrupt product companies
      3. Ex: iPhone started as product, became platform

VIII. Market Timing
   A. Too early
      1. Market not ready
      2. Technology not mature
      3. High education costs
   B. Too late
      1. Established competitors
      2. Hard to differentiate
      3. Compressed margins
   C. Right timing
      1. Technology ready
      2. Market demand emerging
      3. Before consolidation
