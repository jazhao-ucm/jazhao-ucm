# Product Management Notes (Apr 21)

## Main topic

Technology adoption and market segmentation: how products move from first users to mainstream users.

## Core framework

- Diffusion of innovation curve:
  - Innovators
  - Early adopters
  - Early majority
  - Late majority
  - Laggards
- Not all groups buy for the same reasons.
- Product strategy must change as the product moves from one group to the next.

## Key concept: Crossing the Chasm

- There is a major gap between early adopters and the early majority.
- Many products fail at this transition.
- Early users tolerate rough edges; mainstream users usually do not.
- Early majority buyers want reliability, proof, references, and low risk.

## Bowling Alley strategy

- Treat segments like bowling pins.
- Target one narrow segment first.
- Win that segment with a clear use case and value proof.
- Use that credibility to enter adjacent segments.
- Repeat segment by segment instead of trying to win everyone at once.

## Why products fail

- Teams over-focus on features and under-focus on adoption behavior.
- Teams assume demand transfers automatically from early users to mainstream.
- Teams skip the validation and references needed by cautious buyers.
- Teams misread willingness to pay across segments.

## Segment behavior (practical)

- Innovators and early adopters: motivated by novelty, speed, and first access.
- Early majority: motivated by practical value, social proof, and lower risk.
- Late majority and laggards: adopt when products are standard, affordable, and trusted.

## Strategic implications

- Product-market fit is segment-specific, not one-time.
- Messaging, pricing, onboarding, and proof points should evolve by segment.
- Adoption is a process, not a launch event.

## Class takeaway

A product grows by sequencing segment wins and de-risking adoption as it moves from visionary users to pragmatic users.
