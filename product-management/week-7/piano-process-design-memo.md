# Process Design Comparison Memo: Piano Manufacturing

**Jade**  
**Product:** Pianos  
**Videos:** Traditional craftsman (Spain) vs. Yamaha factory (Japan)

---

## Framework: Manufacturing Process Dimensions

Process design involves trade-offs across six dimensions:

| Dimension         | Small batch / craft                          | Large batch / line                            |
| ----------------- | -------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| **Lot size**      | Few units, differentiation focus             | Many units, economies of scale                |
| **Equipment**     | General-purpose (flexible, lower fixed cost) | Specific-purpose (dedicated, high fixed cost) |
| **Skills**        | High artisan skill, higher wages             | Specialized cell workers, standardized tasks  |
| **Scheduling**    | Make-to-order, flexible                      | Flow/assembly line, planned                   |
| **Product range** | Broad variety, custom                        | Limited standard models                       |
| **Inventory**     | Low; WIP per order                           | High raw + WIP; finished goods                |

## Video Comparison

|                                          | Video 1: Traditional Craftsman (Spain)                               | Video 2: Yamaha Factory (Japan)                                            |
| ---------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Lot sizing (volume)**                  | Small batch / almost one-by-one                                      | Large batch / high volume                                                  |
| **Equipment**                            | General-purpose tools; woodworking equipment usable across processes | Specific-purpose machines; assembly line, conveyor belts (“chairs moving”) |
| **Skills / Workforce**                   | Very high artisan skill; intricate woodwork, knowledge of the craft  | Specialized workers; each does one task; standardized process              |
| **Scheduling**                           | Make-to-order, flexible                                              | Assembly line; same speed for all units; standardized flow                 |
| **Product range / variety**              | High variety / customised                                            | Limited standard models                                                    |
| **Inventory level (raw, WIP, finished)** | Low finished; mainly WIP per order                                   | High raw + WIP; many units in process                                      |

---

## Key Observations

- **Video 1:** One craftsman (or small team) handles many steps. General equipment, high skill, flexible. Suited to differentiation and premium positioning.
- **Video 2:** Standardized process, “same speed through the line.” Many workers, each specialized. Economies of scale; suited to cost and volume.
- **Why many machines in Video 2?** To support high volume and standardization; each station does one task repeatedly.
