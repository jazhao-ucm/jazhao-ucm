## Key Takeaways

1. Technology S-Curve - Performance follows S-curve: slow start, rapid growth, plateau
2. Innovator's Dilemma - Companies struggle to invest in new technology when old is profitable
3. Kodak Failure - Dominant film company failed to adapt to digital photography
4. Disruptive Innovation - New technology starts with lower performance but improves rapidly
5. Customer Preferences - Customers may prefer new technology even with initially lower performance
6. First Mover Advantage - Being first can provide market position advantages
7. Industry Focus - Industries focus on improving old technology, miss disruptive innovations
8. Radical Innovation - Often comes from outside established companies
9. Timing Matters - Market windows exist for innovation
10. Market Segmentation - High-end vs. low-end markets have different needs
11. Uber Example - Created new market, disrupted traditional taxi industry
12. Technology Persistence - Some technologies persist for decades (silicon chips - 60 years)
13. Performance vs. Expectations - New technology wins when it meets customer expectations better
14. Established Company Failure - Companies fail because they don't understand why customers prefer new technology
