Manuale Vivum Agentis (Living Agent Design Manual)

Version: 0.1 | March 31, 2026

Working manual for designing agentic systems that are technically robust, culturally adaptive, and operationally trustworthy.

Principia Prima (Core Principles)
  • Agents must explain consequential actions in user-readable language.
  • Autonomy is bounded by explicit policy and human confirmation gates.
  • Trust is measured behaviorally, not assumed from output fluency.
  • Cultural context is a design variable, not an afterthought.
  • Safety and fairness checks are release gates, not optional add-ons.
Audit Template (Replicable Method)
  • Claim: the specific capability or behavior to test.
  • Prompt/Task: one controlled input case.
  • Observed Result: what happened, verbatim first.
  • Metric: explicit score or threshold.
  • Status: validated, partial, unverified, contradicted.
Micro-Experiment (Madrid Pilot)
  • Sample: 5 participants, mixed language/cultural profiles.
  • Task: one high-context and one low-context agent interaction.
  • Capture: hesitation points, override moments, explanation requests.
  • Output: trust diagram and one-page findings memo.
