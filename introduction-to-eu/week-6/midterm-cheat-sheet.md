## Introduction to the EU – Midterm Cheat Sheet

**How to use:** Use for quick recall. Midterm: analyse texts, don't summarise. Key ideas, argument, key expressions.

---

### Course Structure – Four Blocks

1. **Block 1 – History & origins**: Before/after Treaty of Rome, Schuman Plan, coal and steel, economics and money.
2. **Block 2 – Institutions**: Executive (European Council + Commission), Legislative (Parliament + Council), Judiciary (Court of Justice), Other (ECB, Court of Auditors, agencies).
3. **Block 3 – Policies**: Euro, identity, enlargement, migration, citizenship. Most relevant for daily life.
4. **Block 4 – Foreign & security policy**: CFSP, defence, guest expert.

---

### Key Concepts

- **Supranational** = Above national level. EU institutions beyond single countries.
- **Official story** = How EU narrates itself. Success. Unity.
- **Critical view** = Realist history. Contingency. Crises. Not inevitable.
- **27 member states** (post-Brexit).

---

### History – Key Milestones

- **Schuman Plan** (1950): Coal and steel community. Avoid tariffs. Promote cooperation.
- **Treaty of Rome** (1957): EEC. Foundation of today's EU.
- **Luxembourg Compromise**: National interest can veto. Countries block when they disagree.
- **Southern enlargement**: Greece (1981), Spain and Portugal (1986).
- **Single European Act (SEA)**: Deeper integration. Single market.
- **Maastricht** (1992): European Union. Euro. Deeper integration.
- **Schengen**: No border checks. Free movement. Most EU countries.
- **UK opt-outs**: Did not join euro. Opted out of some policies.

---

### Southern Enlargement

- **Greece (1981)**: Military dictatorship fell (1974). Joining EC = consolidating democracy.
- **Spain & Portugal (1986)**: Carnation Revolution (Portugal 1974). Spain: transition after Franco (1975). Negotiated, not revolution.
- **Challenges**: France worried about CAP (Spain/Portugal had large farming sectors). Fishing, steel, textiles. Cohesion policy to help poorer members.
- **Cohesion policy**: Structural funds, Committee of Regions, EIB. Redistribution to poorer members.

---

### EU Institutions

- **Executive**: European Council (heads of state) + European Commission (proposes legislation, implements).
- **Legislative**: European Parliament (elected) + Council of the EU (ministers).
- **Judiciary**: Court of Justice of the EU.
- **Other**: ECB, Court of Auditors, agencies (EUIPO, etc.).

---

### Key Readings & Themes

- **Zweig** (*The World of Yesterday*): Europe that disappeared. Borders, identity, freedom vs. security.
- **Judt** (*Origins of the EU*): Post-war integration.
- **Anderson** (*Ever Closer Union?*): Integration narrative.
- **Vinen** (*History in Fragments*): Fragmented history.
- **Euroscepticism, Brexit, Anglosphere**: Bijsmans, Kenny & Pearce.

### Common Question Angles (Text Analysis)

- **"What is the main idea?"** Identify the central argument. Don't summarise everything.
- **"What are they arguing?"** Trace the logic. Cause and effect.
- **"What is the key expression?"** Quote that captures the argument.
- **"Compare official story vs. critical view."** How does the text frame EU history?
- **"How does [enlargement / institution / policy] fit into the broader narrative?"** Connect to course themes.
