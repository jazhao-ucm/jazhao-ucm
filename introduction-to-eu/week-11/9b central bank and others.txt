The European Council, the European Central Bank, and Other Institutions

Clean text extract notes

1) European Council
- Strategic direction body for the EU.
- Composed of member state leaders, Commission president, and senior EU officials.
- Works mainly by consensus.
- Sets broad priorities; does not serve as day-to-day legislative detail manager.
- Strong focus on preparation, informality, and delegation.
- Central role in major integration and crisis moments.

2) Historical evolution
- Formalized in 1974 to improve strategic leadership and unblock decision-making.
- Gained legal recognition in later treaty development.
- Lisbon Treaty institutionalized president role and regularized summit structure.

3) European Council operating logic
- Summits usually two days, with draft conclusions as key output.
- Leadership dynamics and domestic coalition constraints shape bargaining outcomes.
- Can convene extraordinary sessions for urgent crises.

4) European Central Bank (ECB)
- Created in 1998; based in Frankfurt.
- Original single mandate focused on price stability.
- Core structures:
  - Governing Council.
  - Executive Board.
  - General Council.
- Built with high independence, influenced by Bundesbank model.

5) ECB after eurozone crisis
- Practical role expanded beyond narrow inflation focus.
- Tools such as quantitative easing increased policy reach.
- Crisis phase intensified debate over mandate interpretation and political scope.
- Banking union elements (supervision/resolution mechanisms) increased ECB centrality.

6) European Investment Bank (EIB)
- Long-term project finance institution supporting balanced development.
- Prioritizes cohesion, infrastructure, competitiveness, and strategic investment projects.

7) European Court of Auditors (ECA)
- EU financial watchdog.
- Audits legality and regularity of EU revenues/expenditures.
- Reports can pressure spending reform and accountability improvements.
- Influence depends on institutional follow-through by Parliament and other bodies.

8) Europol and Eurojust
- Europol: criminal intelligence coordination (not a federal police force).
- Eurojust: judicial/prosecutorial coordination among member states.
- Both reflect deeper cross-border cooperation in justice and home affairs.

9) EESC and Committee of the Regions
- Advisory bodies for societal, sectoral, local, and regional representation.
- Consultation can be mandatory in some policy areas.
- Structural limitation: opinions are advisory and not binding.

10) Chapter-level takeaway
- EU governance is layered:
  - European Council for strategic direction.
  - ECB and other specialized bodies for technical execution and oversight.
- Institutional power is a mix of legal mandate, crisis context, and political bargaining.
