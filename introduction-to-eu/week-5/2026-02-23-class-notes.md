# Introduction to the EU — Class Notes
## February 23, 2026 — Week 5

**Topic:** Southern enlargement (Greece, Spain, Portugal); Delors; Single European Act; Maastricht; Schengen.

---

I. Luxembourg Compromise — Recap
   A. Effect
      1. Countries could veto on national interest
      2. National interest over supranational decisions
   B. Context
      1. US role in Cold War
      2. France insisted on sovereignty

II. Southern Enlargement — Greece (1981)
   A. Before accession
      1. Military dictatorship (colonels) 1967–1974
      2. Community initially hesitant
      3. Cyprus conflict 1974 (war with Turkey)
   B. Transition
      1. Fall of dictatorship
      2. Transition to democracy
      3. Joined EC 1981
   C. Significance
      1. Joining EC = consolidating democracy
      2. Anchoring in the West

III. Southern Enlargement — Portugal (1986)
   A. Carnation Revolution (1974)
      1. Peaceful revolution
      2. Flowers in the streets
      3. Different from Spain
   B. Colonial context
      1. Last European empire
      2. Colonies in Africa
      3. Decolonization movements

IV. Southern Enlargement — Spain (1986)
   A. Transition after Franco
      1. Franco died 1975
      2. No revolution — negotiated transition
      3. Democratic consolidation required
   B. Process
      1. Negotiations 1977–1986
      2. 1982 World Cup — soft power
   C. Challenges
      1. France worried about CAP
      2. Fishing industry concerns
      3. Steel and textile restructuring

V. Why Southern Enlargement Mattered
   A. Democracy consolidation
      1. Anchoring new democracies in West
      2. Fear of communism
   B. External factors
      1. US support
      2. Cold War context
   C. Economic integration
      1. Specialization in common market
      2. Cannot produce everything

VI. Jacques Delors and Revival
   A. Background
      1. Commission President 1985–1995
      2. Socialist, former French minister
   B. Achievement
      1. Revived European integration
      2. After "Eurosclerosis" of 1970s–early 1980s
   C. Single market project
      1. Complete internal market by 1992
      2. "1992 project"

VII. Single European Act (1986)
   A. What it did
      1. First major treaty revision since Rome
      2. Created single market goal
      3. Majority voting expanded
   B. Significance
      1. End of unanimous voting requirement
      2. Faster decision-making
      3. Revived integration momentum

VIII. Maastricht Treaty (1992)
   A. Created European Union
      1. Three pillars structure
      2. Economic and Monetary Union
      3. Common foreign and security policy
   B. Euro currency
      1. Single currency planned
      2. Convergence criteria set
      3. Not all joined (UK, Denmark opt-outs)
   C. EU citizenship
      1. Rights across member states
      2. Vote in local elections
      3. Consular protection abroad

IX. Schengen Area
   A. What it is
      1. No internal border checks
      2. Free movement of persons
      3. Stronger external borders
   B. Members
      1. Most EU countries
      2. Plus Norway, Iceland, Switzerland
      3. UK, Ireland not members
   C. Significance
      1. Visible symbol of integration
      2. Daily experience of "Europe"

X. UK Opt-Outs
   A. Euro
      1. Did not join single currency
      2. Kept pound sterling
   B. Schengen
      1. Not member
      2. Kept border controls
   C. Social chapter
      1. Initially opted out
      2. Later joined under Blair
