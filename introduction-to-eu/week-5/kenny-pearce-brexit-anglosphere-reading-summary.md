## Rhetorical Framework (Ethos, Logos, Pathos)

| Appeal | Meaning | Kenny & Pearce's Use |
|--------|---------|----------------------|
| **Ethos** | Credibility, authority, trust | Historical depth; moves beyond short-term explanations; scholarly synthesis |
| **Logos** | Logic, structure, argument | Anglosphere vs. Europe as identity tension; imagined community as analytical category |
| **Pathos** | Emotion, stakes, persuasion | Empire, memory, sovereignty; emotional appeal of Anglosphere narrative |

**Key insight:** Kenny & Pearce show how pro-Brexit actors deploy *pathos* (empire, Anglosphere, sovereignty) to build *ethos* (British identity, natural belonging) and challenge *logos* of EU membership. The Anglosphere is "imagined" but *rhetorically* powerful.

## Summary & Key Points

- The authors connect Brexit to long-standing British ideas about belonging to an "Anglosphere"—a community of English-speaking nations (UK, US, Canada, Australia, etc.) anchored in imperial history.
- They show how pro-Brexit actors mobilised this Anglosphere narrative as an attractive alternative to European integration.
- The chapter emphasises how memories of empire and wartime victory shape contemporary British understandings of sovereignty and international status.

**Rhetorical note:** "Anglosphere" = *pathos* (belonging, identity) + *ethos* (British exceptionalism). "Imagined community" = *logos* (analytical category). Pro-Brexit rhetoric as case study in how narratives shape politics.

---

## Main Arguments

### I. British Identity: Europe vs. Anglosphere

Kenny and Pearce argue that Brexit is partly the result of a deep tension in British identity: between being a European state and being the centre of a wider Anglosphere. Brexit revived the latter imagination, presenting Europe as a constraint and the English-speaking world as a natural sphere of influence.

**Rhetorical profile:**
- **Logos:** Identity tension as cause; Europe = constraint, Anglosphere = natural.
- **Pathos:** "Natural sphere of influence"—emotional appeal of belonging, empire, global role.
- **Ethos:** Pro-Brexit actors as mobilisers of this narrative; British identity as contested.

---

### II. Anglosphere as Imagined Community

They also contend that the Anglosphere is as much an imagined community as a concrete geopolitical project. It is powerful in rhetoric and symbolism, even if its economic and political foundations are uncertain.

**Rhetorical profile:**
- **Logos:** Imagined vs. concrete; rhetoric and symbolism vs. economic/political foundations.
- **Pathos:** Symbolic power—emotional appeal despite uncertain foundations.
- **Ethos:** Kenny & Pearce as analysts of narrative; they take the Anglosphere seriously as rhetoric even while questioning its substance.

## Critical Analysis

### Strengths

- Provides historical depth; moves beyond short-term explanations of Brexit; highlights the role of narratives, symbols and memory

**Rhetorical note:** Strengths align with *logos* (causal depth) and *pathos* (narrative, memory). Complements Bijsmans—Kenny & Pearce show how one form of Euroskepticism is entangled with identity, not just policy.

---

### Weaknesses or Limitations

- Focuses primarily on elite discourse; less detail on how ordinary citizens relate to the Anglosphere idea

**Rhetorical note:** Connects to Krawatzek & Pestel's top-down bias critique. *Pathos* of elites may not match *pathos* of ordinary voters—whose imagination counts?

---

### Questions Raised

- How widespread is attachment to the Anglosphere outside political and media elites?
- How does it intersect with class, region and generation in the UK?

**Rhetorical framing:**
- **Ethos:** Whose identity? Whose narrative?
- **Logos:** What evidence? Surveys, discourse analysis?
- **Pathos:** Different groups may have different emotional attachments.

---

### Connections to Other Readings

- Complements Judt's account of British reluctance to integrate with Europe
- Relates to course themes of historiography and teleology—Anglosphere as alternative narrative to "ever closer union"
- Connects to Bijsmans (Euroskepticism as multifaceted)—identity-based Euroskepticism
- Contrasts with institutional histories (Seidel, Warlouzet)—Kenny & Pearce focus on narrative and identity

**Rhetorical note:** Kenny & Pearce show how *pathos* (empire, Anglosphere) competes with *pathos* (European project) in British political imagination.

---

## Relevance to Course

- **Ethos:** Explains why the UK has often seen itself as a "reluctant European" even before 2016.
- **Logos:** Anglosphere vs. Europe as analytical framework; imagined community as concept.
- **Pathos:** Euroskepticism entangled with imperial memory and global identity, not only EU policies.
- **All three:** Different historical narratives compete when defining a country's place in or out of the EU.
