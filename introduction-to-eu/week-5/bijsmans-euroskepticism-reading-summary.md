## Rhetorical Framework (Ethos, Logos, Pathos)

| Appeal | Meaning | Bijsmans's Use |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| **Ethos** | Credibility, authority, trust | Encyclopedic synthesis; integrates parties, public opinion, crises; avoids simplistic labels |
| **Logos** | Logic, structure, argument | Conceptual map (soft/hard, levels, dimensions); causal factors (domestic politics, crises) |
| **Pathos** | Emotion, stakes, persuasion | Implicit—Euroskepticism as politically charged; appeal to readers seeking nuance over polemic |

**Key insight:** Bijsmans prioritizes *logos* (conceptual clarity, multidimensional framework) to build *ethos* (authoritative, balanced account). *Pathos* is mostly implicit—the stakes of Euroskepticism (legitimacy, crisis, populism) are present but not dramatized.

## Summary & Key Points

- Euroskepticism is not a single attitude but a spectrum of positions, from mild criticism of specific EU policies to principled rejection of integration.
- The article distinguishes between different types (e.g. soft vs. hard) and levels (parties, elites, citizens) of Euroskepticism.
- Domestic politics, socio-economic conditions and EU-level crises (euro, migration, rule-of-law disputes) all shape how and when Euroskepticism becomes politically powerful.

**Rhetorical note:** "Multifaceted" and "spectrum" = *logos* (conceptual precision). Rejection of "single attitude" = *ethos* (avoids oversimplification). *Pathos* in the political stakes—crises, legitimacy.

---

## Main Arguments

### I. Euroskepticism as Multidimensional

Euroskepticism should be understood as a multidimensional phenomenon, not just a simple "for or against" the EU. Different actors may oppose different aspects of integration (e.g. economic policies vs. political institutions vs. identity) and for different reasons.

**Rhetorical profile:**
- **Logos:** Dimensions (economic, political, identity); types (soft/hard); levels (parties, elites, citizens).
- **Ethos:** Bijsmans as conceptual clarifier; challenges "pro-/anti-EU" binary.
- **Pathos:** Implicit—different criticisms carry different emotional and political weight.

---

### II. Domestic Politics and Media Framing

Bijsmans argues that party competition and media framing in domestic arenas are central to explaining how Euroskepticism develops and spreads. National political contexts mediate citizens' views on the EU.

**Rhetorical profile:**
- **Logos:** Cause (domestic politics, media) → effect (Euroskepticism's form and spread).
- **Ethos:** Integrates literature on parties, public opinion, crises.
- **Pathos:** Domestic stakes—elections, referendums, party competition.

## Critical Analysis

### Strengths

- Offers a clear conceptual map; integrates literature on parties, public opinion and crises; avoids simplistic "pro-/anti-EU" labels

**Rhetorical note:** Strengths align with *logos* (conceptual clarity) and *ethos* (balanced, comprehensive). Complements Krawatzek & Pestel's call for diverse perspectives—Bijsmans takes Euroskepticism seriously as object of study, not just threat.

---

### Weaknesses or Limitations

- Largely descriptive and focused on Europe; less attention to how non-European actors or global factors influence Euroskepticism

**Rhetorical note:** *Logos* (causal analysis) may be limited by European focus. *Ethos* as encyclopedic entry may prioritize synthesis over original argument.

---

### Questions Raised

- To what extent is Euroskepticism a normal feature of democratic contestation about policy, rather than a threat to the EU's existence?

**Rhetorical framing:**
- **Ethos:** Who frames Euroskepticism as "threat" vs. "corrective"?
- **Logos:** What evidence supports each view?
- **Pathos:** Stakes of the question—legitimacy, stability, democracy.

---

### Connections to Other Readings

- Links to Gilbert's critique of progressive narratives (Euroskepticism as counter-narrative)
- Connects to course discussions on legitimacy, crisis and populism
- Complements Anderson's critical stance—both address contemporary challenges
- Contrasts with celebratory institutional histories; Bijsmans takes criticism seriously as phenomenon

**Rhetorical note:** Bijsmans occupies a different *ethos*—analytical, classificatory—from Anderson (polemical) or Vinen (methodological). He provides *logos* (concepts) for understanding debates others narrate.
