# Introduction to the EU Class Notes
## January 27, 2026 Week 1

**Topic:** History and foundations; multiple unions; enlargements; four freedoms; key treaties.

---

I. Understanding Europe's Complexity
   A. EU = union of unions
      1. Not just countries joining
      2. Previous unions merged together
      3. Creates complexity
   B. Key distinctions
      1. Council of Europe ≠ EU Council
      2. Customs union ≠ EU membership
      3. Schengen ≠ eurozone

II. Multiple Overlapping Systems
   A. Council of Europe
      1. Includes Turkey, Russia (historically)
      2. Focus: human rights, democracy, rule of law
      3. Not defined by free movement
   B. European Union
      1. 27 member states
      2. Customs union + single market
      3. Schengen (for most) + euro (for most)
   C. Customs union
      1. Includes more than EU members
      2. Ex: Turkey has customs union, not membership
   D. Schengen area
      1. Free movement zone (no border checks)
      2. Includes non-EU: Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway, Switzerland
      3. Excludes some EU: Ireland, Cyprus
   E. Eurozone
      1. Countries using euro
      2. Denmark has opt-out

III. Map of Enlargements
   A. Founding six 1951
      1. France, Germany, Italy
      2. Belgium, Netherlands, Luxembourg
   B. First enlargement 1973
      1. UK, Ireland, Denmark
   C. Second enlargement 1980s
      1. Greece (1981)
      2. Spain, Portugal (1986)
   D. Third enlargement 1995
      1. Austria, Finland, Sweden
   E. Fourth enlargement 2000s
      1. 2004: 10 countries (Eastern Europe + Cyprus, Malta)
      2. 2007: Bulgaria, Romania
      3. 2013: Croatia
   F. Current status
      1. 27 member states
      2. Norway not in EU but in Schengen

IV. Four Freedoms of the EU
   A. Free movement of people
      1. Travel, work, study, live anywhere
      2. Applies to EU citizens and families
   B. Free movement of goods
      1. No tariffs between member states
      2. Common standards and regulations
   C. Free movement of services
      1. Businesses operate across borders
      2. Professionals provide services anywhere
   D. Free movement of capital
      1. Money moves freely
      2. Investment across borders

V. Economic and Social Cohesion
   A. Purpose
      1. Reduce economic disparities
      2. Support less developed regions
   B. Mechanisms
      1. Structural funds
      2. Cohesion funds
      3. Infrastructure support
   C. Reality
      1. Two-speed Europe exists
      2. Some regions more developed
      3. Gaps remain despite funds

VI. Membership Requirements Copenhagen Criteria
   A. Democracy
      1. Stable democratic institutions
      2. Rule of law
      3. Human rights including minorities
   B. Market economy
      1. Functioning market economy
      2. Can cope with EU competition
   C. Acquis communautaire
      1. Adopt all EU laws
      2. Implement EU regulations

VII. Key Treaties Timeline
   A. 1951 Treaty of Paris
      1. Coal and Steel Community
      2. War industries under common control
      3. Founding six countries
   B. 1957 Treaty of Rome
      1. European Economic Community
      2. Common market created
      3. No tariffs between members
   C. 1985 Single European Act
      1. Single market (not just common)
      2. GDP growth resulted
      3. Increased trade
   D. 1995 Schengen enters force
      1. Open internal borders
      2. Stronger external borders
      3. ~3.5M cross borders daily
   E. 1999/2002 Euro currency
      1. Make trade easier
      2. "United in diversity" motto
   F. 2007 Lisbon Treaty
      1. Replaced previous treaties
      2. More power to Parliament
      3. Citizens' initiative added

VIII. Reality Check Free Movement
   A. Works well for some
      1. White Europeans generally
      2. Professionals with resources
   B. Limitations in practice
      1. External borders problematic
      2. Roma face discrimination
      3. Minorities face barriers
   C. Key insight
      1. Legal rights ≠ lived reality
      2. Implementation varies

IX. Democracy Discussion
   A. Monarchies allowed
      1. If parliament elected
      2. If monarch constitutional
      3. Ex: Netherlands, Spain, Belgium
   B. Free press required
      1. Part of democracy requirement
      2. Cannot join without
   C. Pluralism required
      1. Multiple parties
      2. Free elections
   D. Recent concerns
      1. Poland, Hungary rule of law issues
      2. EU using pressure mechanisms

X. 30-Second Cold Call Version.
A. 3-Line Speaking Frame.
   1. TOPIC: STATE THE CORE QUESTION IN ONE LINE.
   2. LOGIC: GIVE 2 DRIVERS/FACTORS THAT EXPLAIN THE ANSWER.
   3. ACTION: END WITH ONE DECISION OR IMPLICATION.
B. Delivery Rule.
   1. 25-30 SECONDS MAX.
   2. NO BACKGROUND STORY; GO STRAIGHT TO CLAIM + EVIDENCE + CLOSE.

DI. Oral Answer Template (60-90 Seconds, Class Use).
A. Opening.
   1. STATE THE CORE QUESTION OR TOPIC IN ONE LINE.
   2. STATE YOUR MAIN CLAIM/THESIS IN ONE LINE.
B. Logic.
   1. GIVE 2-3 KEY FACTORS OR DRIVERS.
   2. LINK EACH FACTOR TO THE CLAIM (CAUSE -> EFFECT).
C. Action Or Implication.
   1. END WITH ONE DECISION, RECOMMENDATION, OR CONSEQUENCE.
   2. CLOSE CLEARLY WITHOUT ADDING NEW IDEAS.
D. Delivery Rule.
   1. 60-90 SECONDS.
   2. CLAIM -> EVIDENCE -> CLOSE.
