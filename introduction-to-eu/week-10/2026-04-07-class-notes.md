   B. Liberals and center-oriented positions

      3. Less interventionist than social-democratic framing
   C. Right and far-right variations
      1. Stronger emphasis on national control and prioritization narratives
      2. Mixed internal positions on market versus state intervention
      3. Migration sometimes linked to housing discourse in political messaging

   A. Distinction between party groups and alliances
      1. Practical alliances can diverge from pure ideology
      2. National interests and geopolitical alignments shape outcomes
   B. Instructor examples
      1. Cases where cross-bloc cooperation appears for strategic reasons
      2. Cases where national context shifts rhetoric or coalition behavior
   C. Takeaway
      1. EU politics analyzed by ideology plus institutional and strategic constraints

IV. Student presentation: EU agencies and institutional evolution
   A. What agencies are
      1. Specialized bodies for expertise, coordination, monitoring, and implementation support
      2. Not full substitute for political lawmaking institutions
   B. Wave logic from reading summary
      1. Early wave with information and networking functions
      2. Expansion wave with stronger coordination and technical roles
      3. Later wave with broader regulatory and crisis-response relevance
   C. Core tension
      1. Formal independence versus institutional control by member states and EU principals
      2. Accountability and democratic oversight versus technical autonomy

V. European Council and ECB focus from second presentation

      1. Strategic direction-setting body of heads of government or state
      2. Informal and consensus-heavy summit practice
      3. No direct ordinary lawmaking role, but major agenda influence
   B. European Central Bank
      1. Initial narrow inflation mandate
      2. Post-crisis expansion in practical role and tools
      3. Discussion of tension between mandate boundaries and crisis management
   C. Other bodies noted
      1. Europol and Eurojust as coordination institutions
      2. EIB as financing actor
      3. Advisory bodies and Court of Auditors in oversight architecture

VI. Class-level synthesis
   A. Housing policy is a test case for EU multilevel governance
      1. Rights language, market design, and fiscal limits intersect
      2. National and EU levels interact but do not move at same speed
   B. Institutional analysis principle
      1. Separate legal powers from real political influence
      2. Track who decides, who implements, and who is accountable
