## Rhetorical Framework (Ethos, Logos, Pathos)

| Appeal | Meaning | Krawatzek & Pestel's Use |
|--------|---------|--------------------------|
| **Ethos** | Credibility, authority, trust | Scholarly review; meta-critique of historiography; balanced assessment |
| **Logos** | Logic, structure, argument | Systematic comparison of approaches; identification of gaps and biases |
| **Pathos** | Emotion, stakes, persuasion | Implicit appeal for diversity, inclusion of marginal voices |

**Key insight:** The article itself uses *logos* (categorizing and critiquing narratives) and *ethos* (positioning as fair reviewers). The *pathos* is indirect—advocacy for "diverse and vernacular voices" appeals to the reader's sense of fairness and inclusion.

**Meta-note:** The *reviewed* scholars (Pagden, Ghervas, Gilbert, etc.) each deploy ethos, logos, and pathos differently in their own narratives of Europe—K&P analyze how those choices shape what gets told and what gets left out.

## Summary & Key Points

### I. The Evolving European Project

- The European project is constantly evolving, shaped by historical junctures and ongoing crises (WWI, WWII, Treaty of Rome, Brexit, war in Ukraine).
- Recent scholarship aims to reach broader audiences, often making political interventions and judgments about Europe's past and present.
- The European project is narrated through multiple lenses: intellectual, institutional, and peace-oriented perspectives.

**Rhetorical note:**
- **Logos:** Crises as structural drivers of narrative change; multiple lenses as analytical categories.
- **Ethos:** K&P position themselves as observers of scholarly trends, not just participants.
- **Pathos:** "Political interventions" suggests scholarship is not neutral—stakes beyond academia.

---

### II. Reviewed Scholarship (Rhetorical Profiles)

#### II.A. Pagden's Intellectual History

- Traces Europe's roots from Enlightenment thinkers to postwar integration
- Highlights the interplay of nationalism, colonialism, and Europeanism
- Emphasizes intellectual foundations of European identity

**Rhetorical profile:** Strong *logos* (ideas as causes); *ethos* (philosophical lineage); *pathos* (identity, belonging).

---

#### II.B. Ghervas's Peace-Oriented Approach

- Centers on Europe's quest for peace
- Argues that peace projects and post-war reconstruction are central to European identity and integration
- Views European integration as fundamentally about preventing war

**Rhetorical profile:** Strong *pathos* (peace, fear of war); *logos* (peace as causal driver); *ethos* (moral purpose of integration).

---

#### II.C. Gilbert's Institutional History

- Focuses on the institutional and political history of European integration
- Emphasizes leaders and executive branches
- Less emphasis on social and cultural integration
- Top-down perspective on European development

**Rhetorical profile:** Strong *logos* (institutions, structures); *ethos* (elites, leaders); weaker *pathos* (ordinary people absent).

---

#### II.D. Van Middelaar's Insider View

- Provides an insider's view of EU institutions
- Analyzes legitimacy and the dynamics between member states and treaties
- Offers institutional perspective on EU functioning

**Rhetorical profile:** Strong *ethos* (insider authority); *logos* (institutional mechanics); *pathos* (legitimacy as emotional stake).

---

#### II.E. Jarausch's Defensive Account

- Defends the achievements of European integration
- Contrasts Europe's social, political, and cultural progress with challenges in the US and elsewhere
- Presents a positive assessment of European integration

**Rhetorical profile:** Strong *pathos* (defense, pride, contrast with US); *ethos* (scholarly defense); *logos* (comparative argument).

---

### III. Critical Observations

- All reviewed works tend to affirm the European project and its institutions, but often privilege top-down, pro-European perspectives.
- The reviewed literature often overlooks more diverse or critical voices, including anti-European, Eurosceptic, and alternative perspectives.
- The European project is narrated through intellectual, institutional, and peace-oriented lenses, but future scholarship should include more diverse and vernacular voices.

**Rhetorical note:**
- **Logos:** K&P's argument—systematic gap in the literature.
- **Ethos:** K&P as fair critics who notice what others miss.
- **Pathos:** Implicit appeal for inclusion; "diverse and vernacular voices" as a value.

## Context & Analysis

### Key Critiques

**Analysis:** The reviewed literature highlights the need for historically grounded debates about Europe's evolution, but also reveals gaps in representing anti-European, Eurosceptic, and alternative perspectives.

**Rhetorical framing:** K&P use *logos* (identifying gaps) to build *ethos* (as rigorous reviewers) and implicitly appeal to *pathos* (fairness, representation).

| Pro-European bias | Official narratives privileged | Certain causes emphasized | Certain voices amplified |
| Underrepresented alternatives | Eurosceptic, critical, bottom-up absent | Gaps in evidence | Whose story counts? |

### The Pursuit of Europe

- The European project is both a story of progress and contestation
- There are ongoing tensions and ambivalences in how Europe is narrated
- The project is constantly being reshaped by crises and historical events
- Multiple narratives coexist and compete

**Rhetorical note:** "Progress and contestation" = *pathos* (hope vs. conflict) + *logos* (competing narratives as structural fact).

---

### Key Themes

1. **Multiple Narratives**
   - **Logos:** Europe narrated through intellectual, institutional, peace-oriented lenses.
   - **Pathos:** Each lens appeals to different values (identity, stability, peace).

2. **Top-Down Bias**
   - **Ethos:** Elites and institutions privileged as sources.
   - **Pathos:** Ordinary people's voices absent.

3. **Pro-European Bias**
   - **Ethos:** Critical and Eurosceptic perspectives underrepresented.
   - **Logos:** Systematic gap in coverage.

4. **Political Interventions**
   - **Ethos:** Scholarship not neutral; scholars as advocates.
   - **Pathos:** Political agendas shape emotional stakes.

5. **Constant Evolution**
   - **Logos:** Crises reshape narratives.
   - **Pathos:** Ongoing uncertainty, stakes.

## Relevance to Course
