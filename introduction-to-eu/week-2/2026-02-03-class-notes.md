# Introduction to the EU Class Notes
## February 3, 2026 Week 2

**Topic:** 1945 to 1954; three strands (economic, political, military); ECSC; NATO; EDC failure.

---

I. Post-WWII Context (1945 to 1949)
   A. Europe in 1945
      1. Devastated by war
      2. Millions displaced
      3. "World of yesterday" gone
   B. Two main goals
      1. Secure peace after two world wars
      2. Rebuild economy (reconstruction, stability)
   C. Key tension
      1. France and Germany repeatedly at war
      2. Challenge: make war unthinkable
      3. Embed cooperation in institutions

II. Three Strands of Cooperation
   A. Economic strand
      1. Trade, aid, recovery
      2. Ex: Marshall Plan, OEEC, ECSC
   B. Political strand
      1. Assembly, human rights
      2. Ex: Council of Europe (Strasbourg)
   C. Military strand
      1. Defense, deterrence
      2. Ex: NATO, EDC (failed)

III. Council of Europe (1949)
   A. Location and symbolism
      1. Strasbourg (France-Germany border)
      2. Symbolically important
   B. Key features
      1. Assembly for discussion between states
      2. Committee of Ministers (executive)
      3. Call for human rights
   C. Important distinction
      1. Council of Europe ≠ EU Council
      2. Separate organization (47 members)
      3. Focus: human rights, democracy, rule of law

IV. Economic Strand Marshall Plan and OEEC
   A. Marshall Plan (1947 to 1948)
      1. US aid for European reconstruction
      2. Huge sums arriving in Europe
      3. Required cooperation to distribute
   B. OEEC (1948)
      1. Created in Paris
      2. 16 European countries
      3. Managed Marshall Plan aid
   C. Result
      1. Doubled output of Western Europe
      2. Mainly West-Central Europe
      3. Rebuilt stable capitalist economies

V. Schuman Declaration (9 May 1950)
   A. Key figures
      1. Robert Schuman (French Foreign Minister)
      2. Jean Monnet (background architect)
   B. Core idea
      1. Pool coal and steel production
      2. France and Germany share control
      3. Make war "materially impossible"
   C. Method
      1. Supranational authority
      2. Above national governments
      3. First step toward deeper integration

VI. ECSC European Coal and Steel Community (1951)
   A. Treaty of Paris
      1. Signed April 1951
      2. Entered force 1952
   B. Members (founding six)
      1. France, Germany, Italy
      2. Belgium, Netherlands, Luxembourg
   C. Significance
      1. First supranational body in Europe
      2. War industries under common control
      3. "Functionalism" sector-by-sector integration

VII. Military Strand NATO (1949)
   A. Context
      1. Cold War beginning
      2. Soviet threat perceived
      3. Western defense needed
   B. Key features
      1. Military alliance
      2. Collective defense (Article 5)
      3. US involvement in Europe
   C. Relationship to EU
      1. Separate from EU institutions
      2. Overlapping membership
      3. Defense remained national/NATO

VIII. EDC European Defense Community (Failed)
   A. The proposal
      1. European army under supranational control
      2. Modeled on ECSC logic
      3. Would integrate military forces
   B. Why it failed
      1. France feared loss of sovereignty
      2. National army under European control
      3. French National Assembly rejected (1954)
   C. Lesson
      1. Supranational works for economics
      2. Defense too sensitive for integration
      3. Sovereignty limits on integration

IX. Key Differences from Post-WWI
   A. After WWI
      1. Germany alone forced to pay reparations
      2. "You lost, you pay" approach
      3. Created resentment
   B. After WWII
      1. Multilateralization shared responsibility
      2. Shared institutions
      3. Germany included, not singled out
   C. Result
      1. More stable peace
      2. Integration instead of punishment
      3. Foundation for EU

X. 30-Second Cold Call Version.
A. 3-Line Speaking Frame.
   1. TOPIC: STATE THE CORE QUESTION IN ONE LINE.
   2. LOGIC: GIVE 2 DRIVERS/FACTORS THAT EXPLAIN THE ANSWER.
   3. ACTION: END WITH ONE DECISION OR IMPLICATION.
B. Delivery Rule.
   1. 25-30 SECONDS MAX.
   2. NO BACKGROUND STORY; GO STRAIGHT TO CLAIM + EVIDENCE + CLOSE.

CI. Oral Answer Template (60-90 Seconds, Class Use).
A. Opening.
   1. STATE THE CORE QUESTION OR TOPIC IN ONE LINE.
   2. STATE YOUR MAIN CLAIM/THESIS IN ONE LINE.
B. Logic.
   1. GIVE 2-3 KEY FACTORS OR DRIVERS.
   2. LINK EACH FACTOR TO THE CLAIM (CAUSE -> EFFECT).
C. Action Or Implication.
   1. END WITH ONE DECISION, RECOMMENDATION, OR CONSEQUENCE.
   2. CLOSE CLEARLY WITHOUT ADDING NEW IDEAS.
D. Delivery Rule.
   1. 60-90 SECONDS.
   2. CLAIM -> EVIDENCE -> CLOSE.
