 C. DIFFERENT DIMENSIONS.
  1. ECONOMIC DIMENSION = MOST COMPLETE (COMMON MARKET, POLICIES).

V. KEY AGREEMENTS AND POLICIES (EXAMPLES).

 C. EXAM EXPECTATION.
  1. BE ABLE TO IDENTIFY MAIN AGREEMENTS FOR EACH STAGE.

VI. EXAM STRATEGY (WHAT PROFESSOR EXPECTS).
 A. BUILD OVERVIEW FIRST.
  1. DRAW TIMELINE OF INTEGRATION ON SCRAP PAPER.
  2. NOTE MAJOR TREATIES, ENLARGEMENTS, CRISES.
 B. READ TEXTS SLOWLY.
  1. UNDERLINE CLUES, DATES, NAMES, KEY CONCEPTS.
  2. LINK EACH TEXT TO AUTHOR, PERIOD, AND TOPIC.
 C. MATCH TEXT TO QUESTION.
  1. IDENTIFY WHICH TEXT ANSWERS WHICH QUESTION MOST DIRECTLY.
  2. AVOID GUESSING WHEN MATCH IS OBVIOUS.
 D. STRUCTURE ANSWERS.
  1. NAME CONCEPT OR AUTHOR FIRST.
  2. DEFINE IN SHORT PHRASE, THEN GIVE TEXT EVIDENCE.

VII. PRACTICAL TIPS FROM CLASS.
 A. PREPARATION BEFORE EXAM.
  1. SLEEP BEFORE, DO NOT PULL ALL‑NIGHTER.
  2. ARRIVE WITH CLEAR TIMELINE AND INSTITUTION MAP IN HEAD.
 B. DURING EXAM.
  1. USE FULL NINETY MINUTES, DO NOT RUSH.
  2. RE‑READ QUESTIONS AFTER DRAFT ANSWERS.
 C. ATTITUDE.
  1. DO NOT PANIC ABOUT DETAILS.
  2. FOCUS ON SHOWING UNDERSTANDING OF PROCESS OF INTEGRATION.

X. 30-Second Cold Call Version.
A. 3-Line Speaking Frame.
   1. TOPIC: STATE THE CORE QUESTION IN ONE LINE.
   2. LOGIC: GIVE 2 DRIVERS/FACTORS THAT EXPLAIN THE ANSWER.
   3. ACTION: END WITH ONE DECISION OR IMPLICATION.
B. Delivery Rule.
   1. 25-30 SECONDS MAX.
   2. NO BACKGROUND STORY; GO STRAIGHT TO CLAIM + EVIDENCE + CLOSE.

DI. Oral Answer Template (60-90 Seconds, Class Use).
A. Opening.
   1. STATE THE CORE QUESTION OR TOPIC IN ONE LINE.
   2. STATE YOUR MAIN CLAIM/THESIS IN ONE LINE.
B. Logic.
   1. GIVE 2-3 KEY FACTORS OR DRIVERS.
   2. LINK EACH FACTOR TO THE CLAIM (CAUSE -> EFFECT).
C. Action Or Implication.
   1. END WITH ONE DECISION, RECOMMENDATION, OR CONSEQUENCE.
   2. CLOSE CLEARLY WITHOUT ADDING NEW IDEAS.
D. Delivery Rule.
   1. 60-90 SECONDS.
   2. CLAIM -> EVIDENCE -> CLOSE.
