# Introduction to the EU - Class Notes
## March 24, 2026 - Week 9

I. CJEU Structure And Core Role.
   A. Institutional Setup.
      1. CJEU = COURT OF JUSTICE + GENERAL COURT.
      2. ECJ AS TOP COURT IN EU LEGAL ORDER.
   B. Treaty Mandate.
      1. ENSURE INTERPRETATION/APPLICATION OF EU TREATIES.
      2. MAINTAIN COHERENCE OF UNION LAW ACROSS MEMBER STATES.
   C. Functional Tools.
      1. PRELIMINARY RULINGS FOR NATIONAL COURTS.
      2. REVIEW OF MEMBER-STATE COMPLIANCE + INSTITUTIONAL LEGALITY.

II. Integration Through Law (Davies Framing).
   A. Foundational Case-Law Path.
      1. DIRECT EFFECT (VAN GEND EN LOOS).
      2. PRIMACY (COSTA V ENEL; LATER CONSOLIDATION).
   B. Interpretive Schools Discussed.
      1. LEGAL ACCOUNTS (CASE-LAW BUILDOUT).
      2. POLITICAL/IR ACCOUNTS (STATE STRATEGY + BACKLASH RISK).
      3. SOCIOLOGICAL ACCOUNTS (TRANSNATIONAL LEGAL NETWORKS).
   C. Key Revision Point.
      1. INTEGRATION NOT LINEAR/SMOOTH.
      2. NATIONAL RESISTANCE PRESENT FROM EARLY PERIODS.

III. Primacy Vs National Constitutional Orders.
   A. Structural Tension.
      1. EU LAW CLAIMS PRIMACY.
      2. NATIONAL CONSTITUTIONAL COURTS ASSERT LIMITS.
   B. Practical Arena.
      1. PRELIMINARY-RULING SYSTEM DEPENDS ON NATIONAL COURTS.
      2. COMPLIANCE CAN BE PARTIAL/CONTESTED IN SENSITIVE AREAS.
   C. Class Emphasis.
      1. EU NOT FULL FEDERAL STATE.
      2. LEGAL AUTHORITY OPERATES THROUGH MULTI-LEVEL NEGOTIATION.

IV. Duff-Style Reform Agenda (As Discussed).
   A. Institutional Capacity Problem.
      1. COURT OVERLOAD/CASE PRESSURE.
      2. NEED TO IMPROVE ACCESS + EFFICIENCY.
   B. Jurisdictional Limits Raised.
      1. GAPS AROUND CFSP/SECURITY-RELATED OVERSIGHT.
      2. LIMITED REACH IN SOME AREAS OF UNION ACTION.
   C. Reform Direction.
      1. TARGETED TREATY-LEVEL UPDATES.
      2. STRONGER ENFORCEMENT PATHS WHERE RULE-OF-LAW RISKS EMERGE.

V. Rule Of Law Backsliding (Hungary/Poland Discussion).
   A. Core Concern.
      1. DEMOCRATIC EROSION VIA INSTITUTIONAL REENGINEERING.
      2. JUDICIAL INDEPENDENCE + CHECKS/BALANCES PRESSURED.
   B. EU Enforcement Problem.
      1. ARTICLE 7 POLITICALLY HARD TO FULLY APPLY.
      2. ENFORCEMENT OFTEN SELECTIVE/INCONSISTENT.
   C. Political Economy Layer.
      1. DOMESTIC SUPPORT BUILT THROUGH WELFARE + IDENTITY POLITICS.
      2. SANCTIONS CAN BACKFIRE IF FRAMED AS EXTERNAL INTERFERENCE.

VI. Transparency, Legitimacy, And Accountability.
   A. Deliberation Model.
      1. SINGLE-VOICE JUDGMENTS; NON-PUBLIC INTERNAL DELIBERATION.
      2. PROTECTION OF JUDICIAL INDEPENDENCE VS TRANSPARENCY COST.
   B. Debate In Class.
      1. MORE TRANSPARENCY MAY IMPROVE PUBLIC TRUST.
      2. COURT AUTHORITY STILL DEPENDS ON MEMBER-STATE ACCEPTANCE.

VII. Session Takeaways.
   A. Big Picture.
      1. CJEU CENTRAL TO INTEGRATION, BUT POLITICALLY CONSTRAINED.
      2. RULE-OF-LAW ENFORCEMENT = LEGAL + POLITICAL PROBLEM.
   B. Exam-Ready Angles.
      1. DIRECT EFFECT + PRIMACY + PRELIMINARY RULING TRIAD.
      2. WHY ENFORCEMENT GAPS PERSIST DESPITE FORMAL EU VALUES.
