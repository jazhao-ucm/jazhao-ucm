European People's Party (EPP) & {\raggedright\textbf{Lexicon:} supply, investment, simplification, legal certainty.\par
\textbf{Tools:} permit/planning simplification, buyer tax incentives, construction support.\par
\textbf{EU stance:} cautious-supportive (EU as facilitator, not heavy regulator).\par

Progressive Alliance of Socialists and Democrats (S\&D) & {\raggedright\textbf{Lexicon:} affordability, social emergency, tenants' rights, public housing.\par
\textbf{Tools:} public/affordable housing funding, tenant protections, short-term rental regulation.\par
\textbf{EU stance:} pro-EU intervention.\par
\textbf{Pass/block:} pass earmarked social/affordable measures; block market-only approach.} \\
\midrule
Renew Europe (Renew) & {\raggedright\textbf{Lexicon:} investment gap, affordability, first-time buyers, market efficiency.\par
\textbf{Tools:} investment tools, tax barrier removal for first-time buyers, incentive-based measures.\par
\textbf{EU stance:} pro-EU coordination.\par
\textbf{Pass/block:} pass investment-led measures; block strict price/rent controls.} \\
\midrule
Greens/European Free Alliance (Greens/EFA) & {\raggedright\textbf{Lexicon:} right to housing, anti-speculation, public housing, Housing First.\par
\textbf{Tools:} public/social housing investment, anti-speculation rules, homelessness-first model.\par
\textbf{EU stance:} strong pro-EU intervention.\par
\textbf{Pass/block:} pass stronger public-housing and anti-speculation rules; block weak compromise outcomes.} \\
\midrule
The Left in the European Parliament (The Left) & {\raggedright\textbf{Lexicon:} financialisation, social right, rent caps, decommodification.\par
\textbf{Tools:} rent caps, large public/cooperative housing, stronger anti-eviction protections.\par
\textbf{EU stance:} pro-EU, but critical of EU fiscal constraints on public investment.\par
\textbf{Pass/block:} pass tenant/public-housing protections; block diluted or market-dominant packages.} \\
\midrule
European Conservatives and Reformists (ECR) & {\raggedright\textbf{Lexicon:} subsidiarity, national competence, simplification, private property.\par
\textbf{Tools:} deregulation, planning simplification, national/local decision control.\par
\textbf{EU stance:} skeptical of binding EU housing rules.\par
\textbf{Pass/block:} pass simplification and national-control measures; block EU-wide rent/social mandates.} \\
\midrule
Patriots for Europe (PfE) & {\raggedright\textbf{Lexicon:} national sovereignty, family burden, energy cost pressure.\par
\textbf{Tools:} national-level policy, lower family burden, reduced cost pressure framing.\par
\textbf{EU stance:} very skeptical of EU mandates.\par
\textbf{Pass/block:} pass sovereignty-focused and cost-relief measures; block binding EU housing intervention.} \\
\midrule
Europe of Sovereign Nations (ESN) & {\raggedright\textbf{Lexicon:} sovereignty, anti-bureaucracy, national priorities.\par
\textbf{Tools:} redirect funding to national/regional priorities, minimal EU market intervention.\par
\textbf{EU stance:} very skeptical/hostile to EU-level housing regulation.\par
\textbf{Pass/block:} pass national-priority framing; block supranational regulatory expansion.} \\
\midrule
Non-Inscrits (NI) & {\raggedright\textbf{Lexicon:} no single line; depends on individual MEP.\par
\textbf{Tools:} case-by-case (individual MEP position).\par
\textbf{EU stance:} mixed/variable.\par
\textbf{Pass/block:} mixed/variable by member.} \\
