\section*{\color{eublue}Wrap-up (write this last)}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Similarities (3):}
  (1) Supply urgency: almost all groups treat the housing crisis as an emergency about availability (even if they disagree on the best fix).
  (2) Targeting vulnerable people: most groups connect housing to protecting vulnerable groups/tenants and affordability (including Housing First-style thinking).
  (3) Short-term rental regulation: multiple groups frame Airbnb/short-term rentals as a driver of local affordability problems (even if they disagree on how strict rules should be).
\item \textbf{Differences (3):}
  (1) Rent control: S\&D/Greens/The Left push rent-caps/controls, while EPP/Renew/ECR resist them ``because'' they frame rent control as harmful to investment/supply.
  (2) EU competence: S\&D/Greens/The Left want an EU strategy with funding/regulation ``because'' they see housing as a structural, cross-border failure; ECR/PfE/ESN push subsidiarity/national control ``because'' they frame housing as national sovereignty.
  (3) Green mandates: Greens (and often S\&D) push stricter energy/renovation requirements ``because'' they connect energy poverty with long-term habitability; PfE/ESN resist ``because'' they frame these rules as costly bureaucracy for families.
\item \textbf{One Vienna connection}: The Left + Greens match Vienna's non-market/prevention logic best; S\&D aligns with Vienna on the need for large-scale public/affordable investment; EPP/Renew/ECR clash with Vienna because they prioritize market-led supply/investment over keeping a large share of housing out of speculation.
\end{itemize}

\section*{\color{eublue}VII. Extra institutional recap (from transcript) --- Democratic deficit}
\begin{itemize}
\item Democratic deficit (plain words): the EU can make big decisions, but citizens don't always feel they can directly influence those decisions.
\item Early integration: decisions were shaped largely via national contexts and big institutions (especially the Council); Commission/Council had strong role, while EP was supposed to represent citizens but had limited power.
\item Why it matters for today's debate lens: connects ``institutions + who controls law-making'' to workshop tasks (parliament vocabulary, procedures, groups).
\end{itemize}
