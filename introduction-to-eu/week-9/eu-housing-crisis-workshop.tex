Party & Answer snapshot \\
\midrule
European People's Party (EPP) & \textbf{Lexicon:} supply, investment, simplification, legal certainty. \\
& \textbf{Tools:} permit/planning simplification, buyer tax incentives, construction support. \\
& \textbf{EU stance:} cautious-supportive (EU as facilitator, not heavy regulator). \\
& \textbf{Pass/block:} pass supply-side measures; block rent caps and strong anti-speculation intervention. \\
\midrule
Progressive Alliance of Socialists and Democrats (S\&D) & \textbf{Lexicon:} affordability, social emergency, tenants' rights, public housing. \\
& \textbf{Tools:} public/affordable housing funding, tenant protections, short-term rental regulation. \\
& \textbf{EU stance:} pro-EU intervention. \\
& \textbf{Pass/block:} pass earmarked social/affordable measures; block market-only approach. \\
\midrule
Renew Europe (Renew) & \textbf{Lexicon:} investment gap, affordability, first-time buyers, market efficiency. \\
& \textbf{Tools:} investment tools, tax barrier removal for first-time buyers, incentive-based measures. \\
& \textbf{EU stance:} pro-EU coordination. \\
& \textbf{Pass/block:} pass investment-led measures; block strict price/rent controls. \\
\midrule
Greens/European Free Alliance (Greens/EFA) & \textbf{Lexicon:} right to housing, anti-speculation, public housing, Housing First. \\
& \textbf{Tools:} public/social housing investment, anti-speculation rules, homelessness-first model. \\
& \textbf{EU stance:} strong pro-EU intervention. \\
& \textbf{Pass/block:} pass stronger public-housing and anti-speculation rules; block weak compromise outcomes. \\
\midrule
The Left in the European Parliament (The Left) & \textbf{Lexicon:} financialisation, social right, rent caps, decommodification. \\
& \textbf{Tools:} rent caps, large public/cooperative housing, stronger anti-eviction protections. \\
& \textbf{EU stance:} pro-EU, but critical of EU fiscal constraints on public investment. \\
& \textbf{Pass/block:} pass tenant/public-housing protections; block diluted or market-dominant packages. \\
\midrule
European Conservatives and Reformists (ECR) & \textbf{Lexicon:} subsidiarity, national competence, simplification, private property. \\
& \textbf{Tools:} deregulation, planning simplification, national/local decision control. \\
& \textbf{EU stance:} skeptical of binding EU housing rules. \\
& \textbf{Pass/block:} pass simplification and national-control measures; block EU-wide rent/social mandates. \\
\midrule
Patriots for Europe (PfE) & \textbf{Lexicon:} national sovereignty, family burden, energy cost pressure. \\
& \textbf{Tools:} national-level policy, lower family burden, reduced cost pressure framing. \\
& \textbf{EU stance:} very skeptical of EU mandates. \\
& \textbf{Pass/block:} pass sovereignty-focused and cost-relief measures; block binding EU housing intervention. \\
\midrule
Europe of Sovereign Nations (ESN) & \textbf{Lexicon:} sovereignty, anti-bureaucracy, national priorities. \\
& \textbf{Tools:} redirect funding to national/regional priorities, minimal EU market intervention. \\
& \textbf{EU stance:} very skeptical/hostile to EU-level housing regulation. \\
& \textbf{Pass/block:} pass national-priority framing; block supranational regulatory expansion. \\
\midrule
Non-Inscrits (NI) & \textbf{Lexicon:} no single line; depends on individual MEP. \\
& \textbf{Tools:} case-by-case (individual MEP position). \\
& \textbf{EU stance:} mixed/variable. \\
& \textbf{Pass/block:} mixed/variable by member. \\
\bottomrule
