\section*{\color{eublue}What to extract from Europarl (exact, evidence-first)}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{1 Europarl source link} for the debate/initiative you use (paste URL in your notes).
\item \textbf{Parliament vocabulary} used in the debate (words that show the procedure/argument framing).
\item \textbf{Lexicon + roots}: for each group, list key housing words they repeat + the underlying framing (``what is the housing problem?'' as they define it).
\item \textbf{Policy mechanisms} they emphasize (the ``how'': regulation/funding/targeting, public housing logic, affordability rules, support for buyers, etc.).
\item \textbf{Procedure logic} language: what should \emph{pass} vs what should be \emph{blocked/skipped} (based on whether the proposal can proceed in the parliamentary flow).
\item \textbf{Debate output}: the arguments should stay close to the specific statements you found (not general textbook summaries).
\end{itemize}

\section*{\color{eublue}Housing issue map (pick 1 focus per group)}
\begin{itemize}
\item Supply side: \textbf{construction / new dwellings}, \textbf{housing inventory}, \textbf{planning & delivery}
\item Demand side: \textbf{affordability}, \textbf{rents / prices}, \textbf{buyer access} (first-time buyers, families)
\item Social impact: \textbf{social / public housing}, \textbf{vulnerable groups}, \textbf{homelessness}
\item Market design: \textbf{rental regulation}, \textbf{tax rules}, \textbf{subsidies / targeted support}
\item Governance frame: \textbf{EU competence} (what the EU can/cannot do), \textbf{subsidiarity logic}, \textbf{who implements}
\end{itemize}

\item Identify the \textbf{political groups} involved (who speaks / which positions appear).
\item For each group, extract 4 outputs (lexicon, tools, EU-role stance, procedure logic).
\item Prepare short group presentations (use note-lines + 1 quote slot if possible): \textbf{about 45 minutes total for group work}, then share findings.
\end{enumerate}
