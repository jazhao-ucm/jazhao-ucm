# Introduction to the EU Class Notes
## February 17, 2026 Week 4

**Topic:** EC after oil crisis; merger of Communities; Hague Summit; first enlargement details.

---

I. Merger of Communities (1967)
   A. What merged
      1. ECSC (coal and steel)
      2. EEC (economic)
      3. Euratom (atomic energy)
   B. Result
      1. Single European Community (EC)
      2. 1 July 1967
   C. Customs union
      1. Achieved ahead of schedule
      2. Completed 1968

II. De Gaulle's Resignation (1969)
   A. Impact
      1. After De Gaulle left, integration resumed
      2. "European bandwagon started to roll again"
   B. Context
      1. Luxembourg Compromise had slowed progress
      2. French veto blocked UK
      3. New leadership opened doors

III. Hague Summit (December 1969)
   A. Initiator
      1. Georges Pompidou (French President)
      2. De Gaulle's successor
   B. Issues considered
      1. Enlargement of membership
      2. Economic and monetary union
      3. Technological and social cooperation
      4. Regular Foreign Minister discussions
   C. Outcome
      1. Enlargement agreed June 1971
      2. Effective 1 January 1973

IV. First Enlargement 1973 (Six → Nine)
   A. New members
      1. United Kingdom
      2. Ireland
      3. Denmark
   B. Special arrangements Commonwealth
      1. Eased difficulties for Commonwealth exports
      2. Commonwealth food subject to Community tariffs
   C. Special arrangements Fisheries
      1. New members had extensive fishing fleets
      2. Fisheries policy gave equal access to waters
      3. Would suffer most from competition

V. Referendum Results
   A. Norway 53% against
      1. Fishing industry concerns
      2. Relationship with Scandinavian neighbors
      3. Did not join
   B. Denmark 63% in favor
      1. Agriculture would benefit from CAP
      2. Chief exports to Britain and Germany
   C. Ireland 83% in favor
      1. 70% of exports went to Britain
      2. Logical economic choice

VI. Oil Crisis (1973) Background
   A. Europe's dependence
      1. Prosperity depended on cheap oil
      2. $2/barrel average 1950–1973
      3. Oil = 65% of Western Europe's electricity
   B. Supply situation
      1. Plentiful supply kept prices low
      2. New producers charged low prices
      3. Changed dramatically in 1973

VII. Oil Crisis The Shock
   A. Trigger
      1. Arab-Israeli War (Yom Kippur)
      2. Arab embargo on oil supplies
      3. Four-fold price increase
   B. Immediate effects
      1. Energy shortage
      2. Economic recession
      3. End of post-war "Golden Age"
   C. Stagflation
      1. Inflation + unemployment together
      2. Previously thought impossible
      3. Challenged economic orthodoxy

VIII. European Response
   A. Lack of coordination
      1. No common energy policy
      2. Each country acted alone
      3. France/UK sought bilateral deals
   B. Netherlands problem
      1. Hit hardest by embargo
      2. Other EC members did not help
      3. Exposed divisions
   C. Long-term lessons
      1. Energy security became priority
      2. Need for common approach recognized
      3. But coordination remained difficult

IX. Economic Aftermath
   A. End of Golden Age
      1. 1945–1973 = rapid growth
      2. After 1973 = slower growth
      3. Structural unemployment
   B. Policy challenges
      1. Keynesian approaches questioned
      2. Monetarism gained influence
      3. Welfare state pressures
   C. Integration slowdown
      1. Countries focused on domestic problems
      2. Less appetite for supranational solutions
      3. "Eurosclerosis" period began

X. 30-Second Cold Call Version.
A. 3-Line Speaking Frame.
   1. TOPIC: STATE THE CORE QUESTION IN ONE LINE.
   2. LOGIC: GIVE 2 DRIVERS/FACTORS THAT EXPLAIN THE ANSWER.
   3. ACTION: END WITH ONE DECISION OR IMPLICATION.
B. Delivery Rule.
   1. 25-30 SECONDS MAX.
   2. NO BACKGROUND STORY; GO STRAIGHT TO CLAIM + EVIDENCE + CLOSE.

CI. Oral Answer Template (60-90 Seconds, Class Use).
A. Opening.
   1. STATE THE CORE QUESTION OR TOPIC IN ONE LINE.
   2. STATE YOUR MAIN CLAIM/THESIS IN ONE LINE.
B. Logic.
   1. GIVE 2-3 KEY FACTORS OR DRIVERS.
   2. LINK EACH FACTOR TO THE CLAIM (CAUSE -> EFFECT).
C. Action Or Implication.
   1. END WITH ONE DECISION, RECOMMENDATION, OR CONSEQUENCE.
   2. CLOSE CLEARLY WITHOUT ADDING NEW IDEAS.
D. Delivery Rule.
   1. 60-90 SECONDS.
   2. CLAIM -> EVIDENCE -> CLOSE.
