**Topic:** Guest class preview; 1957 unity; first enlargement 1973; oil crisis; Gibraltar.

I. Guest Expert Preview — Gibraltar
   A. Topic
      1. Spanish accession to EC
      2. British attitude
      3. Gibraltar as case study
   B. Gibraltar context
      1. British overseas territory in Spain
      2. Tax haven
      3. Frequent UK-Spain tensions
   C. 1982 World Cup
      1. Spain's soft power strategy
      2. Present as new, democratic, modern country
      3. Part of accession narrative

II. Recap — Technical Successes of Integration
   A. Economic achievements
      1. Increased production
      2. Countries worked together without war
      3. "Never again" in practice
   B. Social achievements
      1. Movement, rights, identity
      2. Raised standard of living
   C. Film — *Bienvenido Mr. Marshall*
      1. 1953 Spanish film
      2. Town waiting for Marshall aid
      3. Spain did not receive aid like others

III. European Army Controversy (Recap)
   A. French opposition (1950s)
      1. Loss of sovereignty
      2. Military under supranational control
   B. De Gaulle's position
      1. Did not want American influence
      2. "Europe of nations" — each country sovereign
      3. Close to today's far-right position
   C. Current relevance
      1. Trump: "Europe should pay more"
      2. Ukraine: need European defense capability
      3. Macron: "almost time" for European army

IV. How United Was Western Europe by 1957?
   A. Economic dimension
      1. Progress since ECSC and Treaties of Rome
      2. But not all countries in favour
      3. EEC vs. EFTA competing
   B. Political dimension
      1. Most had parliamentary democracy
      2. Spain and Portugal exceptions (dictatorships)
      3. Council of Europe weak; EC mainly economic
   C. Tensions
      1. UK and others feared Six's power
      2. War ended only 12 years before
      3. Many still thought "each country for itself"

V. EFTA — European Free Trade Association
   A. Formation
      1. Founded 1960
      2. UK, Austria, Denmark, Norway, Portugal, Sweden, Switzerland
   B. Purpose
      1. Alternative to EEC
      2. Free trade without supranational institutions
      3. Less integration than EEC
   C. Outcome
      1. Many later joined EEC/EU
      2. Some remain (Norway, Switzerland, Iceland)

VI. First Enlargement — 1973
   A. New members
      1. UK, Ireland, Denmark
      2. Six → Nine
   B. Context
      1. De Gaulle vetoed UK twice (1963, 1967)
      2. After De Gaulle resigned (1969), doors opened
      3. Hague Summit agreed enlargement

VII. Oil Crisis — 1973
   A. Background
      1. Europe depended on cheap oil
      2. $2/barrel average 1950–1973
      3. Oil provided 65% of electricity
   B. The crisis
      1. Arab-Israeli War
      2. Arab oil embargo
      3. Four-fold price increase
   C. Effects
      1. End of "Golden Age"
      2. Recession
      3. Stagflation (inflation + unemployment)

VIII. European Response to Oil Crisis
   A. Initial response
      1. No common energy policy
      2. Each country for itself
      3. France/UK sought bilateral deals with Arabs
   B. Problems
      1. Lack of solidarity
      2. Netherlands affected most
      3. Crisis exposed divisions
   C. Lesson
      1. Interdependence without coordination dangerous
      2. Led to later energy cooperation discussions
