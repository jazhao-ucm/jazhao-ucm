# Introduction to the EU — Class Notes
## March 10, 2026 — Week 7

**Topic:** Exam preparation; text analysis; Ribera vs. von der Leyen; international law; multilateralism; EU unity.

---

I. Exam Format
   A. Structure
      1. ~2 hours
      2. 2–3 short texts
      3. Could be speeches, news, historical extracts
   B. Prompts
      1. Provide context for statement
      2. Identify debate/author/moment
      3. Explain specific meaning of key terms
      4. Compare/contrast texts
   C. What professor wants
      1. Place text in time and space
      2. Link to course blocks
      3. Use course vocabulary

II. How to Approach Text Analysis
   A. Not just summary
      1. Explain context
      2. Identify main argument
      3. Connect to course themes
   B. Think of it as movie
      1. Know whole story first
      2. Identify main scenes (treaties, crises)
      3. Exam text = one frame
   C. Key skill
      1. Show you understand "movie"
      2. Place "scene" inside it

III. Example Text — Ribera vs. von der Leyen
   A. Context
      1. Public disagreement on Middle East
      2. Ribera criticizes von der Leyen's words
   B. Ribera's argument
      1. "Respect for international law is basic premise"
      2. Von der Leyen seemed to question this
      3. Links to European security
   C. Issues raised
      1. International law vs. politics
      2. Who speaks for Europe?
      3. EU unity and credibility

IV. Key Concepts in Text
   A. International law
      1. Rules states agree to follow
      2. UN Charter, Geneva Conventions
      3. Post-1945 order
   B. Multilateralism
      1. Many countries working together
      2. Opposite of unilateral action
      3. Creates balanced agreements
   C. EU unity
      1. "Speak with one voice"
      2. Common foreign policy
      3. Credibility with partners

V. Costa Text — Multilateralism
   A. Main argument
      1. Defends multilateralism
      2. Criticizes one-sided US policy
   B. Example used
      1. EU united against US threats to Greenland
      2. Unity gives EU weight
   C. Theme
      1. Europe must act together
      2. Not just follow another power

VII. Exam Preparation Strategy
   A. Follow chronological blocks
      1. After WWI/WWII
      2. Founding treaties
      3. Enlargements

   B. For each block, know
      1. Key treaties, dates, actors
      2. How they connect to later debates
   C. Level expected
      1. Intro course
      2. Know milestones and main ideas
      3. Extra readings optional

IX. Key Vocabulary for Exam
   A. International law
      1. Rules between states
      2. UN-based order
   B. Multilateralism
      1. Many countries deciding together
      2. Cooperation over unilateralism
   C. Discourse
