## Rhetorical Framework (Ethos, Logos, Pathos)

| Appeal | Meaning | Warlouzet's Use |
|--------|---------|-----------------|
| **Ethos** | Credibility, authority, trust | Institutional history; synthesis of treaties and summits; scholarly narrative |
| **Logos** | Logic, structure, cause-and-effect | Chronological arc (crisis → sclerosis → renewal); institutional development |
| **Pathos** | Emotion, stakes, human dimension | Crisis, paralysis, renewal; oil shock, recession, revival of integration |

**Key insight:** Warlouzet structures the chapter around a *logos* arc—turning point (Hague) → crisis (oil) → stagnation (Eurosclerosis) → renewal (SEA, Maastricht). *Pathos* appears in the drama of crisis and recovery; *ethos* in the authoritative treatment of treaties and summits.

## Summary & Key Points

- The Hague Summit (1969) marked a turning point, setting agenda for enlargement and economic and monetary union
- First enlargement in 1973 (UK, Ireland, Denmark) expanded Community from 6 to 9 members
- The 1973 oil crisis revealed weaknesses in European coordination and led to economic recession
- Period of "Eurosclerosis" in 1970s–1980s with slow integration progress
- Direct elections to European Parliament began in 1979, increasing its legitimacy
- The Single European Act (1986) and Maastricht Treaty (1992) marked major steps forward in integration
- This period saw transition from European Community to European Union

**Rhetorical note:** Narrative arc—turning point → crisis → stagnation → renewal—builds *pathos* (hope, setback, recovery) on a *logos* structure (timeline, institutions).

## Main Arguments

### I. The Hague Summit as Turning Point

After De Gaulle's resignation, The Hague Summit in December 1969 set a new agenda focusing on enlargement, economic and monetary union, and deeper cooperation. This marked the end of the paralysis period.

**Rhetorical profile:**
- **Logos:** Cause (de Gaulle's exit) → effect (new agenda); end of paralysis.
- **Pathos:** "Turning point," "end of paralysis"—relief, new momentum.
- **Ethos:** Summit as authoritative moment; institutional framing.

---

### II. First Enlargement and Its Challenges

The 1973 enlargement brought in UK, Ireland, and Denmark, but coincided with economic crisis. The enlargement process revealed the need for special arrangements (Commonwealth, fisheries) and showed how expansion complicated integration.

**Rhetorical profile:**
- **Logos:** Enlargement + crisis = complications; special arrangements as evidence.
- **Ethos:** Institutional complexity; Warlouzet as guide to negotiations.
- **Pathos:** Ambivalence—expansion as achievement and burden.

---

### III. The 1973 Oil Crisis

The oil crisis revealed the Community's weakness—inability to coordinate a common response. Each country acted individually, showing limits of integration and leading to economic recession and stagflation.

**Rhetorical profile:**
- **Pathos:** Crisis, vulnerability, failure; "each country acted individually" = fragmentation.
- **Logos:** Cause (oil shock) → effect (no coordination) → consequence (recession, stagflation).
- **Ethos:** Crisis as test of integration; limits exposed.

---

### IV. From Eurosclerosis to Renewal

The 1970s–1980s saw slow progress ("Eurosclerosis"), but also important developments like direct elections to Parliament (1979) and the Single European Act (1986), which revived integration.

**Rhetorical profile:**
- **Logos:** Stagnation → partial progress (direct elections) → breakthrough (SEA).
- **Pathos:** "Eurosclerosis" = disease metaphor; "revived" = renewal, hope.
- **Ethos:** Warlouzet balances setback with progress; nuanced narrative.

---

### V. Maastricht Treaty

The Maastricht Treaty (1992) created the European Union and established the framework for Economic and Monetary Union, representing a major step forward in integration.

**Rhetorical profile:**
- **Logos:** Treaty → new institutions (EU, EMU framework); structural change.
- **Pathos:** "Major step forward"—achievement, culmination.
- **Ethos:** Maastricht as landmark; institutional milestone.

## Critical Analysis

### Strengths

- Provides comprehensive overview of a crucial period spanning from crisis to major treaty achievements

**Rhetorical note:** Strengths align with *logos* (comprehensive arc) and *ethos* (scholarly synthesis).

---

### Weaknesses or Limitations

- May focus on institutional developments rather than social or popular perspectives

**Rhetorical note:** Connects to Krawatzek & Pestel—top-down bias; *pathos* of ordinary Europeans (oil crisis, recession) underrepresented.

---

### Questions Raised

- How did the oil crisis affect ordinary Europeans?
- What were the social consequences of economic recession?

**Rhetorical framing:**
- **Ethos:** Whose experience counts?
- **Pathos:** Whose story is told?
- **Logos:** What evidence is missing?
