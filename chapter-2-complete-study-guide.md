## üìå INTRODUCTION TO CROSS-CULTURAL COMPETENCE

### What is Culture?
**Definition:** "The set of shared beliefs, values, and other norms that distinguish one group of people from another."

### Why Culture Matters in Business
- Working with people from different cultures is **more complicated** than working with people from the same culture
- **Language is only one aspect** - cultural norms, values, and behaviors are equally important
- Understanding culture makes international business **easier and more successful**
- Cultural competence is essential for managing international operations

### The Reality of International Business
- Companies operate across **national and international boundaries**
- **Cannot avoid** dealing with different cultures when doing business globally
- Cultural differences affect:
  - Communication styles
  - Decision-making processes
  - Management approaches
  - Business relationships
  - Trust and cooperation

## üåç GEERT HOFSTEDE'S CULTURAL DIMENSIONS FRAMEWORK

### About Geert Hofstede
- **Dutch interculturalist** and one of the most noted academics exploring culture
- **IBM psychologist (1967-1973)**
- Collected and analyzed data from **over 100,000 individuals** from **over 50 countries**
- Developed a model identifying cultural dimensions to differentiate cultures
- Framework widely used in international business

### The Six Cultural Dimensions

#### 1. Power Distance Index (PDI)
**Definition:** The extent to which members who are less powerful in a society accept and expect that power is distributed unequally.

**HIGH Power Distance Cultures:**
- Members accept power distance as part of social order
- Determines what is right and wrong
- More powerful members perceive subordinates as **unequal**
- **Subordinates fear** the more powerful members
- Not much trust among co-workers
- Majority of people are dependent on others
- **Subordinates are blamed** for mistakes
- Social inequalities are accepted
- Every person has a high or low place in social order (protected by law)
- Acceptance of privileges that come with power

**Examples of High PDI Countries:** Malaysia, Philippines, Mexico, India, China

**LOW Power Distance Cultures:**
- Power is exerted **only where necessary**
- People are equal; social inequalities considered obsolete
- Cooperation based on principle of **solidarity**
- **Subordinates considered as older, more experienced colleagues**
- Co-workers prone to trust one another
- **The system is blamed** for mistakes (not individuals)
- Everyone should have equal rights
- Strong and weak members coexist in harmony

**Examples of Low PDI Countries:** Austria, Denmark, Netherlands, Sweden

**Business Implications:**
- **High PDI:** Hierarchical structures, top-down decision making, formal communication
- **Low PDI:** Flat organizations, participative decision making, informal communication
- **Critical for managers:** Adapt leadership style to cultural context

---

#### 2. Individualism vs. Collectivism
**Definition:** The degree to which people prefer to act as individuals rather than as members of groups.

**INDIVIDUALISM (High Score):**
- "I" consciousness
- Individual achievement valued
- Self-reliance important
- Personal goals prioritized
- Direct communication style

**COLLECTIVISM (Low Score):**
- "We" consciousness
- Group harmony valued
- Interdependence important
- Group goals prioritized
- Indirect communication to maintain harmony

**Business Implications:**
- Hiring practices (individual merit vs. group connections)
- Motivation strategies (individual rewards vs. team bonuses)
- Decision-making (individual vs. consensus)

---

#### 3. Masculinity vs. Femininity
**Definition:** The distribution of emotional roles between genders and preference for achievement vs. quality of life.

**MASCULINE Cultures:**
- Achievement, success, competition valued
- Live to work
- Material success important
- Clear gender roles

**FEMININE Cultures:**
- Quality of life, relationships, caring valued
- Work to live
- Work-life balance important
- Fluid gender roles

**Business Implications:**
- Work-life balance expectations
- Competitive vs. collaborative environments
- Motivation factors

---

#### 4. Uncertainty Avoidance Index (UAI)
**Definition:** The extent to which members of a culture feel threatened by ambiguous or unknown situations.

**HIGH Uncertainty Avoidance:**
- Need for rules and structure
- Resistance to change
- Preference for predictability
- Detailed planning required

**LOW Uncertainty Avoidance:**
- Comfortable with ambiguity
- Open to change and innovation
- Flexibility preferred
- Adaptable to situations

**Business Implications:**
- Contract detail and formality
- Innovation vs. tradition
- Risk-taking behavior

---

#### 5. Long-Term vs. Short-Term Orientation
**Definition:** The extent to which a society values long-term planning and perseverance versus short-term results.

**LONG-TERM Orientation:**
- Future-focused
- Persistence and perseverance
- Delayed gratification
- Adaptability valued

**SHORT-TERM Orientation:**
- Present and past focused
- Quick results expected
- Immediate gratification
- Tradition valued

**Business Implications:**
- Investment horizons
- Performance evaluation timeframes
- Strategic planning approaches

---

#### 6. Indulgence vs. Restraint
**Definition:** The extent to which a society values the satisfaction of human needs and desires.

**INDULGENCE (High Score):**
- Gratification of desires allowed
- Enjoying life and having fun important
- Freedom of expression valued
- **In the simulation: Countries with high Indulgence scores are MORE ATTRACTIVE** for family restaurants

**RESTRAINT (Low Score):**
- Gratification of desires suppressed
- Strict social norms
- Duty and obligation prioritized
- Self-control valued

**Business Implications for Restaurant Industry:**
- Dining out behavior
- Spending on leisure
- Consumer attitudes toward casual dining
- Market attractiveness

## üìä CULTURAL DISTANCE CALCULATION

### What is Cultural Distance?
**In the simulation:** Cultural Distance between two countries is calculated based on the **sum of the differences in scores on each of the six dimensions of culture.**

### Formula Concept:
```
Cultural Distance = 
|Country A PDI - Country B PDI| +
|Country A Individualism - Country B Individualism| +
|Country A Masculinity - Country B Masculinity| +
|Country A UAI - Country B UAI| +
|Country A LTO - Country B LTO| +
|Country A Indulgence - Country B Indulgence|
```

### Why Cultural Distance Matters:
- **Greater cultural distance = Greater challenges** in international operations
- Affects:
  - Communication difficulties
  - Management complexity
  - Employee training needs
  - Adaptation requirements
  - **Entry mode selection** (greater distance ‚Üí more careful entry)

### Business Strategy Implications:
- **Low cultural distance:** Easier to transfer business practices, lower adaptation costs
- **High cultural distance:** Need more localization, higher training investment, consider local partners

## üìà MARKET ATTRACTIVENESS FACTORS

### GDP and Population Indicators

#### Gross Domestic Product (GDP) per Capita
- **Measure of income level** of people in the country
- Higher GDP per capita = Greater purchasing power
- Indicator of ability to spend on dining out

#### GDP Growth Forecast
- **Indicator of expected market growth**
- Based on International Monetary Fund data
- Shows market potential and opportunity

#### Population Growth
- Combined with GDP per capita and GDP growth
- **Key indicators of market attractiveness** for family restaurant market
- Growing population + rising income = expanding market

### The Value Creation Chain

**Professor's Framework:**
```
Raw Materials ‚Üí Transform ‚Üí Manufacturing ‚Üí Sell ‚Üí Customers
```

**Key Points:**
- Need **good and strong** transformation capabilities
- Requires **good manufacturing and logistics**
- Must know **how to sell** and **how to move products**
- Each step adds value
- End goal: satisfy customer needs profitably

**Pricing Strategy:**
- **Sell better if cheaper** than competition (price competition)
- **Sell higher** if you have superior value (differentiation)
- Balance: **Buy low, sell high**
- Consider what **people want** and are willing to pay for
- Quality and reliability matter - "people want good quality things that are reliable"

## ‚ö†Ô∏è COUNTRY RISK FACTORS

### 1. Country Risk
**Definition:** A broad measure indicating risks from:
- Economic changes
- Political changes
- General instability

**Business Impact:**
- Affects investment safety
- Influences entry mode choice
- Requires contingency planning

### 2. Trade Barriers (Tariffs)
**Impact:** Cost of importing ingredients from home country

**Considerations:**
- Higher tariffs = Higher costs = Lower margins
- May require local sourcing
- Affects pricing strategy
- Can make market less attractive

### 3. Industry Rivalry
**Definition:** The intensity of competition in a market

**High Rivalry:**
- More competitors
- Price pressure
- Need strong differentiation
- Lower profit margins

**Low Rivalry:**
- Fewer competitors
- More pricing power
- Easier market entry
- Higher profit potential

## üí≠ POTENTIAL EXAM QUESTIONS & ANSWERS

### Question 1: Explain Hofstede's Power Distance dimension and its business implications.

**Answer Framework:**
- **Definition:** Extent to which less powerful members accept unequal power distribution
- **High PDI:** Hierarchical structures, subordinates blamed for mistakes, fear of superiors, formal communication
- **Low PDI:** Flat organizations, system blamed for mistakes, trust among colleagues, informal communication
- **Business implications:** 
  - Management style adaptation required
  - High PDI ‚Üí top-down decisions, respect hierarchy
  - Low PDI ‚Üí participative decisions, empower employees
- **Examples:** Malaysia (high PDI) vs. Denmark (low PDI)

### Question 2: How is cultural distance calculated and why does it matter for international business?

**Answer Framework:**
- **Calculation:** Sum of differences across all six Hofstede dimensions
- **Significance:** Greater cultural distance = greater complexity
- **Business impacts:**
  - Communication challenges increase
  - Higher adaptation costs
  - More training needed
  - Entry mode selection affected
- **Strategy:** High distance ‚Üí consider local partners, more localization needed

### Question 3: What factors make a country attractive for a restaurant chain expansion?

**Answer Framework:**
- **Economic factors:**
  - High GDP per capita (purchasing power)
  - Strong GDP growth (market potential)
  - Population growth (expanding customer base)
- **Cultural factors:**
  - High Indulgence score (dining out behavior)
  - Cultural distance from home (adaptation needs)
- **Risk factors:**
  - Low country risk (stability)
  - Low trade barriers (cost of imports)
  - Moderate industry rivalry (competition level)
- **Integration:** Must balance all factors for optimal decision

### Question 4: Compare high vs. low uncertainty avoidance cultures and their business implications.

**Answer Framework:**
- **High UAI:**
  - Need detailed rules and procedures
  - Resistance to change
  - Prefer predictability
  - Detailed contracts required
  - **Business approach:** Provide clear guidelines, detailed plans, formal processes
- **Low UAI:**
  - Comfortable with ambiguity
  - Open to innovation
  - Flexible and adaptable
  - Less formal contracts
  - **Business approach:** Encourage innovation, allow flexibility, empower decision-making
- **Example application:** Product launches, change management

### Question 5: What are the essential skills for managing people across cultures?

**Answer Framework:**
- **Language:** Basic communication requirement
- **Empathy:** Understanding different perspectives
- **Respect:** Critical for relationships, especially in high PDI cultures
- **Communication:** Beyond language - context, non-verbal cues, style adaptation
- **Additional considerations:**
  - Age/experience in high PDI cultures
  - Train, hire, and support approach
  - Adaptability to local norms
- **Success factors:** Cannot rely on control; must build relationships and trust

- **Have contingency plans:** Prepare for unexpected events
