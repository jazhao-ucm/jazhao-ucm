## üìö Key Concepts

### 1. Globalization vs. Slowbalization

**Globalization:**
- Free movement of goods, services, capital across borders
- High production + high exportation
- Winners: China, India, Indonesia
- Characterized by interconnected economies

**Slowbalization:**
- The slowing down of globalization
- Countries becoming more protectionist
- Reduced exports/imports
- Focus on localization over globalization
- Quote: "Doing more, much less, doing something well"

---

### 2. Economic Complexity

**Definition:** The ability to transform raw materials into complex products using technology, innovation, and R&D

**Examples:**
- Raw materials ‚Üí Complex products (computers, iPhones)
- Complex products = Higher selling prices
- Requires: Technology, innovation, research & development

**Top Countries in Economic Complexity:**
- USA
- Britain
- Netherlands
- UK
- All have strong innovation and technology sectors

**Key Point:** Innovation in technology is the difference between economic complexity and global distribution. To export & import diverse products requires economic complexity.

**Africa's Status:** Currently lagging behind in economic complexity

---

### 3. Major Global Players

#### China & India - The Globalization Winners

**Why They're Winning:**
- **Demographics:** Massive populations
- **Production:** High production capacity
- **Exports:** China = **world's biggest exporter**
- **Labor:** Cheap labor force (e.g., working for $2/day)
- **Politics:** Single party system (China) = less political friction for business
- **Status:** Ahead of US by years in certain sectors
- **Resources:** Access to natural resources

**Key Statistics:**
- China: Biggest exporter globally
- India: Second largest population, high production
- Both: Top two in population, production, and exploitation

#### USA

- **World's biggest importer**
- Strong in software and technology
- Using tariffs to protect domestic companies and jobs
- Strategy: "If we can't stop China, we slow them down"

#### Indonesia

- Top 3 in GDP (Gross Domestic Product)
- High production capacity
- Significant player in globalization

---

### 4. Localization

**Definition:** Moving from global to local

**Purpose:**
- Companies adapting products/services for specific local markets
- Necessary before internationalization
- "Think global, act local"

**Process:** Localization ‚Üí Internationalization ‚Üí Globalization

---

### 5. Geopolitical & Economic Shifts

#### Western Politics Moving East

- Production and business shifting from Europe/USA to Asia
- Middle East importance (Saudi Arabia)
- Business and production moving away from traditional Western centers

#### Venezuela Situation

- Has **twice the oil reserves of Saudi Arabia**
- Described as "biggest gas station ever" - 100 years of reserves
- Despite reserves, experiencing economic difficulties
- Very poor country despite massive resources

#### Saudi Arabia

- Led by Crown Prince **Mohammed bin Salman (MBS)**
- Royal family (House of Saud)
- 50+ years of business dominance in region
- Very rich country with significant gas reserves
- Young prince, very wealthy

**Key Point:** Access to resources alone doesn't guarantee economic success - Venezuela vs. Saudi Arabia example

---

### 6. Trade & Tariffs

#### US Trade Policy

- **Tariffs on China, Indonesia, and others**
- **Purpose:** Protect domestic companies and jobs
- **Strategy:** "If we can't stop China, we slow them down"
- Using tariffs as protectionist measures

#### Current Conflict

- Countries producing too fast
- "Nobody's gonna stop us from fixing it"
- Competition over production capacity
- Mass production and exportation affecting global market

**Example - France vs. Brazil:**
- France exports meat, chicken, food
- Brazil produces rice and other products cheaper (larger land, lower costs)
- French producers can't compete with Brazilian prices
- Creates tension over trade agreements
- Good vs. bad trade deals debate

---

### 7. The G20

**Composition:** 20 countries with:
- Highest GDP
- Most important for exports/imports
- Best industrialization
- Represents ~50% of global economy (with G7, G5 also mentioned)

**Why 20 countries?**
- They represent the biggest economies
- Most influential in global trade
- Spain is part of G20 (example given)
- When working at G20 summit, you meet with the 20 most important countries

**Key Point:** Not about location - it's about economic power, production, exports/imports, and industrialization

---

### 8. Demographics & Future Projections

#### Current State (2026)

- **Green line = Europe**
- **Blue line = Asia**
- Asia has largest population on Earth
- Europe and North America populations much smaller

#### Next 50-60 Years (3 generations)

**Asia:**
- Will peak then decline
- Currently has billions of people
- Eventually going down

**Africa:**
- Will see massive growth
- Nigeria alone experiencing significant population increase
- Africa will become the dominant demographic region
- "Eventually it'll go down but Africa" - referring to population growth

**Europe:**
- By ~2032: **400 million people total** (27 countries)
- **60% will be over 60 years old**
- Aging population = major economic challenge
- Very small compared to Asia/Africa
- Spain is in the bottom (demographically)

**Implications:**
- Can't produce a lot without workers
- Labor force availability affects pricing
- Demographics impact business and production capacity
- "You cannot produce a lot if you don't have workers"
- Cheap labor force = competitive advantage

---

### 9. GDP & Production

**Gross Domestic Product (GDP):**
- Measurement of production of goods and services annually
- Based on previous year's performance
- Top 3: **China, India, Indonesia**

**Key Factor Missing from Discussion:**
- Not language, demographics, or culture alone
- **Access to resources and innovation/technology** differentiate high-production countries
- Examples: Brazil, Germany, USA, South Korea

**Production Limitations:**
- Huge facilities between countries (e.g., Brazil and France)
- Competition over production capacity
- Predictions show growth rates declining
- UK example: 0.1% growth, been like this for 10 years
