## üìå CORE DEFINITIONS

### Globalization
- **Free movement** of goods, services, and capital across borders
- Characterized by **high production + high exportation**
- **Interconnected economies** working together
- **Winners:** China, India, Indonesia
- Creates global supply chains and integrated markets

### Slowbalization
- The **slowing down of globalization**
- Countries becoming more **protectionist**
- **Reduced exports and imports**
- Focus shifting from **globalization to localization**
- **Current trend** we're experiencing now (2025-2026)
- Driven by trade tensions, tariffs, and national security concerns

### Glocalization
- Moving from **global to local**
- Companies **adapting products/services** for specific local markets
- Necessary step **before internationalization**
- Balance between global presence and local adaptation
- "Think global, act local" approach

## üí° ECONOMIC COMPLEXITY

### Definition
The ability to **transform raw materials into complex products** using:
- Technology
- Innovation
- Research & Development (R&D)

Measures how **advanced and diverse** a country's economy is based on:
- Types of products manufactured
- Export diversity
- Technological sophistication

### Why It Matters
- **Complex products = Higher selling prices**
- Differentiates advanced economies from developing ones
- Key to competitive advantage in global markets
- Indicates economic development level
- Predicts future growth potential

### The Process
```
Raw Materials ‚Üí Technology + Innovation + R&D ‚Üí Complex Products
(e.g., minerals) ‚Üí (manufacturing process) ‚Üí (computers, iPhones, semiconductors)
```

### Examples

**Low Economic Complexity:**
- Country exporting **only raw materials**
- Limited product diversity
- Basic production capabilities
- Lower value-added exports

**High Economic Complexity:**
- Exports **cars, electronics, medical equipment**
- **Semiconductors** (requires advanced knowledge, skilled labor)
- Diverse and sophisticated production
- Higher competitiveness and economic complexity index

### Top Countries in Economic Complexity
1. **USA**
2. **United Kingdom**
3. **Netherlands**
4. **Britain**

**Common Factor:** All have strong innovation and technology sectors

### Regional Differences
- **Africa:** Currently lags behind in economic complexity
- **Europe/North America:** Strong in complex product creation
- **Asia:** Mixed - China/India excel in production volume, but complexity varies

### Key Principle
**More attractive and competitive products = Higher economic complexity index**

### Product Diversity Correlation
Higher product diversity typically indicates:
- More developed economy
- Better positioned for global competition
- Greater resilience to market shocks

**Source:** The Atlas of Economic Complexity

## üöö TRANSPORTATION MODES - COMPARATIVE ANALYSIS

### Road Transport
**Typical Usage:** Door-to-door, mixed cargo, complementary to other modes

**Advantages:**
- Necessary for door-to-door delivery
- Cheaper for short distances
- Flexible routing

**Disadvantages:**
- Limited to continental transport
- Urban congestion issues
- Road damage concerns
- **Weight limits (24.5 tons in Spain)**

---

### Rail Transport
**Typical Usage:** Domestic continental and intercontinental (e.g., **China to Spain train routes**)

**Advantages:**
- Ideal for long distances (**efficient after 300km**)
- Environmentally friendly
- Cost-effective for volume
- Depends on rail infrastructure quality

**Disadvantages:**
- Requires connection to rail system
- Needs large volumes (complete trains)
- Infrastructure varies by country

---

### Air Transport
**Typical Usage:** Fast delivery, high-value goods

**Advantages:**
- **Fast and safe**
- Good for high-value items
- Used by fast fashion (**Inditex example**)
- Good for perishables (fruits)
- Cargo flights or commercial flight cargo

**Disadvantages:**
- **Expensive**
- Size and weight limitations
- **Pay for volumetric weight**

---

### Sea Transport
**Typical Usage:** Bulk shipments, **90% of world shipments**

**Advantages:**
- Ideal for all goods types
- **Most cost-efficient**
- Handles large volumes

**Disadvantages:**
- Inflexible routes
- Long lead times
- Rigid timetables (**ships won't wait for missing containers**)
- **Port-to-port only** (needs other transport modes for door-to-door)

## ‚ö†Ô∏è PROJECT RISK MANAGEMENT

### Four Project Phases

**Phase 1: Conceive (C)**
- Initial planning stage
- Risk starts to increase
- Lower amount at stake
- Conceptualization

**Phase 2: Develop (D)**
- Development stage
- Risk continues rising
- Amount at stake increases
- Detailed planning

**Phase 3: Execute (E)**
- **‚ö†Ô∏è PERIOD OF HIGHEST RISK IMPACT**
- Maximum amount at stake
- Combined risk peaks
- **Production begins**
- Most critical phase

**Phase 4: Finish (F)**
- Risk decreases
- Amount at stake reduces
- Project completion
- Wrap-up activities

### Key Insight
**Risk and amount at stake move inversely:** As you plan and produce, risk impact is highest during execution when the most resources are committed.

## üí≠ POTENTIAL EXAM QUESTIONS & ANSWERS

### Question 1: Define and compare globalization and slowbalization. Provide examples.

**Answer Framework:**
- **Globalization:** Free movement of goods/services/capital, high production+exportation, interconnected economies. Winners: China, India, Indonesia.
- **Slowbalization:** Slowing of globalization due to protectionism, reduced exports/imports, focus on localization. Current trend (2025-2026).
- **Examples:** US tariffs on China (slowbalization), China's export dominance (globalization), Brexit (slowbalization)
- **Causes of slowbalization:** Trade tensions, national security concerns, job protection

### Question 2: What is economic complexity and why does it matter?

**Answer Framework:**
- **Definition:** Ability to transform raw materials into complex products using technology, innovation, and R&D
- **Process:** Raw materials ‚Üí Tech/Innovation/R&D ‚Üí Complex products (semiconductors, electronics)
- **Why it matters:** 
  - Complex products = higher selling prices
  - Indicates economic development
  - Provides competitive advantage
  - Differentiates advanced vs developing economies
- **Examples:** USA, UK, Netherlands (high complexity) vs. Africa (low complexity)

### Question 3: Analyze the demographic shifts and their business implications.

**Answer Framework:**
- **Africa:** Growing from 1.52B to 5.44B (41% by 2100) ‚Üí Future market opportunity
- **Europe:** 400M people, 60% over 60 by 2032 ‚Üí Labor shortage, production challenges
- **Asia:** Declining share 59% to 45% ‚Üí Demographic dividend ending
- **Business implications:**
  - "Can't produce a lot without workers"
  - Future markets in Africa
  - Labor costs rising in Asia
  - Europe needs automation/immigration

### Question 4: Compare transportation modes for an electronics company shipping from China to Spain.

**Answer Framework:**
- **Sea:** 90% of shipments, most cost-efficient, but long lead time, port-to-port only
- **Rail:** China-Spain route exists, efficient after 300km, environmentally friendly, good middle option
- **Air:** Fast but expensive, good for high-value electronics, limited by volumetric weight
- **Recommendation:** Mix strategy - sea for bulk/low-margin, air for high-value/urgent, rail for balance
- **Consider:** Product value, urgency, volume, inventory costs

### Question 5: Explain the G20 and its significance in global trade.

**Answer Framework:**
- **Definition:** 20 countries with highest GDP, most important for exports/imports, best industrialization
- **Representation:** ~50% of global economic activity
- **Purpose:** Forum for collaboration among leading economies, trade policy coordination
- **Examples:** Spain is member, discusses tariffs, trade agreements, economic policies
- **Significance:** Decisions affect global trade patterns, protectionism vs. free trade debates
